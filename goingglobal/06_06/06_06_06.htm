How Is Currency Exchanged?

People buy and sell currency as they do other goods and services. Business people and travelers must have the currency of a country to buy its products. If you visit Mexico, for example, you might want to buy or exchange U.S. dollars for Mexican pesos. You can exchange currency at banks, airports, and other places visitors often visit.

People and businesses also buy and sell currency as a way to invest. If you buy a country's currency when its value is low and then sell when its value is higher, you have made a profitProfit: the amount that remains after you subtract your total costs from your income.

The many exchanges that happen daily affect the value of currency. Even small changes in value have a great impact on individuals and businesses. Watch this video to see more about the value of currency in exchange.
