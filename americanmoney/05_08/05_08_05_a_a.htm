Quiz: Answers Text Version

  1. How does price motivate business owners?
    1. They will supply less of a product if they can charge less for it.
    2. They will supply more of a product if they can charge more for it.
    3. They will demand less of a product if they can charge more for it.
    4. They will demand more of a product if they can charge less for it.

    Feedback: Business owners supply goods and services. If they can charge a higher price, they will want to supply more of a product to earn more profit.

  2. Which entrepreneur started in radio and became famous for philanthropy?
    1. Oprah Winfrey
    2. Vera Wang
    3. Joseph Unahue
    4. Ben Cohen

    Feedback: All of these people are famous entrepreneurs. Oprah Winfrey is the one who began her career in a radio station.

  3. A single business that controls the market for a good or service is called a
    1. command
    2. patent
    3. monopoly
    4. stock

    Feedback: A monopoly is a business that controls all or most of the market for a good or service. For example, if you could buy cell phones from only one company, that company would be a monopoly. U.S. anti-trust laws try to prevent monopolies to encourage competition.

  4. Adam Smith believed that the best type of economy is a ___________ economy.
    1. market
    2. command
    3. mixed
    4. traditional

    Feedback: All of these answers are types of economies. Adam Smith believed a market economy was best, where supply and demand determine prices and use of resources. The United States is mostly a market economy, but we refer to it as "mixed" because it does have laws and regulations that affect the economy.

  5. How do banks affect the economy?
    1. banks hurt the economy because they hold on to people's deposits.
    2. they give credit to people and businesses who in turn buy products and services.
    3. they control the debt of the federal government.
    4. banks run the businesses that borrow money from them.

    Feedback: By offering credit through loans and other means, banks help people meet their goals. Examples are buying houses and starting businesses, which help grow the economy.

  6. A mortgage payment is an example of a
    1. fixed expense
    2. variable expense
    3. credit union
    4. government tax

    Feedback: A mortgage is a loan to purchase a house or property. In a budget, this amount usually does not change from month to month. Therefore, it is a fixed expense. A variable expense would be something that does change, such as the cost of eating out.

  7. What do individual workers supply to businesses?
    1. goods
    2. markets
    3. regulation
    4. labor

    Feedback: Labor is the supply of work or workers. Individuals supply labor to businesses and governments. Regulations create rules around work, such as the minimum amount of pay.

  8. Which is a similarity between household and government budgets?
    1. The expenses of both support the common good.
    2. Both types have fixed and variable expenses.
    3. Pay from working at a job is the main source of income for both.
    4. Running a deficit is the goal for both types.

    Feedback: Both household and government budgets have fixed and variable expenses. The basic process is the same for both as wellâ€”figure income, then expenses, then analyze the amounts and make changes. Taxes are the main source of government income and support the common good.

  9. A disadvantage of using credit is that it
    1. Helps people meet their goals sooner
    2. May assist entrepreneurs to expand their businesses
    3. Is useful in an emergency such as a natural disaster
    4. Can lead to people spending money they don't have

    Feedback: Credit, when used wisely, has many advantages such as those listed here. However, the tendency to spend more money than a person has is a major disadvantage of credit. Businesses and governments could use credit in this manner, and could lead to problems.

  10. Before the Federal Reserve can increase sales of federal bonds,
    1. the U.S. Treasury must give its permission
    2. individual banks must increase their interest rates on loans
    3. congress must approve raising the deficit of the federal budget
    4. the president must adjust the budget to decrease spending

    Feedback: The U.S. Treasury issues bonds, but the Federal Reserve determines how many are sold in a given time period. However, sales of the bonds can lead to an increase in the deficit. Only Congress can approve increasing the deficit to the federal budget.

  11. Imagine your favorite cereal is made of corn, and most of this year's corn crop was destroyed by wildfires. The supply of your favorite cereal decreases. What will likely happen?
    1. The price of your favorite cereal will increase.
    2. The price of your favorite cereal will decrease.
    3. The demand for your favorite cereal will increase.
    4. The demand for your favorite cereal will decrease.

    Feedback: Demand for your favorite cereal should stay the same because people will still want to eat the same amount of it. However, since there is less of it to buy, the price will go up. The cereal is more scarce than before.

  12. You can either study for your civics exam or watch television. If you choose the TV, your opportunity cost is the
    1. time and experience of studying for your exam and possibly earning a higher grade
    2. time and experience of watching TV and possibly talking to your friends about the shows
    3. the time you would spend talking on the phone to your friends
    4. the money you spend on electric and cable service for the TV

    Feedback: Opportunity cost is the time and experience of the activity you give up. In this example, the opportunity cost of watching television is the time you would spend studying. That experience could have included a higher grade on the exam.
