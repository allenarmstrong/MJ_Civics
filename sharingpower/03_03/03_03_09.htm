What Happens When the Laws Are Not Clear?

As you saw in the introduction, sometimes a rule or law causes confusion. Some people may not agree with a law. In the United States, a law can be made when the majority of lawmakers or the people agree with it. However, often some people will disagree with laws that are created. The courts play an important role in resolving disputes over the law. In addition, if certain issues or challenges to a law require rewording to be clearer, it may be changed. Another reason to change a law could be to allow certain exceptions.

The important thing to remember is that laws must be just and applied fairly and equally to everyone. Imagine your local park had a sign posted that said "No Vehicles in the Park," reflecting a local law. Your city defines a "vehicle" as any means of transportation using wheels.
