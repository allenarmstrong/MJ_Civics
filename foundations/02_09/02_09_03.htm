What Happens to Most Amendment Ideas?

To be added to the Constitution, an amendment idea must successfully complete two steps. First, it must be proposed with two-thirds support in both the House of Representatives and the Senate. Then, three-fourths of the state governments must ratify the amendment. Therefore, because there are 50 states, 38 states would have to ratify for the amendment to become part of the Constitution. Members of Congress introduce dozens, even hundreds, of ideas for amendments each year. However, many of these ideas are repeats. As you can see in this image, the majority of ideas do not achieve ratification. In fact, most do not even get through Congress.
