What Are the Types of News?

Visit any news website or pick up any newspaper, and you likely will see the same topic pages. You will see information about weather, sports, and entertainment. Stories will often be grouped by geography. Local, state, national, or domesticDomestic: relating to issues within a country, and foreignForeign: relating to, from, or located in another country or international news are common breakdowns. For example, a story about a local high school football game falls under local news. A holiday parade in a foreign country would be international news.

You can also classify most stories as politicalPolitical: relating to government or the actions of government, economicEconomic: relating to the production and use of goods and services in a society, or socialSocial: relating to the interactions of people or the conditions of living in a community news. For example, a story about an election would be political news. Economic news might include an article about a new business. Social news can include stories about health, education, and crime.

Many stories could be multiple types of news. The news editor has a tough job of deciding where everything will go!

"Number of U.S. College Graduates Rising"

Where might the editor place this story?

  • U.S. domestic
  • Social
  • Economic
Note that it could be both economic and social news, depending on what details the article includes.

"Leaders of 27 European Union Countries Sign Treaty"

Where might the editor place this story?

  • International
  • Political
  • Economic
Note that it could be both political and economic news, since government leaders meet, and their agreement could relate to economics.
