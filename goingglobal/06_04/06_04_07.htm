What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be ready to tackle the news! Imagine you are an international correspondent for a major news agency. The editor assigns you the task of summarizing top news stories from a foreign country.

Steps

  1. Find international news stories. Choose one foreign country to focus on. It may help to scan and search the news first before choosing your country. You might consider researching a country you have studied before to expand your knowledge. Scan news articles for your country to find an important article for each type of news:
    • political
    • economic
    • social

    Be sure to save the article and its source. You need to include the whole URL, or Internet site address link, in your work. You and your instructor may need to find the article later.

    You may find this activity on how to Research helpful for this step.

    You may also find these Links helpful for starting your research. Look for links to "world" or "international" news to begin. The site may have further links to specific regions or countries for articles, videos, and more.

  2. Read your articles carefully. Write a one-paragraph summary of the ideas, events, or information in each article. Each summary should be four to six sentences in length. Try this Activity if you need help with summarizing text.

  3. Compare the news for your country with current news in the United States. You may refer to the same news sites above for information on the United States. Do the countries have similar issues happening? How might events in these two countries have an effect on each other? Why are the news articles important to the world? Write at least one paragraph that answers these questions.

  4. Create a newsletter for your country that contains your three summary paragraphs and the paragraph comparison to U.S. news and issues. Your newsletter can be text, audio, video, or a combination of these. Save your presentation to one file to submit to your instructor.

There are many 21st-century tools available for creating and submitting your work in the online environment. For more information on tools your school uses, you may contact your instructor or visit the Web 2.0 tools area.
