What Is GDP and Standard of Living?

Gross domestic productGross Domestic Product: the market value of all goods and services produced within a country in a given period, also known as GDP (GDP) is a figure people often use to compare standard of living in different countries. It is a complex calculation. GDP includes the market value for all final goods and services produced within a country in a given period. It is the amount of "stuff" that the people of a country create. Let's break down what that means.

  • "Market value" means how much a person pays for the good or service.
  • "Final" means the finished good or service. For example, it would not include the cost of cutting down trees, only the final cost of the paper made from them.
  • "Produced within a country" means the product was made inside the country's borders. Therefore, McDonald's food sold in China, though it is a U.S. company, would not be included in U.S. GDP.
  • "In a given period" means it is based on a certain amount of time. For example, we could calculate GDP for a month, a year, or ten years.

Standard of living means a level of needs and wants enjoyed by people each day. We use many different types of dataData: information, facts, or statistics to judge a country's standard of living. The same can be said for examining different types of data. For example, a country could have very low crime but most people could also be very poor. Standard of living should focus on a variety of factors. The chart explains several important data people use to determine standard of living.

Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
  • GDP is the market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given period.
Crime Rate
  • A crime rate is the number of crimes committed per 10,000 people for a specific area, like a city or country.
Poverty Rate
  • A poverty rate is the portion of the population that earns less than a certain amount of income.
Literacy Rate
  • A literacy rate is the portion of the population that can read and write in an area.
Average Life Expectancy
  • The average life expectancy is the number of years a person born now can expect to live in a certain area.
Internet Users
  • Internet users are the number of people that have access and the knowledge required to use the Internet.
