Legislative Process Text Version

Instructions: Practice placing in order the steps of the federal lawmaking process.

Steps

  • Conference Committee meets to resolve differences between House and Senate versions of the bill
  • House debates and votes on the bill
  • Senate votes on compromise version of the bill
  • House subcommittee debates and votes on the bill
  • Bill sent to the president, who can sign it, veto it, or do nothing
  • The bill is introduced in the Senate
  • Full committee in the House debates and votes on the bill
  • Senate debates and votes on the bill
  • The bill is introduced in the House
  • House votes on compromise version of the bill
  • House Rules Committee sets rules for debate and voting on bill for the whole House
  • Senate subcommittee debates and votes on the bill
  • Full committee in the Senate debates and votes on the bill

Remember, this process often happens at the same time in the House of Representatives and in the Senate. For example, the Senate may debate and vote on a bill to change healthcare law in the United States at the same time that the House of Representatives is debating an voting on a similar bill.
