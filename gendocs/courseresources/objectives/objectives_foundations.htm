Foundations Objectives

After completing this unit, you will be able to:

  • print and use the Foundation Checklist
  • complete the Foundation Pretest
  • recognize how the Enlightenment influenced the Founding Fathers
  • identify the Magna Carta, English Bill of Rights, Mayflower Compact, and Common Sense
  • recognize these documents' impact on colonial views of government
  • describe issues that led to the writing of the Declaration of Independence
  • analyze ideas and complaints in the Declaration of Independence
  • identify how weaknesses in the Articles of Confederation led to the writing of the Constitution
  • illustrate and analyze the structure, functions, and processes of government under the U.S. Constitution
  • describe limits on power under the Constitution
  • explain differing viewpoints on ratification of the Constitution
  • evaluate rights contained in amendments to the Constitution
  • distinguish how the Constitution both safeguards and limits individual rights
  • explain the constitutional amendment process
  • analyze the impact of the 13th, 14th, 15th, 19th, 24th, and 26th amendments on minority political participation
    • identify amendment ideas that have not achieved ratification (Advanced)
    • research and describe arguments for and against an amendment idea (Advanced)
  • review the major concepts and terms from the module
  • complete a Discussion-Based Assessment with your instructor
