What Is Plessy v. Ferguson?

The case of Plessy v. Ferguson is one example of judicial review where citizens challenged a law through the courts. In 1896, Homer Plessy and his legal team lost their case in the U.S. Supreme Court. Plessy believed that a law in his state requiring black train passengers to ride in a separate section from white passengers was unconstitutional. When the court reviewed the state segregationSegregation: the practice of keeping people of different ethnic, racial, religious, or gender groups separate in society such as in schools, housing, and transportation law, it determined that the law was acceptable under the Constitution. It said such laws were acceptable as long as the accommodationsAccommodation: something supplied to offer convenience or to meet a need, such as food, seating in a public space, or services were equal. This is the case where the phrase "separate but equal" originated.

Because the court upheld the law, segregation continued and Americans experienced more than 60 additional years of legal segregation in their daily lives. Watch this video to learn more.
