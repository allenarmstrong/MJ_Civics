What Choices Must People Make During the Process?

Try this activity to see how an idea can become a bill, and eventually a law, in the state of Florida. You will make choices like a real legislator and answer some questions about the process.

Scenario 7

Your bill passes both the Florida House and Senate. Now the passed bill goes to the governor. What may the governor do?


Veto the bill

Sign it into law

Both options are possible. The governor may sign the bill into law. If he or she takes no action, the bill will become law after 15 days. If the governor vetoes the bill, the legislature may overturn the veto if two-thirds of representatives and two-thirds of senators vote for it, just like in the U.S. Congress.
