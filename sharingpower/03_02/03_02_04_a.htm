State Government OrganizationText Version

The legislative, executive, and judicial branches share power and authority in a state government.

Legislative Branch

Makes laws: Examples include taxes and laws for within the state.

Executive Branch

Enforces the laws: The highest state executive is the governor.

Judicial Branch

Interprets the laws: The state determines the structure of the courts and how to judge appointments are made.
