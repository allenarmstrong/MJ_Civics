What Do I Have to Do?

You can now see how challenging it is to create the federal budget. Members of Congress, the president, and American people have different opinions on what should take priority. Now you will conduct a survey to make recommendations for budget changes.

Steps


© 2011 Photodisc/David De Lossy/Thinkstock
  1. In your CAP File, examine your list of 12 defense and non-defense spending programs of the federal government. Select six of the 12 programs that you feel are most important in the federal budget.
  2. List the six programs you chose in the Budget Survey Template. Then give at least six people a copy of the survey chart. You may ask friends, family members, and other trusted adults. Ask each person to rank the programs from most important (6) to least important (1).
  3. Collect and calculate the results from your surveys into the Survey Results Template. Answer the questions at the bottom of the results chart. You will use your results chart and question answers in the final step.
  4. Create a presentation about your findings and budget recommendations. You may choose your format, such as a slide presentation, essay, or video. Save your presentation to one file to submit to your instructor. You may create your presentation by hand, using a computer program, or using a Web 2.0 tool. Be creative, and use color and pictures. Include the following items in your presentation:
    • The results chart from your survey
    • Your responses to the survey results questions
    • Your responses to the survey reflection questions

There are many 21st century tools available for creating and submitting your work in the online environment. For more information on tools your school uses, you may contact your instructor or visit the Web 2.0 Tools area.
