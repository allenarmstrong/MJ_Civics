Legislative Process: Answers Text Version

Steps

  1. The bill is introduced in the House
  2. The bill is introduced in the Senate
  3. House subcommittee debates and votes on the bill
  4. Senate subcommittee debates and votes on the bill
  5. Full committee in the House debates and votes on the bill
  6. Full committee in the Senate debates and votes on the bill
  7. House Rules Committee sets rules for debate and voting on bill for the whole House
  8. House debates and votes on the bill
  9. Senate debates and votes on the bill
  10. Conference Committee meets to resolve differences between House and Senate versions of the bill
  11. House votes on compromise version of the bill
  12. Senate votes on compromise version of the bill
  13. Bill sent to the president, who can sign it, veto it, or do nothing

Remember, this process often happens at the same time in the House of Representatives and in the Senate. For example, the Senate may debate and vote on a bill to change healthcare law in the United States at the same time that the House of Representatives is debating an voting on a similar bill.
