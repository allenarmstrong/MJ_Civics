What Are the Three Basic Questions?

The ways a country answers three basic questions determines the type of economy it has and the role of competition. The questions are:

  1. What will be produced?
  2. How will it be produced?
  3. For whom will it be produced?

What do these questions mean? Check out these examples for a household and a business.

Household

Have you ever chosen on your own to help with chores at home? You chose:

 

Maybe you could cook a pasta dinner for the family.
You will determine what tools to use, such as pans, stove, and spoons, what cooking methods you will use, the food ingredients and amounts you need such as the pasta type and two jars of sauce, and what utensils, plates, and cups you will use to serve it.
You will determine who will eat the dinner you prepare and how much of a share each person will eat.
