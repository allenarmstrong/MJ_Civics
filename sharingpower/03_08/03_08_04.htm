How Does Public Policy Differ from Citizen Action?

Public policy is a course of action accepted or used by a government to respond to a problem. Citizen action includes the activities people take to improve society in their community. Citizen action may include volunteer work or service. It may include advocacyAdvocacy: active spoken or written support for a cause or position. Often, the work of advocates is to try to convince lawmakers to create or change public policy.

If you wished to advocate for a change in public policy, whom would you contact? Local and state governments have many departments or agencies. The agencies enforce public policy for certain activities. For example, your city probably has a utilities office. People in this office organize and manage electricity and water service to homes and businesses. A zoning and permits office makes rules about where and how people may build homes and businesses. The department you contact depends on the issue or problem you plan to address.

You can find information about your state's government through USA Government Made Easy at usa.gov.

How Can I Find Information About My Local or State Agencies?

Your local phone book lists government agencies and departments, often in a special section on a different color paper. You can find phone numbers for the courthouse offices, police department, and zoning office, for example.

The Blue Pages

 You can visit your local or state government's website. It should have information and links to its departments and agencies, such as the Department of Agriculture.

The Internet

 You can talk to a trusted adult, like a parent or your instructor, about local and state agencies. They can give you ideas about who to contact, such as an animal control office or health department. Mandy will contact these departments for her concern about the feral cats.
