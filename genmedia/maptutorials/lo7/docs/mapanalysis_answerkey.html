What map features are most important to understanding the map?

Ridges on the map indicate mountains. The light blue shows water. The thick, solid lines show borders between countries. The words on the map show names of the countries and cities, mountain ranges, and bodies of water.

List specific things you see in this map that you think are important.

What patterns do you see in the map features?

What connections can you make between other maps or what you know and what you see in this map?

Map Interpretation Questions

Why do you think the patterns and connections you noticed in the map exist?

I think most of the cities are along the coast because of the need for water access. These areas probably have more contact with outside nations. That also explains the location of major cities along rivers. I expect China is so large because of the many mountains offering a natural boundary and protection from outsiders.

Why do you think this map was created? Explain your response using features you analyzed in the map.

I think the map was created simply to show the physical features of Southeast Asia. People studying history or culture or politics of this region can look at the landforms and water locations and better understand why people live the way they do in certain areas. Businesspeople would be interested in the locations of major cities to consider trade opportunities.
