Where Do Entrepreneurs Find Their Ideas?


© 2011 AP Images

Many entrepreneurs are inventors who turn their idea into a product they can sell. Some are innovatorsInnovator: a person who has new ideas for methods or products who improve an existing product or service. Do you or a friend have a cell phone? Imagine it weighing six pounds and having to carry it with its charging case everywhere you go. That is what "portable phones" were like in the 1980s. Thankfully, clever people came along who improved the technology to make it smaller and lighter and, well, more portable!

Some people become entrepreneurs because they see a problem they can solve, like how to get the rest of the ketchup out of the bottle (redesign the bottle with the cap on the bottom). Others develop a business out of difficult situations, like losing a job. Watch this video to see how one sharp American turned a challenge into a successful business.
