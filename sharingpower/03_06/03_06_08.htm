Where Do the People Go?

With two levels of courts, each having various types of courts to meet specific needs, it can be very confusing which court will hear a particular case. The United States is very large, so each state has at least one U.S. District Court to hear federal cases. If you imagine the state you live in, consider that your state has several types of courts within it. Not only does it have state-level trial courts, courts of appeal, and a state court of last resort, but also it has at least one U.S. District Court. It may have a U.S. Court of Appeal. If you are ever involved in a dispute or called to serve on a jury, how would you know where to go?
