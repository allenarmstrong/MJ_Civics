Jimmy's Arrest

Federal law gives law enforcement the right to arrest and place robbery suspects in jail. Jimmy was arrested and sent to jail. He is accused of robbing a bank and will appear in court. What is the type of law relates to Jimmy's arrest?

  1. Constitutional
  2. Criminal
  3. Civil
  4. Military

Feedback: Breaking the law is a criminal matter.

Federal law gives law enforcement the right to arrest and place robbery suspects in jail. Jimmy was arrested and sent to jail. He is accused of robbing a bank and will appear in court. What is the source of law related to Jimmy's situation?

  1. Statute
  2. Regulation
  3. Case Law

Feedback: A statute makes it against the law to rob banks. However, this question relates specifically to Jimmy's arrest and placement in jail. A regulation gives law enforcement the power to arrest someone suspected of breaking the law.

Elisa vs. Lydia

A state law says that property owners have the right to seek payment from those who cause damage to their property. The Seventh Amendment to the Constitution says that people have the right to a jury trial in property disputes between citizens.

Elisa is suing Lydia in court for shattering a set of glass sliding doors. She wants a jury trial. What is the type of law related to Elisa's situation?

  1. Constitutional
  2. Criminal
  3. Civil

Feedback: Though the Constitution protects Elisa's right to a jury trial, that is not the law in question. This is a civil dispute between Elisa and Lydia.

Was Amy's Privacy Violated?

The Fourth Amendment to the Constitution protects people from searches without a strong reason to suspect wrongdoing.

Amy is the wife of a soldier but not in the military herself. She was stopped by police while shopping at the mall and all her belongings searched. They let her go, finding no stolen goods. Amy was embarrassed and believes her rights were violated by the search. The officer says the law allows him to search anyone who looks suspicious. Amy wants to challenge this law in court.

What is the type of law related to this situation?

  1. Constitutional
  2. Criminal
  3. Civil
  4. Military

Feedback: Though Amy is a soldier's wife, she is not in the military herself, so military law does not apply in this situation. Since she believes her rights under the Constitution were wrongly taken away, it is a question of constitutional law.
