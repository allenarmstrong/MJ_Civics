What Is Better, a Strong or Weak U.S. Dollar?

If U.S. business owners hear that the value of the dollar is strong, what does that mean? Will they be happy? First, it means the value of the dollar has risen compared to other currencies. Whether the business owner will be happy about it, however, depends on the business.

A strong dollar makes American products appear more expensive to foreign companies. The demand for U.S. goods will decrease because they have to pay more. Foreign currencies and products appear less expensive to Americans, so the U.S. demand for imported products will increase.

Therefore, U.S. business owners will be happy if they sell foreign products in the United States. They will be unhappy if they sell U.S. products to other countries. Examine these images to learn more.
