What is Gideon v. Wainwright?

Amendments to the Constitution include protections for people accused of crimes. Sometimes, questions arise about how far the rights protected by the Constitution extend. For example, the Sixth Amendment protects the right to have the help of an attorney. What should happen if a suspect cannot pay for the help of an attorney? Under the idea of due process, must the government provide an attorney to people who can't afford one?

The Supreme Court was faced with these questions when they heard the case Gideon v. Wainwright in 1963. Watch this video to the learn more about this case and the court's decision.
