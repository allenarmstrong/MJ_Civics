What Are the Civil War Amendments?

Â© 2011 AP Images

The Civil War was a bitter war between sections of the United States over slavery and the rights of state governments. It lasted from 1861 to 1865. States ratified the 13th, 14th, and 15th amendments in the five years following the Civil War. These three amendments changed the Constitution to establish former slaves as U.S. citizens, giving them the same protections and rights as other citizens.

Before and during the Civil War, some slaves escaped to states that did not have slavery. They hoped to start a better life in freedom. The 13th Amendment declared that slavery anywhere in the United States was against the Constitution. With this amendment, people no longer had to risk their lives to escape slavery.
