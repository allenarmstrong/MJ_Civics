ReCAP!

You should now be familiar with U.S. participation in international organizations. In addition, you should know how the United States has responded to a few conflicts.

Review your notes and the information in the lesson. Did you print or save your CAP File and place it in a safe location?

Now it's time for a game! Try this activity to review what you learned in the lesson.

Questions:

 
The United States is a permanent member of the Security Council in which international organization?
 
Which international organization has people all over the world helping bring emergency relief to victims of war and natural disaster?
 
The United States is part of a military alliance that responded to the September 11, 2001 attacks. What alliance is it?
 
What country has the United States helped defend from communism since shortly after World War II ended?
 
What country did the United States hold as territory after a war with Spain?
 
Thousands of Americans serve people in other countries each year to improve their lives and good will toward the United States. What organization do they join?
 
In what conflict did the United States first stay isolated, then helped with supplies, and then eventually declared war itself?
 
What conflict do U.S. and other world leaders regret they did not act to stop?
 
If the leaders of two countries would like help settling a dispute, where can they go for help?
