Map Tutorial Glossary

Absolute Location: a latitude and longitude coordinate for a specific point on Earth

Analysis: comparing features on maps and between different maps

Axis: center of Earth’s rotation

Bias: any personal judgment about people, places, or things

Cardinal Directions: north, east, west, and south

Cartograms: maps that show statistics as they relate between land areas

Cartographer: person who makes maps

Choropleth Map: uses color to display information such as climate and weather information

Compass Rose: map feature that shows the cardinal directions as they relate to the map layout

Contour Map: show elevation of Earth’s surface

Density: an average across a unit of area, such as population per square mile

Distortion: alteration of an object’s natural shape

Dot-density Map: shows the frequency of a specific item or event over a geographic area

Elevation: height above or below sea level

Global Information Systems (GIS): sets of descriptive or scientific data for a place

Global Positioning System (GPS): uses satellite technology developed for the military to locate a precise coordinate point for someone or something

Globe: a model of Earth

Grid System: map feature that divides areas on the map into sections labeled with letters and numbers

Hemisphere: half section of Earth

Historical Map: represents Earth’s features at a previous point in time

Intermediate Directions: northeast, northwest, southeast, and southwest

Interpretation: with a map, asking and thinking about why patterns and connections exist

Latitude: angular distance on Earth’s surface, measured north or south in degrees from the Equator

Longitude: angular distance on Earth’s surface, measured east or west in degrees from the Prime Meridian

Map: flat representation of Earth

Map Distortion: the bending, twisting, or forcing of Earth out of its natural shape

Map Key or Legend: map feature indicating how symbols are used in the map

Map Layering: including multiple data sets in a map for comparison

Map Orientation: the relationship between directions on the map and the compass directions north, south, east, and west

Map Projection: mathematic means and description for representing Earth on a flat surface

Mental Map: the way an individual thinks about and pictures the world

Model: copy of an object usually built on a smaller scale

Physical: real and touchable

Physical Map: map that focuses on the natural features of Earth

Political: concerned with government

Political Map: map that focuses on the human-made features of Earth

Qualitative data: information you can see and describe but not measure

Quantitative data: information that is measurable in terms using numbers

Rate Data: ratio of one count to another

Relative Location: description of how a place compares to other landmarks, using compass directions and approximate distance

Scale: a size ratio, such as 1 unit equaling 50 units in the original object

Symbol: in a map, an image that represents the location of a physical or political feature

Thematic Map: map that focuses on a single piece of information or theme

Topography: the land features of an area
