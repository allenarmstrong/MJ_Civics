Can You Spend Money to Make Money?

The responsibilities of government are both shared and divided between the local, state, and federal levels. Much of the federal budget is distributed to state and local governments for use.

When officials debate the federal budget, one key point is whether increasing federal spending encourages economic growth. In other words, does increasing the federal budget lead to more money and jobs for Americans in general? In a large nation like the United States, finding a direct cause and effect relationship between government spending and economics is tricky. Many argue that the evidence of the benefits is not there to justify increasing spending, especially if it will increase the deficit. However, this is the argument some leaders make when they want to increase spending on a program or start a new program.

Study this example of how a federal grantGrant: money given by a government or other organization for a specific purpose, such as education program could lead to economic growth to see this idea in action.

Imagine that Congress creates a federal grant for students seeking a medical degree.
Members of Congress may have to raise taxes or reduce spending on other programs to fund the grant.
The grant program would create jobs because the government would hire people to manage the grant program. This includes managing paperwork and sending funds to the universities.
More students would likely seek a medical degree since money is available to pay their education costs.
Universities would hire more instructors and staff to handle the growth.
Eventually, there would be more people working in the medical field. They would contribute new ideas for disease prevention, proper health management, and treatment options. The grant program contributes to overall economic growth.
