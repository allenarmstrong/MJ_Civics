What Was the Enlightenment?

Â© 2011 AP Images

The Enlightenment was a time of change in ways of thinking. Starting in the 1600s, philosophers in European countries like Britain and France began to challenge old practices and beliefs. They thought people could use reasonReason: the ability to think logically, clearly, and without emotion to solve problems.

Important people from this time include John Locke and the Baron de Montesquieu. They studied the governments of ancient Greece and Rome in forming their ideas about how government should work. Founding Fathers like Thomas Jefferson used many Enlightenment ideas to create the basis for the U.S. government.
