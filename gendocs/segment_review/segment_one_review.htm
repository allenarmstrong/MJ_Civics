Segment One Exam Review

Congratulations! You will soon complete the first segment of this course. Examine this presentation for topics from segment one. Your exam will cover this content.

This review covers the main objectives you will need to know in order to prepare for your Segment Exam. Be sure to revisit any Unit you feel you need more time to study before beginning your exam.

Citizenship

Objectives

  • use a map to locate the 50 U.S. states and capital cities
  • use a map to locate U.S. territories and protectorates
  • locate major physical U.S. landmarks
  • define culture
  • identify major cultural regions of North America
  • locate major U.S. cultural landmarks
  • interpret maps to identify political boundaries
  • use GIS technology to study current information about
    the United States
  • define ecology and conservation
  • use a map to study issues of conservation or ecology
  • describe how citizenship and ecology are connected
  • define "citizen"
  • identify paths to U.S. citizenship
  • identify and evaluate duties and responsibilities of
    citizens
  • experience citizen responsibilities
Advanced

If you are not taking the course for Advanced credit, you do not need to review the objectives on this page.

  • conduct an interview
  • identify different types of service

The Advanced Segment One Exam will include short essay responses based on the advanced lessons.

Foundations

Objectives

  • recognize how the Enlightenment influenced the
    Founding Fathers
  • identify the Magna Carta, English Bill of Rights,
    Mayflower Compact, and Common Sense
  • recognize these documents' impact on colonial views of
    government
  • describe issues that led to the writing of the Declaration
    of Independence
  • analyze ideas and complaints in the Declaration of
    Independence
  • identify how weaknesses in the Articles of Confederation
    led to the writing of the Constitution
  • interpret the Preamble of the Constitution
  • illustrate and analyze the structure, functions, and
    processes of government under the U.S. Constitution
  • describe limits on power under the Constitution
  • explain differing viewpoints on ratification of the
    Constitution
  • evaluate rights contained in amendments to the
    Constitution
  • distinguish how the Constitution both safeguards and
    limits individual rights
  • explain the constitutional amendment process
  • analyze the impact of the 13th, 14th, 15th, 19th, 24th,
    and 26th amendments on minority political
    participation
Advanced

If you are not taking the course for Advanced credit, you do not need to review the objectives on this page.

  • identify amendment ideas that have not achieved ratification
  • research and describe arguments for and against an amendment idea

The Advanced Segment One Exam will include short essay responses based on the advanced lessons.

Sharing Power

Objectives

  • identify federalism
  • differentiate responsibilities of the different levels of
    government
  • compare the constitutions of the United States and
    Florida
  • define rule of law and recognize its influence
  • identify sources and types of law
  • illustrate the lawmaking process at different levels of
    government
  • diagram the levels, functions, and powers of courts at
    the state and federal levels
  • identify concepts and steps of the trial process
  • simulate a jury trial
  • develop a plan to resolve a state or local problem
  • identify government resources to address the issue
Advanced

If you are not taking the course for Advanced credit, you do not need to review the objectives on this page.

  • research a recent bill or law
  • analyze the potential impact of legislation on different
    levels of government

The Advanced Segment One Exam will include short essay responses based on the advanced lessons.
