What Happened in the 2000 Election?

As you saw, neither George Bush nor Al Gore won over 50% of the popular vote. Even more troublesome, the results in one very large state were in dispute. Florida's 25 electoral votes would determine the winner of this close election. Bush was winning by a small number of votes.

Florida law required a recount of votes with such close results. Two of the largest counties could not complete their recounts on time. This was especially true in counties that used the "butterfly" or other disputed ballot types. When the deadline passed for the counties to recount their votes, state officials announced George Bush the winner. The democratic candidate, Al Gore, did not think it was fair to stop the recount in those large counties. He also found fault with the methods used to conduct the recounts.



As of 2011, Florida has 29 electoral votes. Every 10 years the U.S. Census Bureau conducts a demographic study of the nation. Population shifts between states can lead to changes in both representation and electoral votes.




Al Gore requested a recount in four Florida counties shown on the map below. From North to South, the counties are Volusia, Palm Beach, Broward, and Miami-Dade.

Roll over the labeled counties to see more information about each. The counties of Duval, Brevard, and Hillsborough appear for comparison. Can you determine why Al Gore might have selected these counties in particular?
All seven counties have large populations and numbers of total votes. Notice how three of the counties where Gore requested a recount all have a million or more people.

Gore won the popular vote in each county where he wanted a recount. In the other counties shown here, Bush won the popular vote by a large number of votes. Since the election was so close, the recount of Florida votes would end up determining the winner.

Gore could conclude that these four counties were the most likely to hold the additional votes needed for him to win the election. They had a large number of voters and the results showed that a majority in each county did support Gore.
