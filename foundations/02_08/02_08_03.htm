Can Americans Change the Constitution?

Yes! The Founding Fathers knew it would be important for the Constitution to endure over time. People and societies change over time. The Constitution needed to be able to meet the country's needs even as ways of thinking changed. Article V (Five) of the Constitution explains the process for amending the Constitution.

The Bill of Rights is the name we give to the first 10 amendments to the Constitution. The states ratified them as a group within a few years of the Constitution itself. In 1992, the states added the 27th Amendment. That means that over 200 or so years, Americans have made only 27 changes to the Constitution!
