What Are the Types of Economic Systems?

You heard Smith, Marx, and Keynes discuss their ideas for how to organize economic activity. Each represents one of the three main types of economic systemsEconomic System: the approach a country uses to deal with scarcity and achieve its economic goals. Some areas of the world follow a fourth type of economy that we call a traditional economyTraditional Economy: an economy based on culture and tradition to control the market. Most countries of the world are mixed economiesMixed Economy: an economy with individual economic freedom mixed with some government economic planning and regulation, meaning they have characteristics of both market and command economiesCommand Economy: an economy that is planned and controlled by the national government. This is true in the United States. The mixed economies vary in how much of a role government leaders take in answering the three basic questions.

In the United States, the Founding FathersFounding Fathers: name given to the early leaders of the United States who helped design the nation's government thought it was best for people to choose how to make their own living and run their lives. They protected people's freedoms in the U.S. ConstitutionU.S. Constitution: plan detailing the structure, functions, and powers of the government of the United States, also known as the Constitution. At first, the United States was closer to being a market economyMarket Economy: an economic system that relies upon supply and demand to distribute resources and determine prices. It grew more mixed as the government added laws and regulations to protect the rights and choices of Americans. Market or mixed economies work well with governments like the United States because they are based on protecting people's rights and freedoms.
