Plaintiff’s Attorney (audio): Good morning members of the jury, judge, and all of you with us today. I am Emile Proofinder, representing the plaintiff, Suzy Snoozer. Ms. Snoozer awoke late one summer morning to find her birds missing that she left in their cage outside overnight for air. We will prove to you today that her neighbor, Ms. Felina Early, was negligent in allowing her cats to attack and kill Ms. Snoozer’s birds. Ms. Snoozer is seeking compensation for the loss of her beloved birds and her pain and suffering.

Defense Attorney (audio): Judge, jury, and respected citizens—good morning. I am Bobby Refuter, today representing Ms. Felina Early. Today I will show you that the plaintiff lacks the evidence to prove Ms. Early is liable for the loss of Ms. Snoozer’s birds. The loss of her birds is unfortunate. However, falsely placing blame and forcing compensation will not replace the birds.

(Image: close up of the judge)

Judge (audio): Members of the jury, you have heard both sides of this case. Your community depends on you to consider the evidence fairly and make a wise decision. Please keep these points in mind:

  • You must follow the law. The question here is whether Ms. Felina Early is responsible for the loss of Ms. Snoozer’s birds. You must determine this based on the evidence available, not any assumptions you may make.
  • Do not make a decision based on feeling sorry or angry with anyone involved in the case, including the attorneys. Your feelings about anyone in this room should not affect your decision.
  • Your decision as a jury must be unanimous. Each juror must agree to the same verdict.
