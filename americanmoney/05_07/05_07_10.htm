What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be able to discuss the relationship between government, credit, financial institutions, and the economy. You will discuss with other students whether you think people should permit the federal government to borrow money and why.

Imagine the Fed has hired you to create a website! It will teach elementary-age students about the role of banks and credit in the United States. The Fed wants input from many teens, so you will be posting your thoughts to the discussion board for feedback from others.

Steps


© 2011 Brand X Pictures/Thinkstock
  1. Write one paragraph that summarizes clearly why banks are important to the U.S. economy and how government actions can affect how banks operate.
  2. Write a second paragraph that explains whether you think the federal government should be allowed to use credit. Remember, people debate this topic often so there is no clear right or wrong answer. The important thing is to support your opinion with facts from the lesson. Include at least three reasons for your opinion.
  3. Read the Discussion Posting Guidelines. Then, post your two paragraphs to the discussion area.
  4. Respond to at least two other students' posts. Tell them why you agree with their posts, facts that explain why you disagree, or what you would add. Use information from the lesson in your posts.

Please view the Writing Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Discussion to American Money 06 How Does Borrowing Money Affect Us?
