Lesson 1 of 4:

People make maps often. Examples include military leaders mapping battle plans, historians showing there an event took place and what existed there, professional cartographers mapping data, and students showing they understand the ecology of a region. We can create maps by hand, using simple computer software for images, or even geographic information systems (GIS) data with professional cartography software that can change projection with a button click.

Lesson 2 of 4:

The most important issue to determine when you plan to make a map is its purpose. Why you make the map and whom it is for will guide the rest of the process, from how you make the map to the level of detail.

Impact of your map

Lesson 3 of 4:

When you make a map, all the choices lead to a map that focuses in on a specific purpose but leaves out details. These choices result in map bias.
