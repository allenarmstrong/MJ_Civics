Is Credit Good for the Economy?

This question has no right or wrong answer. Using credit has its costs and benefits, just like any other choice a person or government has to make. It has the potential to create or prevent financial ruin.

Governments in the United States have used credit to assist people, especially during emergencies like natural disasters or widespread job losses. Credit for households and businesses can encourage the economy to grow and improve. The government funds special programs that extend credit to encourage growth of small businesses and encourage college education.

Governments make rules for banks and other creditors to try to prevent ruin from human error. For example, in recent years Congress has passed laws related to what credit card companies can charge in interest and fees if a person makes a late payment.
