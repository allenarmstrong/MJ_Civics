Should Only Adults Have Driving Privileges?

Some American communities have increased the minimum driving age in recent years. Let's look at the reasons for and against this idea as our final issue for the lesson.

Follow the slides to experience the 'Perspectives' talk show!

Newscaster: Welcome to today's edition of Perspectives! Today we examine the issue of one state's plan to increase the minimum driving age from 16 to 18. Our guest Mr. Safety is the leader of a citizen group trying to convince state lawmakers to increase the driving age. Our other guest, Mrs. Practical, is the leader of a citizen group against increasing it. Welcome!

Mr. Safety and Mrs. Practical: Thank you.

Newscaster: Let's start with Mrs. Practical. Please, tell us why you believe your state should not increase the minimum driving age.

Mrs. Practical: We are a large state that is mostly rural. With so few cities, traffic is not a great problem. Increasing the driving age is unnecessary.

Mr. Safety: It's not just about traffic. Young people lack driving experience. They are a danger to themselves and others on the road. They have no need to drive at 16 years old.

Mrs. Practical: Age is not a predictor of driving ability. It's about practice. Whether you increase the minimum age or not, new drivers are always going to be the most inexperienced. Besides, today's teens need to be able to drive to work and school. Many operate tractors and trucks for their family farms.

Mr. Safety: We have data that show drivers aged 16 to 20 years are the most likely to have traffic accidents and more likely to die in those accidents. We would increase the time drivers practice using a driver's permit, so as long as a 16-year-old had an adult in the vehicle, he or she can drive. They just would not drive alone until 18 years old, the age of adulthood.

Mrs. Practical: It is unreasonable to expect an adult to be with a young driver every time they drive for two years. It's also unnecessary. The current driver's exam requires that a driver has had 50 hours of driving practice. That is only about one hour a week for a year. Our current plan of permit at age 15 and full license at age 16 does not need a change. We do not have enough public transportation options for working teens.

Mr. Safety: We should expand public transportation. That would be a priority under this new law. Please understand me. I am not trying to create a burden on teens, their families, or family farms. I am concerned about the safety of our state's young people.

Newscaster: Thank you, Mrs. Practical and Mr. Safety. That's all the time we have for today. Well, viewers, now you have heard a couple perspectives on increasing the minimum driving age. Is there a middle ground? Do you have a different perspective? We hope to hear from you. Tune in next time to Perspectives. Good night.

Mr. Safety and Mrs. Practical: Thank you!
