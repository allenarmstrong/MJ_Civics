What Makes a Business Successful?

Some entrepreneurs are happy to keep their business running with enough income and profit to cover the costs of running the business and their personal wants and needs. Others want to see their businesses grow and keep on growing.

For example, a restaurant could grow by opening a new, additional location. Maybe the owner decides to bake all the bread instead of buying it. The owner purchases a bakery that bakes just for the restaurant. The owner might start a partnership with a local farmer so that the restaurant can offer the freshest foods possible on the menu. Perhaps the restaurant owner attracts new customers by building a stage and having live music. The incentive for the owner to grow is to serve more customers and thus make more sales. In all these ways, the entrepreneur grows the business and, with luck, increases profit.

Price also motivates entrepreneurs. They may supply more of a product if they know they can charge more for it. They tend to supply less if the price they can get is lower. Competition with other businesses affects price. See more about competition in this video about pizza.
