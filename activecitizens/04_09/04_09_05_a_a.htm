Expression Matching: Answers Text Version

  1. Active citizens should strive to be
    b. Informed citizens–Active citizens should strive to be informed citizens by examining multiple perspectives on important issues.
  2. Today officers make sure to read a suspect his or her rights when making an arrest because of
    a. Miranda v. Arizona–The decision in Miranda v. Arizona is that the suspect did not have a fair trial because he was not made aware of his rights. An example is the right to an attorney. Today, an arresting officer is sure to read suspects their "Miranda rights."
  3. How can interest groups influence an election?
    d. They can donate money to a particular candidate's campaign.–Some interest groups raise and donate money to candidates that support their issue. It would be against the law for an interest group to pay voters or otherwise control their choices at the polls. They might influence candidates to pay more attention to their issue, but cannot stop them from discussing other issues.
  4. Which of the following political parties and ideas match?
    b. Republican Party– limited role of government in people's lives–The Republican Party platform favors a limited role for government in the economy and people's lives. The Democratic Party platform favors increasing spending on programs that provide direct assistance to people such as food and housing.
  5. The case Marbury v. Madison established the Supreme Court's power of
    d. Judicial review–The decision of Marbury v. Madison established the Supreme Court's power of judicial review. The Supreme Court has the final say on questions related to the meaning of the Constitution.
  6. Part of the case decision in Tinker v. Des Moines was that
    c. Students have the right to symbolic speech in school–The case of Tinker v. Des Moines related to the wearing of anti-war armbands, a form of symbolic speech. The Supreme Court ruled that students do have the right to freedom of speech in school as long as they are not disrupting the school.
  7. What do maps of data for the 2000 election show?
    a. The results between George Bush and Al Gore were very close.–Maps of the 2000 election results show just how close the votes were between George Bush and Al Gore. Determining a winner came down to a few hundred votes in Florida.
  8. The states must provide people accused of crimes help from an attorney due to the case of
    a. Gideon v. Wainwrigh–The Supreme Court decided in Gideon v. Wainwright that if a suspect could not afford to hire an attorney, the state must provide one for the suspect's defense.
  9. Which of the following cases overturned the decision of Plessy v. Ferguson as it applied to public schools?
    d. Brown v. Board of Education–All of these cases relate to minors. Except for in re Gault, all relate to schools as well. However, Brown v. Board of Education is the case that outlawed segregation in public schools. This overturned the Plessy decision in school.
  10. Why is it important to communicate with elected officials?
    d. They need to know the citizens' concerns in order to represent them well.–Representatives depend on information from their communities to know how to represent the citizens.
  11. What was the main decision in Bush v. Gore?
    a. Ordering a new recount in Florida was unfair because the methods used in the recount were different between polling places.–In the case of Bush v. Gore, the Supreme Court ruled that Florida should not have ordered a new ballot recount because different methods were used to conduct the recount from place to place. It was not a fair practice. Therefore, the prior court decision to stop the recount and declare George Bush the winner would stand.
  12. Showing a candidate in black and white in a television ad
    c. Is a symbolic way to express a negative view of that person–A group can use black and white pictures of a candidate in the media to show them in a negative way. They usually show the candidate they support in color. Viewers tend to prefer images that appear in color.
