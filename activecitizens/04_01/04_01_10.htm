What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be familiar with different perspectives on several issues! In this activity, you will choose an issue from the lesson to discuss with your classmates in more detail.

Steps


© 2011 Creatas/Thinkstock
  1. Review your CAP file. Make sure you have information on each issue from the lesson listed in the file.
  2. Choose the issue you found most interesting from the lesson. Discuss the issue with a parent or trusted adult in your community.
    • increasing spending on programs for those in need
    • building new offshore drilling rigs
    • replacing a strip mall with a superstore
    • increasing the minimum driving age
  3. Write a paragraph that explains your perspective on one of these issues. Be sure to include reasons for your perspective. There is no wrong answer, but you must use details from the debate on your chosen topic from the lesson.
  4. Write a second paragraph to describe how else the issue may be addressed, such as the example you described in your CAP file. Include any new ideas from discussion with a trusted adult.
  5. Read the Discussion Posting Guidelines. Then post your two paragraphs to the discussion area.
  6. Respond to at least two other students' posts. You may choose to respond to posts on different issues. Tell them why you agree or disagree with their explanation or what you would add. Use information from the lesson in your posts.

Please view the Response Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Discussion Posts to Active Citizens 01 How Can Citizen Perspectives Differ?
