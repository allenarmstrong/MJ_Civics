What Is Globalization?

Countries around the world continue to grow more connected. Travel and communication advances like airplanes and the Internet make the world "smaller." This means that being far away is not as much of an obstacle to meeting as it was decades ago. Trade with other countries has grown because the agreements and shipments of goods are faster and easier. Countries around the world are interdependentInterdependence: the relationship of being unable to exist or survive without each other. We call this process globalizationGlobalization: the process of increasing the connections and interdependence of the world's countries, especially through trade.

The use of currency is even more important as trade increases. Just as currency makes it easier in our everyday lives to get goods and services, it simplifies trade over long distances. Without currency, the vast amount of international trade that happens daily would be far more difficult. Watch this video to learn a bit about globalization.
