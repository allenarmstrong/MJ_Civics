Having learned the different steps involved in research writing, you are provided with a scenario to try yourself. The screen displays mail content and is followed by a set of questions to answer. Try answering them with the knowledge gained in the course.

Read the following urban legend and then answer the questions about how you would research it.

Urban Legend

Hi! I just read this. Really scary stuff. Please forward to anyone you know who travels!

TRAVELERS BEWARE!!!

Law enforcement agencies in major cities throughout the United States are scrambling to protect thousands of people who have had their identities stolen. The culprits: hotel employees.

Room keys are digital cards that contain frightening amounts of your personal information, including:

  • Your FULL name
  • Your home address
  • Your phone number
  • Your family members’ names
  • Your credit card and banking information

When you turn in your key card after a nice vacation, you are actually handing over your most personal information to the people behind the desk. These are part-time employees who are difficult to trace, and they can walk out with your card at any time, stealing your identity.

If you have visited a hotel with an electronic key card, check your bank account! Change your passwords! Do everything in your power to protect your livelihood. In the future, NEVER return the key cards to the hotel desk. It isn’t worth it!

Identify the best option to the following questions.

Which of the following questions would be a strong research question for this subject?

Options:

  • What are the major hotels in the United States?
  • What information is stored on a hotel key card?
  • How many people have experienced identity theft?
  • Are you allowed to keep your key card from a hotel?

What would be a strong key word search for this subject?

Options:

  • Hotels AND Identity theft
  • Hotels OR Key cards
  • Part time NOT Full time
  • Identity theft AND Police

How can you best use an author’s work to locate more sources for your search?

Options:

  • Plagiarize your paper from someone.
  • Make the sources up on your own.
  • Choose the first sources you find.
  • Look at the sources the author used.

If you want to write your paper about the order in which events occurred, you should organize your notes.

Options:
