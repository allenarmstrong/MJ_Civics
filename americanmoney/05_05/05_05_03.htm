What Is a Budget?

A budget is a plan of incomeIncome: payment for work or profits on investments over time and spending. It shows how much money is coming in and how much is going out. Budgets are based on a certain amount of time. For example, a family might make a budget for one month of spending. The purpose of a budget is to plan spending so that you spend no more than you have. A budget shows what you can afford and what you cannot.

A teen could make a budget based on allowance or money earned from babysitting. A household could make a budget that tracks all spending for its family members. Businesses track the cost of resourcesResources: a material or item that can be used to produce something new they use and record how much they earn from sales. A state or country also budgets money. It spends money on programs like schools and brings in money from taxes and other sources.
