What Do People Think about Building Large Superstores?

Another issue in many American cities is replacing older strip malls and businesses with "superstores". Many companies operate as superstores, large facilities that sell a variety of products.

Follow the slides to experience the 'Perspectives' talk show!

Newscaster: Welcome to Perspectives, the show about current views on hot public issues. Today we will look at the issue of plans to build a new store in one small city. Our guest Ms. Super is an owner of the chain of superstores wishing to build in Americana City. Our other guest, Mr. Local, runs a small business in Americana and believes a superstore would harm the city and its people. Welcome!

Ms. Super and Mr. Local: Thank you.

Newscaster: Let's start with Mr. Local. Please, tell us why you believe your city should fight a new superstore.

Mr. Local: We are a small city that has lived pretty much the same way for many years. Local families support local businesses, and vice versa. Americana City has only about 5,000 people. We are too few in number to support such a big store.

Ms. Super: Your city needs us, Mr. Local. Members of your city and the council have complained for years about a lack of jobs and places for residents to shop. We would build a modern store that your city can be proud to claim. It would attract shoppers from other cities.

Mr. Local: Your store would force family-run businesses to close. They would not be able to compete. We are talking about families who have lived here for generations. We take pride in supporting our local businesses run by our neighbors and friends. I myself run a small office-supply store. It's been my livelihood for over 30 years.

Ms. Super: I understand your concern. Your neighbors and friends would easily have a job in the new superstore. In fact, our store would improve your city's quality of life. We can offer more variety in products. We can offer them at lower prices than your local businesses. The store would attract new residents to the city as well.

Mr. Local: The business owners will not be happy about closing. They, including myself, will see working for your store as an insult. Besides, stores like yours cannot afford to pay workers well. The quality of life for those who run small businesses in Americana City will go down, not up. We offer a friendly and familiar shopping experience your store just cannot match.

Ms. Super: Remember that our store will have to pay taxes to your city on our sales. The taxes will increase the money the city has to focus on other needs. You will be able to repair streets and other old buildings. You can improve the schools and hire more teachers. The benefits of our store to your city outweigh the concerns.

Newscaster: Thank you, Mr. Local and Ms. Super. That's all the time we have for today. America, you heard some perspectives on building a super store in Americana City. Is there a middle ground? Do you have a different perspective? We hope to hear from you. Tune in next time to Perspectives. Good night.

Ms. Super and Mr. Local: Thank you.
