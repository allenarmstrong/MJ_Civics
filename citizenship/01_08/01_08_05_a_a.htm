Recap Quiz: Answers Text Version

  1. The U.S. Capital, Washington D.C., borders the states of Virginia and
    a. Maryland—Washington D.C., borders Virginia to the southwest and Maryland to the southeast, northwest, and northeast.
  2. The United States has two territories in the Caribbean Sea: the U.S. Virgin Islands and
    b. Puerto Rico—The U.S. Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico are U.S. territories in the Caribbean Sea. The Cayman Islands are a British territory. Guam is a U.S. territory in the Pacific Ocean. Haiti is an independent nation.
  3. In which U.S. state can you find the famous Mount Rushmore?
    d. South Dakota—Mount Rushmore is in South Dakota. In New York, you will find the Statue of Liberty. Pennsylvania has the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall. California has the Golden Gate Bridge.
  4. The major geopolitical and cultural regions of the United States are the Northeast, Midwest, South, and
    a. West—The U.S. Census Bureau divides the U.S. states into the Northeast, South, Midwest, and West.
  5. Which river in the central United States drains 31 states?
    c. Mississippi—All of these rivers are important to the United States. The Mississippi River, though, flows through the center of the United States. It drains water from 31 states into the Gulf of Mexico.
  6. Which of these nations is part of North America?
    a. Mexico—North America includes Canada, the United States, Mexico, and the nations of Central America and the Caribbean. Japan is in Asia. England is in Europe. Colombia is in South America.
  7. Maps of U.S. Census data make it clear that most Americans live
    b. Near the coasts—Maps of U.S. Census data show the greatest concentration of people near the Atlantic and Pacific coasts.
  8. Acid rain is an ecological issue, especially in the Eastern United States. We can see on a map where it has the greatest impact. Which of the following states experiences the most acid rain?
    d. Ohio—Acid rain is the greatest problem in Eastern U.S. states, including Ohio.
  9. People who wish to become U.S. citizens must first
    a. Live legally in the nation for five years, or three if married to U.S. citizens—Living legally in the United States for at least five years (three if married to a U.S. citizen) is required before applying to be a U.S. citizen. The Oath of Allegiance is the final step. Changing one's birth record is illegal, and you need to be a U.S. citizen to obtain a U.S. passport.
  10. All of the following are examples of citizenship duties required by law except
    c. Voting in elections—U.S. citizens must pay taxes, attend school at least until age 16, and serve on juries when called. Voting is an important responsibility, but law does not require it.
  11. People become U.S. citizens through
    a. Either birth or naturalization—People become U.S. citizens through either birth or naturalization. Minor children born outside the United States may become U.S. citizens when their parents are naturalized.
  12. Obeying laws is an important part of citizenship. Minors
    b. Have to obey laws such as attending school—Minors are people under the age of 18 who, just like other citizens, must obey laws. In the United States, most minors must attend school.
