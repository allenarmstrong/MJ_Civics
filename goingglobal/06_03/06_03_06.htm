What Is Diversification?

DiversificationDiversification: the process of starting new industries to increase variety in an economy is the act of a country producing a greater variety of products. In the activity, you saw that Chocolate Land has a much more diverse economy than Lollipop Land. Let's say that Lollipop Land recognizes the disadvantages and riskRisk: the danger of something going wrong, like damage or loss of producing only lollipops. Its leaders decide to encourage businesses to produce new goods. Perhaps they decide to build plants that convert sugar to fuel. Maybe they discover that sugar grows well in rotation with peanuts, from which they can create dozens of products. The process of becoming a producer of more goods and services is diversification.

It is not always easy for a country to add new products. While it may have a valuable natural resource, the country may lack the needed equipment to make use of it. Another issue is the country might not have people, or labor resources, with required knowledge and training. Sometimes a country can find new products and services to offer using the resources it does have available. Some countries have economies that are more diverse than those of others. Economies change over time.

Benefits of Diversification:

It encourages progress and quality. To continue to be successful in business and trade, people will explore new ways to do new things. They could improve their products and lower costs.

Progress

 It minimizes risk. If a natural disaster or human act ruins one product, the country has others it can trade.

Minimize Risk

 It encourages trade. The country has more to offer other countries in trade.
