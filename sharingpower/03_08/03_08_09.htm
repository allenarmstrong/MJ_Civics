What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be familiar with the process of acting on public policy issues. Use this knowledge and your work from the lesson to create a presentation on your chosen issue.

Steps


© 2011 AP Images
  1. Complete your CAP file carefully. It contains most of the information you need to finish your assessment! Choose a state or local issue. Be sure to have your teacher and parent or guardian approve your choice of issue.
  2. Choose a format for your presentation. You may choose one of the following:
    • letter to a newspaper editor
    • slideshow presentation
    • video or podcast
  3. Your presentation must include the following information:
    • chosen issue and background (identify the issue, conduct background research)
    • government agencies and citizen groups that would address the issue
    • any laws or other rules related to the issue
    • your solution and criteria (what you want to see happen and how you will judge options)
    • public policy alternatives to reach your solution
    • positives and negatives for each possible policy (evaluation of public policy alternatives)
    • the public policy you choose to support and why
    • what you could do to get the policy enacted (make your citizen action plan)

Please view the Presentation Grading Rubric before starting the assignment. If you choose to complete your collaboration in this lesson, select the Collaboration Option for details on meeting your collaboration requirement.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Issue Presentation to Sharing Power 08 How Can We Solve Local Problems?
