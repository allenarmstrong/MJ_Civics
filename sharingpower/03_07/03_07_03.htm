What Is A Jury Trial?

A juryJury: a group of ordinary citizens who decide together whether a person accused of a crime is guilty or not guilty is a group of ordinary citizens who decide together whether a person accused of a crime is guilty or not guilty. A trialTrial: a formal legal process in court to settle disputes or determine the guilt or innocence of those accused of crimes is a formal legal process in court to settle disputes or determine if a person accused of a crime is guilty or not guilty. Sometimes only a judgeJudge: high-ranking court officer who supervises trials, instructs juries, and delivers trial outcomes will hear and decide cases. We call this a bench trialBench Trial: trial held without a jury, where the judge solely reviews the evidence and makes a decision about the case. However, a jury can decide whether a person is guilty or not in a criminal caseCriminal Case: a lawsuit brought before a court involving someone who is accused of committing a crime. A jury may also decide whether a person owes compensationCompensation: money to pay for or make up for a loss to another person in a civil caseCivil Case: a lawsuit brought before a court relating to the rights of citizens, usually a dispute between citizens.

A criminal case would center on a person who is accused of breaking a law. The person is a suspect in a crime against society, so the government pursues the case. A civil case is a dispute between two people or groups of people. An example of a civil case is when one person sues another. People who have disagreements over property and other issues may choose to have a court of law settle their problem.

Maggie thought no one would notice, until she passed the doorway and alarms began ringing. The store's owners recently installed the alarm system due to frequent thefts. The police caught up to Maggie. She will face trial for her actions.



Shelly sues Joey for breaking a window on her house while he was playing baseball. Joey did not mean to break the window and feels his apology should be enough. However, Shelly wants him to pay for the broken window. They will face each other in a court of law.
