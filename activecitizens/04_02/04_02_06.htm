What Is Brown v. Board of Education?

The case of Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka, Kansas challenged the older Plessy decision. In 1954, African American families won the right to send their children to schools with white children. The Supreme Court recognized that "separate but equal" laws that it upheld in Plessy v. Ferguson did not result in equal accommodations.

The Brown decision is an example of the Supreme Court again using its power of judicial review. In this case, it reversed the Plessy ruling as it applied to schools. Future laws would strike down segregation by race in other parts of society, such as restaurants and workplaces. Watch this video to learn more about the Brown case.

Text Version

The U.S. Supreme Court can make new decisions that contradict earlier decisions! New justices, laws, and changes in society can lead to new ways of interpreting the law. The Brown decision is one example. Think about the effect of this case on the lives of people who lived before and after this landmark decision.
