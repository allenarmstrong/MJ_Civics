Which level of government should you contact?

Some responsibilities of local, state, and national governments overlap. The leaders must work together to determine who will take over in a situation. They will figure out how they can work together to solve the problem.

For example, if a major storm causes severe damage through several states, all levels of government will take action. Local law enforcement will help people to safety. Officers will protect homes and businesses. Local service districts will work to restore water and electricity or supply funds to the companies that provide those services. The state will provide funds to emergency teams for cleanup. The state's department of transportation will clear and repair highways to restore transportation flow. The national government will examine technology and other systems to see how damage can be prevented in the future. The national government may provide the affected states with funds and resources for cleanup and repairs.

Another shared power is enforcing laws and punishing lawbreakers. Sometimes the suspect of a crime will flee to another state and break laws there as well. Law enforcement agencies and courts at different levels and in different places will communicate with each other about the suspect. They will determine where to bring the suspect and which crimes to first pursue in court.
