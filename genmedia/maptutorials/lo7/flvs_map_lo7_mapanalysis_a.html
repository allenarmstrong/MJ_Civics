Lesson 1 of 5:

Image of a young boy and girl facing each other.

Screen 1 of 5:

Boy: So I know how to locate places on maps and identify features like symbols and the scale. Is there anything else we can do with maps?

Screen 2 of 5:

Girl: Absolutely! You may have heard the phrase “a picture is worth a thousand words.” Well, a map is worth, like, a million! By analyzing and interpreting the features on maps, you can learn so much about your world besides where to find mountains and borders.

Screen 3 of 5:

Boy: What’s the difference between analyzing and interpreting?

Screen 4 of 5:

Girl: Good question! Analysis is comparing features on maps and between different maps. You identify patterns and look for connections when you analyze a map. Interpretation is taking the next step with a map by asking why those patterns and connections exist.

Screen 5 of 5:

Boy: Wow! I guess a map really is worth a million words! Thanks so much!

Lesson 4 of 5:

Interpretation is another skill related to map analysis. When you interpret a map, you ask why the features and patterns you analyzed exist. You also consider why and for whom the information is important. You may even make a prediction. Interpreting maps can lead to further questions for study and research.

Examine the map for possible interpretations and ideas for further exploration.
