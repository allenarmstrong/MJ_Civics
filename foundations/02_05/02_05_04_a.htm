Llamas Eat Juicy Squash and Sweet Raspberries

Article I (One)—Established the legislative branch

Article II (Two)—Established the executive branch

Article III (Three)—Established the judiciary branch

Article IV (Four)—Describes the states' powers and limits

Article V (Five)—Describes the amendment process

Article VI (Six)—Explains that the Constitution is the supreme law of the land and no laws, state or federal, may conflict with it (Supremacy Clause)

Article VII (Seven)—Describes the process for ratification of the Constitution

Narrator (slowly): "Llamas eat juicy squash and sweet raspberries.

Does that sound funny? This sentence is a mnemonic for the Constitution.

A mnemonic is a short rhyme or phrase to make information easier to remember. This mnemonic will help you remember what the articles are about in the Constitution. Notice how the first letter of each word stands for the main idea of the articles in order. Can you think of your own mnemonic?"
