What Is Money?


© 2011 Stockbyte/Thinkstock

The money you have stashed in a piggy bank is called currencyCurrency: paper and coin money accepted as payment for goods and services. The bills and coins that make up currency make it easier to get the things you need and want. Currency is actually just one part of the money supplyMoney Supply: the total amount of money available in the national economy. Think of how else people pay for things. You have probably seen people use a bank or debit card, which withdraws money directly from a bank account.

Many people use credit cards, but technically a credit card is not money. A credit card represents a debtDebt: an amount of money, service, or other property owed to someone. It is money lent by a credit card company, such as a bank, that the spender must pay back.

Money can include some investmentsInvestment: something that money is placed in with the goal of making a future profit, like a stock or property, like government savings bondsBond: a loan to a company where the company must pay it back with interest after certain period . It includes a person's paycheck. A bank can turn these into currency or into an amount in an account for spending. The supply of money is more than just paper and change!
