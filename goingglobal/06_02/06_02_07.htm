Where Are the Resources?

Many countries are lucky to have plenty of natural resources and arable landArable Land: land that can be used to grow crops. From oil to fuel machines and land to grow corn, the United States is rich in resources. Finding more uses for a natural resource increases its value. For example, corn has many uses besides food. Ingredients made from corn are important to producing paints, paper, stamps, tape, and medicines. Because the United States is rich in natural resources, it has many products it can sell and trade.

Countries that lack natural resources can develop other products. An example is services. Services include web design, restaurants, hotels, and cleaning companies. Islands in the Caribbean, such as the Bahamas, export few types of food. However, the tropical climate and short distance to the United States have created a booming tourist industry!

Other countries have resources the United States does not. For example, bananas are popular among Americans. However, it is difficult to grow bananas in most parts of the United States. The majority of bananas are importedImport: a good or service transported into one country from another country from other countries. The United States exportsExport: a good or service transported from one country to another country products to countries on every continent.



Some Americans are famous for developing more uses for natural resources. George Washington Carver is said to have discovered over 300 uses for peanuts and other crops!
