Should the Government Borrow Money?

Americans debate whether government should borrow money to help improve the economy. For example, government could borrow money to build new highways, paying people to do the work. The jobs created would create income for people who might have been out of work, who then place that money in banks. Now they are able to buy goods and services with their earnings, benefiting other businesses as well.

While that may sound good, government leaders are still human and can make the same mistakes with credit. Most state governments have laws requiring a balanced budget. This means that the total expenses of the state cannot be higher than the money the state collects. Many Americans believe that the federal government should have a balanced budget as well. However, CongressCongress: official name for the legislative branch of the U.S. government currently has the power to approve spending increases that go over budget.

A bank might not give credit to a person who has trouble paying off debt. Likewise, people worry that a nation in debt appears financially unstable to other countries. This may prevent nations or individuals from investing in U.S. businesses. In other words, they think government borrowing hurts the economy over time.
