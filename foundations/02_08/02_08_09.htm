Are There Amendment Ideas Americans Have Not Ratified?

Yes! The amendment process is long and difficult. Many ideas for amendments have failed to survive through ratification. One example is the Equal Rights Amendment, or ERA for short. The goal of this proposed amendment is to ensure equality under the law for women.

Congress proposed the ERA in 1972. Thirty-five states ratified the amendment by 1978. However, 38 states were required to ratify to meet the three-fourths ratification rule. Americans disagree on the effects the ERA could have, with women and men on both sides of the debate.
