What Are Some Other Failed Amendment Ideas?

The slideshow below examines some other failed amendment ideas. As you explore the images and text, think about which of the amendment ideas you find most interesting or important.

Note that while these are examples of ammendments that were not ratified, there are national and state laws in place that do relate to several of these issues. For example, each state does restrict the age at which people can legally begin working. Some Americans believe amendments like these are unnecessary because of already existing laws. However, others note that amendments provide greater protection because laws can be changed much easier than constitutional amendments!




Make notes on these amendment ideas in your CAP File. You will use one in your assessment.

Child Labor

Would give Congress the power to limit, control, or possibly prohibit work for those under 18 years of age.

*approved (proposed) by Congress in 1924 but not ratified by the states


Medioimages/Photodisc

Term Limits for Congress

Would limit how many times a person could be elected to serve in the House of Representatives or Senate.


© 2011 Thinkstock

Balanced Budget

Would prevent the federal government from spending more money than it collects in taxes each year.


Digital Vision

Every Vote Counts

Would end the Electoral College method of electing the president in favor of a direct national election.


© 2011 AP Images

Death Penalty Abolition

Would prohibit the use of the death penalty as punishment anywhere in U.S. territory.


© 2011 AP Images

Flag Desecration

Would make certain images and actions related to the U.S. flag (such as burning the flag in protest) against the Constitution.


© 2011 Thinkstock

Equal Opportunity to Govern

Article II (two) of the U.S. Constitution states that only native U.S. citizens are eligible to be president. This failed ammendent would have allowed naturalized citizens at least 20 years old to run for the office of president.


Public Domain

English as Official Language

Would establish English as the official language of the United States, requiring public signs and notices to be printed in English and no other language, and English-only instruction in public schools.


© 2011 AP Images

Right to Bear Arms

Would repeal the Second Amendment which does not specify a purpose for firearms, and state that people have the right to firearms for "hunting, sport, collecting, and personal defense."
