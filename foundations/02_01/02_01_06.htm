What Did People Say About These Ideas?

Each of these quotes reflects one or more ideas from the Enlightenment. Some are from the Enlightenment thinkers, and some are from American leaders.

Study each of these quotes and think what they mean to you. Then click on the next button in the upper right hand corner to see an interpretation.

Quote

"A social contract or unspoken agreement exists between the government or ruler and the people.

In that contract, the people agree to give the ruler certain powers. In return, the government ruler promises to keep society safe from lawlessness."

- Thomas Hobbes

Quote

"A social contract or unspoken agreement exists between the government or ruler and the people.

In that contract, the people agree to give the ruler certain powers. In return, the government ruler promises to keep society safe from lawlessness."

- Thomas Hobbes

Meaning
A government and its people have a basic agreement that is not found in a written law.

The government agrees to keep the people and property safe from those who break the law. The people agree to give the government the powers needed to keep that agreement. This is called the social contract.

Quote

"All people have certain 'natural rights,' including life, liberty, and the right to own property. These are never given to a ruler, even in a social contract.

All governments rule only with the consent of the people they govern. If rulers become cruel or unfair, citizens have a duty to stop them."

- John Locke

Quote

"All people have certain 'natural rights,' including life, liberty, and the right to own property. These are never given to a ruler, even in a social contract.

All governments rule only with the consent of the people they govern. If rulers become cruel or unfair, citizens have a duty to stop them."

- John Locke

Meaning
People are born with natural rights. No one can take these rights away. They include rights to life, liberty, and owning property.

A government only exists because its people agree to it. The people give government power, and the people can take that power away if the government is cruel or unfair.

Quote

"A representation of the people in one assembly being obtained, a question arises, whether all the powers of government, legislative, executive, and judicial, shall be left in this body? I think a people cannot be long free, nor ever happy, whose government is in one assembly."

- John Adams

Quote

"A representation of the people in one assembly being obtained, a question arises, whether all the powers of government, legislative, executive, and judicial, shall be left in this body? I think a people cannot be long free, nor ever happy, whose government is in one assembly."

- John Adams

Meaning
The tasks of government need to be split among three groups of representative leaders. People cannot be free or happy if only one group of government leaders is responsible for all the legislative, executive, and judicial powers.

Quote

"I had always hoped that this land might become a safe and agreeable asylum* to the virtuous and persecuted part of mankind, to whatever nation they might belong."

- George Washington

*Asylum means a "safe place." The Founding Fathers believed it was the government's side of the social contract to provide protection for people's natural rights.

Quote

"All, too, will bear in mind this sacred principle, that though the will of the majority* is in all cases to prevail, that will to be rightful must be reasonable; that the minority possess their equal rights, which equal law must protect, and to violate would be oppression."

- Thomas Jefferson, First Inaugural Address, March 4, 1801

*In a democracy, the "will of the majority" is determined through voting and polls. Thomas Jefferson speaks of both democracy and the agreement between citizens and government in this quote

Quote

"All, too, will bear in mind this sacred principle, that though the will of the majority* is in all cases to prevail, that will to be rightful must be reasonable; that the minority possess their equal rights, which equal law must protect, and to violate would be oppression."

- Thomas Jefferson, First Inaugural Address, March 4, 1801

*In a democracy, the "will of the majority" is determined through voting and polls. Thomas Jefferson speaks of both democracy and the agreement between citizens and government in this quote

Meaning
Decisions will and should be based on what the majority of people want to happen. However, those people who do not agree with the majority have rights, too. The majority in any situation must remember and respect this fact. The laws must protect the rights of all people, no matter what their opinions.
