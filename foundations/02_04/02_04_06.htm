Why Is the Preamble to the Constitution Important?

The Constitutional ConventionConstitutional Convention: the 1787 meetings of delegates from the U.S. states to address problems in the Articles of Confederation met from May to September 1787. Delegates from each of the 13 states met to address the problems within the Articles of Confederation. The members created a new plan for government called the Constitution of the United States.

The first section of the Constitution describes the purpose of the document. We call this section the "PreamblePreamble: the introduction to the Constitution of the United States." The Preamble reveals the concerns U.S. leaders had at the time. It also describes the purpose and duty of government to its citizens. Study the Preamble in this presentation.
