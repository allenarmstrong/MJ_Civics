Impact of Government Text Version

Introduction: The following areas in a house or on the road can be impacted by government.

Bathroom

Local governments provide safe drinking water that meet state and federal standards. Toiletries must be monitored for toxins before consumer use. Congress-funded National Health Institutes developed these medications.

Bedroom

Federal government helps provide electricity through funds and regulations. The federal government sets the standard time. The Dept. of Agriculture runs cotton and wool programs while the Consumer Product Safety Commission must test fabrics for safety. The federal National Weather Service provides forecast information to news stations.

Kitchen

In most areas of the United States, many papers, plastics, and metals are recycled through government efforts. The U.S. Department of Agriculture monitors the safety of the foods we eat. The U.S. Department of Agriculture provides guidelines for what we should eat to be healthy. Local governments require pets to be registered and vaccinated against diseases. Local governments take garbage to landfills that meet state and federal regulations to be environmentally safe.

Road

Local governments can improve traffic through new routes and improving old ones. State and federal governments can provide funds for road projects. State and local governments set traffic safety laws. Federal standards for emissions, or poisonous fumes, have reduced pollution from car exhausts.

Federal government controls the use of the airwaves. It also provides copyright protection for music and other original works. Governments set safe speed limits for roads. State governments have test requirements to obtain a driver's license. They also require a car owner to have car insurance. Federal standards have improved fuel efficiency in cars, meaning cars use less fuel today than they did years ago. Federal law requires vehicles to have seat belts and air bags, which can save lives in an accident.
