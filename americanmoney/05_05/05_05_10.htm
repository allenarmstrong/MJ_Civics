You should now be a budget master! Now you will demonstrate your learning by presenting a story. Imagine you have a younger sibling. Both of you are at a store. Your sibling is crying because a toy, labeled $3.99, ended up costing more than the four dollars he or she had.

You have a few options to complete your story:

  • Write a story in text only using the Story Frame Template.
  • Create a comic strip. See the Comic Strip Tips for creating a comic strip.
  • Create a slideshow with narration to tell the story.

You may find the template helpful in starting the comic strip and slideshow option as well. Whichever option you choose, be sure that your story contains these features:

  • explains what taxes are
  • explains how taxes support the economy
  • gives examples of how government spending benefits people
  • explains how a budget can help avoid your sibling's problem
  • compares the national budget to a personal budget

Please view the Grading Rubric before starting the assignment. If you choose to complete your collaboration in this lesson, select the Collaboration Option for details on meeting your collaboration requirement.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Story to American Money 05 Why Should We Pay Taxes?
