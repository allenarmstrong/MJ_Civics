How Else Can a Business Change Culture?


© 2011 AP Images

Not only can certain products spread in popularity, they can actually change the way we live. McDonald's® restaurants have changed Americans' expectations of food service and the dining experience. The first McDonalds® opened in 1940. American culture placed a high value on families taking their time to eat dinner together each night. It was a social event where they shared their day with each other. Many Americans still value the family dinner. For many families, though, it does not happen often.

Families are busier than ever before. Students are busy with after-school activities like sports. Parents are busy with jobs outside the home. Households look for ways to make mealtime fast and easy. For many people, it may be difficult to imagine a time before drive-through fast food. Many busy families visit fast-food restaurants for convenience. They want great-tasting food, and they want it fast. Using a drive-through and eating on the go saves time.

Fast-food restaurants like McDonald's® have changed American culture. People have different expectations of mealtime. See how the changes are spreading to other nations around the globe.


  • 1965 – Ronald McDonald, the clown mascot of McDonald's®, appears in his first television commercial. Households across the United States see the ad.

  • 1967 – The first McDonald's® locations open in Canada and Puerto Rico. Today they are in 117 countries around the world.

  • 1975 – The first McDonald's drive-thru opens near an army base. Soldiers were not allowed to get out of their vehicles wearing their uniforms. McDonald's® creates the drive-thru as a service to the soldiers.

  • 1978 – A McDonald's® opens in Kanagawa, Japan. This is the 5,000th McDonald's® to open.

  • 2006 – McDonald's® makes nutrition information available to customers. American culture demands not only fast food but also the information to make healthier choices.
