How Have Amendments Expanded the Right to Vote?

Americans passed many of the later amendments to extend rights to more groups of people. When we see a woman voting on Election Day, we see the impact of the 19th Amendment. It is important to note that new federal or state laws will sometimes limit the effects of an amendment.

A good example of this is how after the 15th Amendment was ratified, many states created poll taxesPoll Tax: a payment collected from people as a requirement to vote. Charging a fee to vote prevented many former slaves from voting (and many poor whites as well). The 24th Amendment ended this practice.
