Who Influences Public Policy?

When citizens have ideas for a new law, they may contact their representativesRepresentatives: a person chosen to speak and act for their fellow citizens in government. Whom they contact depends on who represents the area where they live and what the issue is that needs attention.

Each level of government has a process for discussing an idea, or bill, and passing it into law. Many people, including ordinary citizens, can become involved at multiple points in the process. The interactions of citizens, groups, lawmakers, and other leaders create a process much more complex than a list of steps. Lawmaking at every level of government is about people coming together to make society better for as many people as possible.

For example, one area of life important to most Americans is education. Americans agree that educating all young people is important. They may not agree, however, on the best ways to educate. Since citizens have different opinions and ideas about the best ways to do things, lawmaking can be very difficult and take a lot of time.
