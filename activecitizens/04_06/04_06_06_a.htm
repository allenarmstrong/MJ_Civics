Ballot

Timmy can help choose who represents his community in government by voting on Election Day.

"Vote for Me!" Sign

Tammy can choose to run for an elected position herself. A candidate hopes to win enough support to be a representative of other citizens in government.

Timmy can contact his representatives in many ways. He may have an idea or complaint to share with leaders. Government leaders receive many phone calls, emails, posts to websites, and letters from active citizens like Timmy and Tammy.

TV Camera

Tammy can use many methods to contact the media. The media can spread ideas and concerns to many people quickly.  It helps a person find support for the idea or concern. With wide support, government leaders will likely respond.

Door With “Meeting Today” Sign

Timmy can choose to join a group that influences government. When people work together, they often can have a greater effect on government leaders. A person could help an interest group persuade elected officials to vote for a law the group supports or a volunteer to help a candidate persuade voters support her in a coming election.

Group Protest With Generic "We Won’t Stand It!" Signs

Tammy can join a protest or support rally. An interest group might sponsor a protest against a law it does not want government to pass. She might help write a petition to change the law. A candidate’s campaign workers might organize a parade to gain attention and support for the candidate. Active citizens like Timmy and Tammy could attend and show their support.

Dollar Bill

Timmy could choose to donate money to a cause that influences government. For example, he could send money to help the campaign of a candidate he supports. Tammy could send money to help an interest group she supports. The group could use the money to start a website.
