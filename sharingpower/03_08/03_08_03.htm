How Can Citizens Help the Community?


© 2011 AP Images

Citizens bring problems and issues to the attention of leaders in government. They can make telephone calls or send emails to the officials. They can write letters to newspapers. Citizens can also attend and speak at local government meetings. They encourage officials to consider new public policiesPublic Policy: a course of action accepted or used by a government to respond to a problem that would target problems and improve the community. People may create their own groups. The groups can raise money for activities that help address issues. Groups of people have greater ability to get leaders to act or hear their concerns. By coming together, citizens have the power to find solutions and create positive change.



You can work with your fellow online students to discuss issues in your communities. Your team can brainstorm and debate policy changes. Select the Collaboration Option for details on using this activity to meet your collaboration requirement.
