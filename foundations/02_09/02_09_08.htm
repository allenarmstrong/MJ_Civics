You should now be an amendment master! Now you will research a failed amendment that you choose and take a stand on whether it should be ratified.








Step 1: Choose

Step 1: Choose a failed amendment idea to research from the following list:

  • Child Labor
  • Flag Desecration
  • Balanced Budget
  • Every Vote Counts
  • Right to Bear Arms
  • Death Penalty Abolition
  • Term Limits for Congress
  • English as Official Language
  • Equal Opportunity to Govern

Choices not on this list must be preapproved by your instructor.

Step 2: Research

Step 2: Research the amendment idea. Find facts and information that support answers to the following questions:

  • When was the idea first introduced?
  • What has happened to this amendment idea since introduction?
  • Why do people support this amendment idea?
  • Why are people against this amendment idea?

You may find the FLVS Research Asset activity on how to research helpful for this step.

You may also find these Links helpful for starting your research.

Step 3: Form

Step 3: Form your own opinion. Discuss the idea with trusted adults. Consider the evidence you found in research.

Step 4: Create

Step 4: Create your presentation. You may choose your presentation format. No matter what format you choose, you must include the following:

  • summary of the amendment idea's history
  • arguments for and against the amendment idea
  • who you spoke to about the idea and what they said
  • your opinion on the idea and reasons for your opinion
  • a list of resources you used to find information

There are many 21st century tools available for creating and submitting work in the online environment. For more information about tools your school recommends, please visit the resource tools area in your course or contact your instructor.



Please view the Research Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Research to Foundations 09A What Should Be the Next Amendment?
