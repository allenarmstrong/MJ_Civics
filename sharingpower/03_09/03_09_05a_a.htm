ReCAP! Quiz Text Version

1. The U.S. Constitution delegates to the national government the power to





Answer: The U.S. Constitution delegates the power of creating and maintaining the armed forces solely to the national government.

2. Federalism is important because
A.
B.
C.

Answer: Federalism divides government power into levels. This helps protect people's rights and better serve their needs.

3. Which of the following statements is true of the Constitution of the State of Florida?

A.

C.
D.

Answer: Florida voters have the powers of initiative and referendum, meaning they can suggest and vote on new laws or changes to laws. Like the U.S. Constitution, it gives the state three branches of government, including a bicameral legislature.

4. How has the Rule of Law affected the development of the United States?


B.
C.
D.

Answer: The Rule of Law means that the nation's laws must apply to all Americans fairly and equally. No one is above the law and no one is below it, even the president.

5. Which of the following types of law applies to those serving in the armed forces?

A.
B.

D.

Answer:Military law is a specific type of law that applies to the men and women serving in the armed forces.

6. What happens if the president vetoes a bill passed by Congress?

A.
B.

D.

Answer: Only Congress has the power to overturn a president's veto to make a bill a law. However, it requires that two-thirds of the House and the Senate approve the bill, a difficult amount of support to achieve.

7. A court that hears a case for the first time is called a

A.

C.
D.

Answer: Courts of original jurisdiction are also known as trial courts. They hear cases for the first time.

8. Jury selection is the step in a trial where

A.
B.
C.

Answer: Jury selection is the first step in the trial process. Lawyers ask questions to the people called to jury duty. People approved by both lawyers will serve on the jury.

9. In which step of the trial process can lawyers choose to cross-examine a witness?


B.
C.
D.

Answer: Lawyers may cross-examine witnesses during the trial phase of the trial. This step is where lawyers present all evidence, including witness testimony and physical objects.

10. If you serve on a jury, what should you remember about evidence?

A.

C.
D.

Answer: People do not choose to go to court because they think it is fun. They go because they have a serious issue to address and expect the jury to listen closely to all evidence and arguments. The jury verdict will affect the lives of those involved in the case.

11. The tap water in your home is starting to taste funny. You are concerned about what chemicals might be creating the taste and how they will affect your body. Whom might you speak with to find answers to your questions?

A.
B.

D.

Answer: Local governments have people who work on specific needs in the community, like water quality. If you were concerned about the effects of water on your health, you would contact a local health official.

12. If you observe a problem in your community and have an idea to solve it, what should you do first?

A.
B.

D.

Answer: Citizens are very active in helping resolve local issues. The first step is to learn as much as you can about the issues, including whether local laws or resources exist that relate or address the issue.
