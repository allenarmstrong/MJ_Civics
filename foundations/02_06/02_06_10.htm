What Do I Have to Do?

Imagine it is late 1787. Your town is holding a community picnic to show support for the new government. You are one of a very few people who know about the debate over the Constitution. The townspeople have asked you to explain these events during the picnic in the center of town.

Steps:


© 2011 AP Images
  1. Choose a format. You have choices in how you present this information to the townspeople.
    1. Do you like to write songs? You can create a song using the Song Template.
    2. Are you an artist? You could create a comic strip using the Comic Strip Information.
    3. Do you like to talk? You could write a speech using the Speech Template.
  2. Create your presentation. No matter which format you choose, be sure to include the following:
    1. description of checks and balances
    2. viewpoint of Federalists on ratifying the Constitution
    3. viewpoint of Anti-Federalists on ratifying the Constitution
  3. Have a parent or other trusted adult review your work. They may have suggestions for improvement. Be sure to check your work for errors in grammar and spelling.
  4. Submit your written work to your instructor. You may also choose to include an audio or video file of you performing your song or speech or explaining your comic strip!

Please view the Presentation Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Presentation to Foundations 06 How Are the Powers Balanced?
