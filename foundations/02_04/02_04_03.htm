What Are the Articles of Confederation

The Articles of Confederation were written as the first plan of government for the United States. The Founding Fathers made it a weak government. This means they restricted the powers of the new government to prevent tyrannyTyranny: a government in which a single ruler possesses and abuses absolute power, or the abusive use of power from ruling over them again.

This first government was a confederationConfederation: a group of loosely allied states, such as the United States under the Articles of Confederation. The states agreed to form a partnership. However, each state had the final authority over its own area.

The new government was a busy one. Under the new government, Americans won the Revolutionary War and signed a peace treaty in 1783. The Articles of Confederation were in place for about 10 years. Though it was a brief government, its leaders did achieve some important goals. Examine this chart for other successes of the Articles of Confederation.

Articles of Confederation

  • created first central government of the United States of America
  • organized western territory secured through the peace treaty ending the war with Britain
  • functioned through the end of the Revolutionary War
  • allowed government to:
    • borrow money
    • operate an army and navy
    • ban slavery in the western territory
    • create new states
