How Do the Types of Government Compare?

Many of the types of government overlap. Nations often have aspects of different government types. Each way of organizing government has advantages and disadvantages. A nation's leaders create their own system of laws and government that they believe best meets their needs.

Examine the advantages and disadvantages of the types of government in this chart.

Government Type Advantages Disadvantages
Representative Democracy Citizens have an active role in making decisions. They elect their leaders. The leaders know that they must meet people's needs to stay in office. Leaders who do not represent voters well will not win re-election. In some places, voters could take them out of office early! Making decisions is very time consuming. It is difficult to get the majority of elected leaders to agree on decisions. Leaders do not always make decisions that satisfy the voters.

Confederation Power is focused at the local levels of government. It prevents abuse of power by a large, central government. Members of a confederation can keep their laws and identities while cooperating with others on shared concerns. Central government may be too weak to be effective. There is little to no unity or common laws across members.
