How Has the Supreme Court Helped Interpret Freedom of Speech?

Countless situations in our daily lives relate to freedom of speech. Since the freedom is so broad, but does have limits, active citizens have brought many issues before the courts related to the First Amendment. Some cases even made it all the way to the Supreme CourtSupreme Court: the name for the highest court in the United States, or court of last resort, hears cases related to the U.S. Constitution, where the justices decided whether a law violated rights or people had a certain right under the First Amendment.

Two important cases related to freedom of speech addressed the rights of students in school. Should students have the same protection of rights as adults? In this activity, you will help answer this question. Imagine yourself as a Supreme Court justice hearing the cases of Tinker v. Des Moines and Hazelwood v. Kuhlmeier.
