How Does the Constitution Limit the Government's Power?

The Founding FathersFounding Fathers: name given to the early leaders of the United States who helped design the nation's government were afraid of a government with too much power, which it could use to take away people's basic rights. Therefore, the U.S. ConstitutionU.S. Constitution: plan detailing the structure, functions, and powers of the government of the United States, also known as the Constitution divides the powers of government into three separate branches: legislativeLegislative Branch: the part of government responsible for making laws, executiveExecutive Branch: the part of government responsible for enforcing the laws, and judicialJudicial Branch: the part of government responsible for interpreting the laws and settling disputes. We call this the separation of powersSeparation of Powers: dividing government powers into legislative, executive, and judicial branches.
