Comic Text Version

Scene 1

Erik is watching television. Mom is standing nearby.

[Mom] Erik, you have been sitting on the couch and watching far too much television each day. It’s time to get up and be active for your health. New rule—no TV until after dinner. Off you go!

[Erik] Aww, mom!

Scene 2

Erik is sitting in front of a different TV in his bedroom. His game system appears prominently between him and the TV. He is using it to watch a movie. Mom is standing with hands on hips.

[Mom] Erik! I said no TV until after dinner!

[Erik] But mom, technically it’s not TV. I’m using my game system to watch a movie.

[Mom] That’s the same thing to me! Turn it off!

[Erik] Geesh, sorry…

Scene 3

Erik is playing his personal game system (like a Nintendo DS). Mom is angrier now.

[Mom] Goodness, Erik, what are you doing now?

[Erik] (innocently) Playing my game?

[Mom] I said not until after dinner!

[Erik] But it’s a game, not TV…

[Mom] Turn it off!

[Erik] (sadly) Okay, let me put it on the charger.

Scene 4

Erik is on the home computer in the office. Mom looks like she is about to have a coronary.

[Erik] Mom! Don’t freak out, okay? I’m working on my homework.

[Mom] (through gritted teeth) I don’t see you typing anything.

[Erik] It is, though. I’m watching a program about the effect of video games on the brain, for science.

[Mom] (still irritated) Well, dinner’s ready. Save your work and let’s go.

[Erik] Okay, doing it now, Mom.

[Mom] One more thing—let me clarify the rule. From now on, it’s “No screens until after dinner.”
