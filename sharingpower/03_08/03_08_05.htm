What Issues or Problems Are in Your Community?

Local issues often relate to the environment, public transportation, land use, and education. For example, feral cats may threaten local wildlife or spread disease. Cities with heavy traffic areas may have high accident rates and many road repairs. Every local government uses zoning or land-use laws. Zoning laws determine where people may build businesses, homes, schools, and other structures. People like to manage growth to keep neighborhoods quiet and driving on the streets safe. An education issue might be the need for a new school to reduce overcrowding.

Do any of these issues sound familiar? Do they remind you of an issue specific in your community? Talk to a parent or other trusted adults about issues facing your local area.
