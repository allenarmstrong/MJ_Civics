Cultural Traits Text Version

The United States is home to diverse natural features. The Mississippi River is one of many familiar physical landmarks.

Read each slide to learn about a few U.S. physical landmarks.

Slide 1

An image showing a map of North America, with the United States colored gray, Canada is mostly white with green and yellow color coding. The map has a map key, which shows the density of French-speaking Canadians; green is for high density populations from 5–99.2 %, yellow is for low density 0.1–4.9 %, white is sparse population under 0.1 %. The color green appears mostly in the area of Quebec. All maps in the slideshow have a compass rose with north pointing to the top edge of the map.

Question 1
Where are most French speakers in Canada? East or West

Slide 2

An image showing a map titled "The Foreign-Born Population: 2000." The map also shows a map key .The map is of the United States and each state is colored based on the map key. Illinois is light-blue, California is colored blue. The map key shows a color coding for population density; white or no color is a range of 1.1 to 4.4 %, green is 4.5 to 11 %, light-blue for 11.1 to 13.9, and blue for 14 to 26.2 %.

Question 2
Which state has more people who were born outside the United States? Illinois or California

Slide 3

An image showing a map titled "Prevailing Religion." The map shows all the continents of earth and are all colored based on the map key. The map key shows the different religious groups and a color for each; purple is Catholic Christianity, rose is Orthodox Christianity, blue is Protestant Christianity, dark green is Sunni Islam, green is Shi'ite Islam, light green is Islam (other groups), dark orange is Hinduism, red is Judaism, light orange is Chinese Religion, dark yellow is Thervada Buddhism, yellow Mahayana Buddhism, light brown is Vajrayana Buddhism, Brown is Nature Religions, Gray is for other groups. The United States are colored blue and Canada is colored blue and purple.

Question 3
Which religion is most common in North America? Christianity or Buddhism

Slide 4

An image showing a map titled "Percent American Indian or Alaska Native Alone or in Combination: 2000." The map is of the United States, each state is colored based on the map key which shows; white or no color as 0.4 to 1.4 %, green 1.5 to 4.9, light blue 5.0 to 12.8, and blue 19.0 (AK). Alaska is blue, and states such as California, Oregon, Washington,Nevada, Arizona, New Mexico, Colorado, Idaho, and Montana are green and light blue. States that show up in white are New York, Massachussetts, New Jersey, Connecticut, North Carolina, South Carolina, Maryland, Georgia, Florida, Mississippi, Louisiana, Tennessee, Kentucky, Ohio, and Iowa.

Question 4
Where do you think Native Americans have the greatest influence on culture? East or West

Slide 5

An image shows the number of U.S.-born senior citizens living in each Mexican state in the year 2000. Mexican states near the U.S. border are labeled for the purpose of answering the question. Mexican states with 500-999 U.S.-born senior citizen residents include Baja California Sur, Chihuahua, Coahulia, and Tamaulipas. The Mexican states with 1000-2499 U.S.-born residents include Sonora. Baja California has over 2500 U.S.-born senior citizen residents.

Question 5
Which Mexican state was more popular with U.S.-born seniors in 2000? Baja California or Coahuila
