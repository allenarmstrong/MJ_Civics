Direct Democracy and Republic Text Version

Direct Democracy

  • In direct democracy, citizens vote directly on laws and other issues.
  • The people of ancient Greece were the first to practice direct democracy.
  • Direct democracy can work for a local area, like a small town.
  • Some towns in the Northeast United States still have "town meetings" where citizens vote in person on issues.

Republic

  • In a republic, citizens elect representatives. The elected individuals represent the voters and make decisions on their behalf.
  • The people of ancient Rome had a republic form of government for many years.
  • A republic can work for a large area or population, where not enough time or space exists for every citizen to vote on every issue.
  • The federal government of the United States today is a republic. Citizens vote for representatives to meet in Congress, which is the name for the U.S. legislative branch. They vote on national issues and laws.
