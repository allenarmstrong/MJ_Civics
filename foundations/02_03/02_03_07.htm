How Can I Analyze the Declaration of Independence?

We analyzeAnalyze: to examine something in detail in order to understand it better, often by breaking it down into parts a document, by focusing on small sections of the text one at a time. We figure out the meaning of new words or phrases to understand the whole document.

Do you write your assignments the same way you would talk? You probably do not. People usually pay more attention to correct grammar and spelling in a formalFormal: official writing task. The Declaration of Independence is a formal document. One reason the delegates chose Thomas Jefferson to write the document was because he was a skilled writer. The way people wrote and spoke in the 1700s was different from the way we write and speak today.

In this activity, you will focus on single quotes from the Declaration of Independence.

Introduction

Each question will help you analyze and understand the meaning behind the words.

You may use a dictionary or visit the Virtual Library to help you with new words. In addition, you may wish to review the parts of the Declaration of Independence first. A copy is in your CAP file. Knowing the section that a quote is from will help you determine meaning.

What does the "consent of the governed" mean?





"Consent" means to agree or give permission for something. "The governed" are the people who live under a certain government and must follow its rules. Quote:

"For Quartering large bodies of armed troops among us:"

What is meant by "quartering" in this quote?





"Quartering" troops refers to making arrangements for living or sleeping. The colonists were angry that Britain forced them to allow British soldiers to stay in their homes and barns. The soldiers carried weapons with them and stayed in the colonies for years after the end of the French and Indian War. Many colonists felt threatened by their presence.

Quote:

What is meant by "forbidden?"





"Forbidden" means not permitted, or disallowed. The colonists here are upset that the king has not allowed the governors of the colonies to pass laws the colonists thought were important. He prevented the colonists and governors from taking care of local issues on their own.

Quote:

"For protecting them (British soldiers), by a mock Trial, from punishment for any Murders which they should commit on the Inhabitants of these States:"

Which of the following statements is the best explanation of this quote?





The colonists were upset that British officers involved in the fighting at Lexington and Concord were sent to Britain for questioning and trial. The colonists call it a "mock Trial" because they said the king had no intention to punish any soldiers, who they believed were responsible for the battles. The colonists saw the deaths of the colonists in Massachusetts as murder because the soldiers were supposed to be there for protection of the colonies.

Quote:

"In every stage of these Oppressions We have Petitioned for Redress in the most humble terms: Our repeated Petitions have been answered only by repeated injury. A prince whose character is thus marked by every act which may define a Tyrant, is unfit to be the ruler of a free people."

Which of the following statements is the best explanation of this quote?





The colonists here are explaining that they contacted the king many times with their complaints. King George III did not respond to their petitions. They were insulted that he would ignore them and that he did not try to make up for their troubles. To the colonists, this made the king a tyrant, a leader who abuses power. They are saying that tyrants have no right to rule free people.
