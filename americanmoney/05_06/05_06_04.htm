How Are Federal Priorities Determined?

Remember that Congress and the president represent the people. Interest groupsInterest Group: a group of people with shared goals who work together to influence public policy, the mediaMedia: the various means of mass communication as a whole group, such as television, radio, newspapers, magazines, and the Internet, and current issues are some reasons for budget changes. For example, if scientists predict fewer hurricanes than usual, Congress might decrease the budget for disaster cleanup.

A large portion of the budget is mandatoryMandatory: needing to be done or followed, usually because of an official requirement such as a law spending. For example, paying interestInterest: rate paid or charged for use of money on the national debtDebt: an amount of money, service, or other property owed to someone is set by law. The federal government includes making payments on the interest to the federal debt.

The U.S. Federal Budget

This chart shows a simple breakdown of the federal budget. Notice that the largest portion is for Social Security, Medicare, and Medicaid. These programs are set in law. That means they are mandatory, fixed expenses. The programs provide medical and financial benefits to people who are retired, disabled, or have low income.

Other mandatory programs include student loans, food stamps, and housing support for low-income families. It includes unemployment benefits for people who lose their jobs. The postal service and protection for bank deposits are also fixed in the budget. Paying the interest on the national debt is a fixed expense as well. Together, you can see that mandatory expenses make up well over half the federal budget!

U.S. Defense Spending

The defense-spending category includes military costs. It includes pay and benefits for soldiers. Training, construction, equipment, transportation, and other supplies are in this category. It funds the Army, Navy, Marines, Air Force, and Coast Guard. Spending on the military can be adjusted.

U.S. Non-defense Spending

The last portion of the federal budget is called "Non-defense Spending." Major programs include education, transportation, and law enforcement. Federal law enforcement costs include the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) and Border Patrol. This category also includes scientific research, housing, and the federal court system. It includes many programs that affect Americans daily. This spending category sees the most changes from year to year.

In the next few slides, we will look closer at some of the categories within non-defense spending.

Administration of Justice

This spending category includes the costs of federal law enforcement, criminal justice, federal prisons, and the federal courts. An example cost in this category is paying for the activities of the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), which investigates federal crimes.

General Government

This category includes work of the legislative and executive branches. That includes managing the budget. It also includes management of property, records, and federal workers. The president's pay falls into this category.

International Affairs

This spending category includes international development and security assistance. It also includes humanitarian help and agreements with foreign nations. An example is helping to rebuild areas of the world destroyed by war.

Education, Training, Employment, and Social Services

This spending category includes public schools and universities. It also includes career and job training, and other programs that help people improve their lives.

These three budget categories relate to science and the environment.

Energy

This category includes the information, conservation, and supply of energy. It includes preparing for emergency energy needs. Energy public policy and regulation are also part of these programs.

Science and Technology

This category includes general science and technology research and development. An example is the space program.

Natural Resources and Environment

This spending category includes land and water conservation. Recreation, pollution control, and management of other natural resources are included. Costs of the National Park Service programs are in this category.

Agriculture

This category includes farm subsidies, which is money given to farms to help support them. It includes agricultural research and services. An example would be a university professor working with a local farm to experiment with new growing techniques.


© 2011 Jupiterimages/Photos.com/Thinkstock

Community and Regional Development

This spending category includes community and regional development projects. An example could be a city that requests federal money to restore a war memorial. A group of states might request money to build a new highway between them. It also includes disaster relief and disaster insurance for people within the United States. Help for people forced out of their homes due to earthquakes, hurricanes, widespread fires, tornadoes, or similar would fall into this category.

Transportation

This spending program includes ground, air, and water transportation. Building a new high-speed train to cross several states would be part of this category.

Veterans' Benefits and Services

This spending category includes various services and benefits to military veterans. Medical care for soldiers suffering injury or illness while they were on duty is part of this category.
