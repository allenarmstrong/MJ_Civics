Siblings Arguing Text Version

Dad hands three siblings a chore list. Dad says, "These chores need to be done. It's up to you three to decide how to split up the responsibility."

Youngest says, "I'm the baby. I shouldn't have to do anything."

Middle child says, "No way! Dad handed it to you, too. You are big enough to handle walking the dog and dusting."

Older child says, "That will work. So then, you (pointing to middle child) can take cleaning the bathroom and vacuuming. I will sweep and mop the floors. Then I will â€¦ "

Middle child says "Hey! Why do you get the easy job? Just because you are oldest doesn't mean you get to tell everybody what to do."

Older child says (and is a bit angry), "I wasn't done yet. I will also take cleaning out the hall closet and laundry. Those will take forever."

Youngest says, "Will you go with me to walk the dog?" (to middle child, who looks exasperated)

Middle child: "OK, I'll go with you. We can check the mail when we come back in."

Older child says, "Great! We have everything covered. I just need you (to middle child) to do the bathroom soon so I can clean the floor in there."

Middle child: "Yuck! Now you're going to tell me when to do each job!?"

Youngest says, "We'll be done faster if we work together!" (middle child still annoyed, oldest child smiling at the youngest)
