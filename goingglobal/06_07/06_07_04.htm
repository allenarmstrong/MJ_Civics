How Does the United States Participate?

The United States has been a founder and active member in many international organizations. Examples include the Peace Corps and UN. The U.S. government signs treatiesTreaty: an agreement between two countries or groups of people to begin membership and offer support. It sends ambassadorsAmbassador: an official sent by one country to be its representative in another country to represent the government and people. Ambassadors are U.S. citizens who want to help make a positive difference in the world. U.S. citizens can serve in official positions. They can volunteer through dozens of programs.

Each organization has a different focus. For example, one may have military allianceAlliance: a group of countries that agree to work together for a common purpose focus. Members agree to help protect each other if one is attacked. Another group may focus on economic issues and helping developing countries. It could assist countries with increasing trade and managing their resourcesResource: a material or item that can be used to produce something new.
