How Can Art Inspire Action?

People interested in a social movement may use their talents in art to help bring awareness to an issue. For example, a band might write and perform a song about the effects of war. A photographer might take pictures to show how pollution affects people. An author might write a story about a family that tells of their struggles with povertyPoverty: the state of being poor, not having the means to take care of basic needs such as food, clothing, and housing. Music, photos, books, and other art are forms of free speechFree speech: right to express opinions in public. The artists use this freedom to express their own feelings about problems they see in society.

Many people find works of art to be very powerful. The art can make us feel sympathy for those affected. It may even make us angry or sad about the situation. We might wonder if others know about the issue and question whether they are doing anything to help. Hearing and seeing these works of art may inspire more people to get involved. Art can inspire people to act on their civic responsibilityCivic responsibility: obligations of citizens to their community to help others.
