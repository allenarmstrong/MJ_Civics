How Do These Ideas Affect the United States?

Scarcity, choice, supply and demand, and opportunity cost are the driving forces that shape the U.S. economy. When people are free to make choices, such as starting a business, we call this a market economyMarket Economy: an economic system that relies upon supply and demand to distribute resources and determine prices. As individuals, they consider the effects of prices and opportunity cost.

The United States has a mixed market economyMixed Market: an economy with individual economic freedom mixed with some government economic planning and regulation. This means that it is a market economy, but the government also makes some decisions that affect the choices people can make. For example, U.S. law says that a business must pay employees a minimum amount per hour of work. This government decision protects workers but limits the business owner's choice in what to pay employees. In a mixed market economy, the government makes some decisions to protect the people from the negative effects of supply and demand.
