Why Protect the Rights of Suspects?


© 2011 AP Images

As you saw with Al Fraydo, without certain rights, law officers could arrest people and take them to jail without the accused knowing the reason for their arrest. Law enforcement could hold them forever and without being able to contact family or an attorney. They might never appear before a court or have a trialTrial: a formal legal process in court to settle disputes or determine the guilt or innocence of those accused of crimes for the crime.

In the United States, a person is assumed innocent unless proven guilty in a court of law. If people do not know the chargesCharge: an accusation of wrongdoing against them, have access to an attorney, or appear in court they cannot defend themselves against the charges. Without rights protected in the Constitution and upheld by the courts, innocent people could spend years in jail.
