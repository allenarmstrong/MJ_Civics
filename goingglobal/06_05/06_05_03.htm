What Are Domestic and Foreign Policy?


© 2011 AP Images

Domestic policy is the decisions, laws, and programs that address issues within a country. It is like the rules you have inside your home. The lawmaking process in Congress determines domestic policy. Leaders use input from the people and other officials.

Foreign policy is the decisions and programs that affect relations with other countries. It is like the rules you follow when you are away from home. Government decisions that affect programs and people in other countries are foreign policies.

Domestic and foreign policy can affect each other. For example, many foreign policies require funding. The money might come from the federal budgetFederal Budget: plan or record of income and expenses for the United States, which is a domestic policy. Another example is how immigrationImmigration: the act or process of people entering a new country to live there permanently policies can affect relationships with other countries.
