How Does Geography Affect Standard of Living?

Many things affect the productive lives of people in a country. Examples include culture and government structure. GeographyGeography: the study of physical and political features on Earth's surface also plays an important role in how countries grow and change. It is one important factor that affects standard of living data such as GDP. In this lesson, we are focusing on the impact of geography.

The geography of an area includes its land and water features, natural resources, and climate. Geography affects what the people can produce and how they interact with others. It shapes where people live, how they live, whom they trade with, and the way they organize government.

A country's people will trade for products they cannot produce themselves. This helps increase their standard of living. Some countries trade more than others because of geography. For example, the United States shares borders with Mexico and Canada. It makes sense that these countries would trade more with each other than distant countries because it is easier to move goods shorter distances.

Most countries of North America border an ocean. Some, such as the United States, border two oceans, a bay or gulf, and have large rivers. Countries with access to large waterways have a lower transportation cost for goods. It is less expensive for them to trade with distant countries. The Mississippi River makes it easier to trade products produced in the central areas of Canada and the United States.
