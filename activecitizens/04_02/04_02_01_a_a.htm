Case Practice: Answers Text Version

Instructions: Read each situation. Answer "Yes" if you believe the law or laws related to that situation are acceptable under the Constitution. Answer "No" if you believe they are unacceptable.

  1. Under California law, a person was sent to jail for 50 years up to a life sentence for being caught stealing three times. Yes

    Case of Lockyer v. Andrade (2003)
    The Supreme Court ruled that the California "three strikes" law was constitutional. This law requires the state court to give a much longer jail or prison sentence to those who are convicted of serious crimes three or more times. The Supreme Court ruled that this law is not cruel or unusual punishment, banned by the 8th Amendment.
  2. Natural-born citizens can have their U.S. citizenship taken away as a punishment. No

    Case of Trop v. Dulles (1958)
    ? The Supreme Court ruled that taking away U.S. citizenship was cruel and unusual punishment.
  3. A state government can ban the teaching of any language other than English. No

    Case of Meyer v. Nebraska (1923)
    The Supreme Court ruled that a state could not ban foreign-language instruction. Under the 14th Amendment, government must respect the legal rights of all citizens. The Supreme Court ruled that this ban limits the right to knowledge and education.
  4. A person who burns the American flag in a public protest can be sentenced to prison. No

    Case of Texas v. Johnson (1989)
    The Supreme Court ruled that acts of protest, such as burning the American flag, were protected forms of free speech under the 1st Amendment.
  5. A police officer may search passengers of a vehicle stopped for a traffic law violation. Yes

    Case of Arizona v. Johnson (2009)
    The Supreme Court ruled that police officers could search passengers of stopped vehicles under certain conditions. An officer must have reason to suspect the person is commiting a crime to legally perform the search.
