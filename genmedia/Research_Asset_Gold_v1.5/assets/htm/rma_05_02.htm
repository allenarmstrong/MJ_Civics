What is reliable information? How to perform research

Step 5: Evaluating Library Sources

Alicia collects three books from the library for her research. She starts to analyze the credibility of the library sources collected. The screen displays information about the three books that Alicia has collected.

Examine Alicia’s library research results to determine which documents she found that are credible and which are not. Identify the credibility of the sources from the library.

Book#1

Book Details

Urban Legends

Author: Jerome Scholar, PhD

Publication date: 2010

Documentation: Index of sources available in the back

Snippet of the Book: A look at the most famous debunked urban legends of our time.

Book Details

Why I’ll Never Go Scuba Diving, a Memoir

Author: Julia Singer

Publication date: 2000

Documentation: None

Snippet of the Book: A memoir of a famous opera singer, documenting her fear of the water. She cites the legend of the scuba diver and explains that it gives her nightmares.

Book#3

Book Details

Fires in California

Author: Jackson Firefighter

Publication date: 1996

Documentation: Index of sources available in the back

Snippet of the Book: A detailed history of California fires and how they have affected the general public. The author cites the legend of the scuba diver and explains that there is no evidence for it being true.

[Check Your Answer]

Book#1

The book has been recently published and is written by a well-known author. Also, the book provides source details in the index and does not express any specific opinion that can be deduced from the summary of the book. Hence, the book can be considered as a credible source for research.

Book#2

The book was published long ago and does not provide any documentation to support the ideas. Also, the summary of the book represents that the content of the book is all about the author’s opinion; hence, it cannot be considered credible for research.

Book#3

Though the book was published long ago, it is written by a well-known author. Also, the book provides source details in the index and does not express a specific opinion of the author which can be deduced from the summary. Hence, the book can be considered as a credible source for research.

Helpful Hints

Look for answers to the following key questions:

  • Why was this document written?
  • What option does the document represent?
  • When was the document published?
  • How good is the information?
  • How well documented is the work?
