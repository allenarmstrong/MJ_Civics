American Toys Timeline Text Version

A timeline is shown from 1900 to 2000. Different points along the timeline mark when a popular American toy was first introduced.

1903 – Crayola Crayons

Crayons are still popular today. By age 10, an American child has probably worn down well over 700 crayons!

1916 – Lincoln Logs

Inspired by one of his father's buildings, the son of famous architect Frank Lloyd Wright invented Lincoln Logs. Lincoln Logs were one of the first toys to be advertised on television!

1929 – Yo-yo

The yo-yo has been around for well over 2,000 years. In the United States, Donald Duncan purchased a yo-yo factory and business from Pedro Flores. Duncan brought national popularity to the yo-yo, which it still has today.

1935 – Monopoly

Monopoly began its rise to fame in 1935 with several Americans contributing ideas to the game's look and function. Today, millions of people around the world play this popular board game.

1948 – Slinky

Richard James was a navy engineer in World War Two when he had the idea for the Slinky. He even invented the machine that made them, using 80 feet of steel wire!

1950 – Silly Putty

Silly putty was never meant to be a children's toy! A great excess of the rubber inspired one storeowner to put it in plastic eggs and sell it to children. Now it comes in 15 colors!

1960 – Etch-a-Sketch

An American toy company saw the "Magic Screen" at a toy fair in Germany. They began producing and selling them to Americans in 1960. The toy still works the same way it did when it was invented!

1978 – Rubik's Cube

The Rubik's Cube has been challenging, inspiring, and frustrating Americans for over 30 years. It has more than 43 quintillion possible combinations but only one solution!

1984 – Transformers

The toys that inspired comic books, cartoons, and live action movies began as models in Japan! New characters have appeared over the years, including a female in the late 1990s, but none ever reached the popularity of Optimus Prime and Megatron.

1998 – Furby

Over 40 million Furby toys were sold in its first three years. Americans learned to speak "Furbish" to interact with their robotic pets!

2000 – Razor Scooter

A bicycle maker in Taiwan invented this sleek scooter. Over five million Razors were sold in its first year! Many young Americans have enjoyed riding the Razor.
