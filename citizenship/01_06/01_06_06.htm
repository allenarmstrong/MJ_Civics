Why Does Good Citizenship Matter?

The U.S. government exists by the consentConsent: approval or agreement of its citizens. The citizens agree to support the government. They pay taxes and choose people to represent them. In return, the government provides services like police protection and education. This is the contractContract: formal agreement between citizens and government. Each side has duties to the other in return for benefits.

Without government, citizens would not have protection for their rights. Without good citizens, government could not exist to give protection.

Good citizens work hard to meet their responsibilities. They take very active roles in their community. Some write letters to government officials to inform them about problems. Volunteers help in hospitals, schools, and libraries. Some even organize protestsProtest: a public demonstration of disapproval if they believe people's rightsRights: freedoms protected by law are threatened.
