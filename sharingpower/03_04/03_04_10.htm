What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be ready to make some laws! You will use your knowledge of the lawmaking process to explain how an idea of yours may become a law.

Steps


© 2011 AP Images
  1. Choose an Idea–Think of a possible new state or federal law or public policy that you think would improve society. You may choose one of the ideas below or choose your own to discuss with a trusted adult. If you choose your own, be sure to ask your instructor's permission first.
    • requiring all American citizens to attend college
    • expanding Internet access to all homes
    • prohibiting littering and dumping waste in public spaces
    • funding free health care clinics that are available 24 hours per day, seven days a week
  2. Explain Your Idea–Explain why you think this idea would make a good law. Summarize the discussion you had with a trusted adult about your idea. Who would benefit? Who may not support the idea and why? What adjustments might you make to gain more support?
  3. Explain or Illustrate the Process–Explain or make a chart to show the steps your idea would have to go through to become a law at the federal and state levels of government. Research how your local government works. You may use the Bill Template to complete your assignment.

Please view the Bill Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.
