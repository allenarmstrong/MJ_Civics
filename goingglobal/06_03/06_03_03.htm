What Are North America's Natural Resources?

Resources are what we use to produce goods and services. The four major types of resources that a country uses are:

  • Land–the natural products of earth like water, wood, and minerals
  • LaborLabor: supply of work or workers–people who have the skills to create goods and offer services
  • CapitalCapital: cash for investment, or the tools and knowledge needed to produce something–tools, equipment, money, and information
  • EntrepreneurEntrepreneur: person who takes a risk to start a new business and try new ideas–the leadership and ideas needed to start and grow business

Land resources are also called natural resourcesNatural Resource: material found in nature that people could use for their own benefit. It includes not just arable landArable Land: land that can be used to grow crops, but other resources that come from Earth. Different countries have varied natural resources. Some natural resources are renewableRenewable: not likely to run out because of new growth or great supply while others are non-renewableNon-renewable: likely to run out because the supply cannot keep up with use.

North America is the continent that includes Canada, Mexico, and the United States. It also includes Central America and the Caribbean countries. Examine these maps to see what resources North America contains and how it compares to other parts of the world.

Fresh Water Resources Per Capita


Fresh water is an important resource to a country. People need fresh water to survive. They cannot drink ocean water because it contains salt. Growing crops and raising farm animals requires fresh water. Fresh water is a renewable but limited resource. That means that humans could use it faster than it can renew itself if people are not careful. As you can see on this map, available fresh water varies around the world. "Per capita" means by each person, so to make this map, the amount of fresh water resources for a country was divided by its population.

Question:
Which country of North America has the most available fresh water per capita?





Canada has the greatest amount of fresh water per capita in North America. It has over 10,000 cubic meters available per person, per year. However, within each country, fresh water resources can vary by location. For example, Florida has a greater challenge with fresh water access than most other U.S. states.

Natural Forest Cover


Trees are an important natural resource. This map shows the portion of each county's land that has trees growing closely together. Trees provide clean air to breathe, nutrients for soil, and help protect water resources. Trees are very important to human building activities as well. Deforestation refers to land areas where humans and natural events consume trees but do not replace them. Greenland has no significant amount of forest area.

Question:
For most of North America, what portion of the land does natural forest cover?





Most of North America has between 15 and 30 percent natural forest cover. Imagine a checkerboard with 100 squares, and between 15 and 30 of the squares are covered with trees. That is the portion of natural forest cover in North America.

Top Countries with Crude Oil Reserves


The countries in color on this map are the countries with the most crude oil reserves. Crude oil means the natural oil in the ground before humans process it into motor oil or other fuels. It is an extremely valuable resource because it is limited and humans use it in so many activities.

Question:

Mexico and the United States are the only countries in North America with oil reserves among the top in the world. Saudi Arabia is in Southwest Asia, not North America. The best answer is Cuba, since it is in North America but not a top country for crude oil reserves.

Top Countries with Gold


Gold is a valuable natural mineral resource. We most often think of gold for jewelry, but it has many other uses. The value of gold to humans stretches far back into history. People have used it as money for trade and in banking. Today, it has uses ranging from medicine to glassmaking, in addition to the still-popular jewelry and decoration.
