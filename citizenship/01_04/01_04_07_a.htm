Ecology Citizenship Text Version

Introduction

Hello! This is Ella Erosion and Manny Mangrove. Here at Eco-News we work hard to bring you news on issues of ecology. Our mission is to bring people together to help make our environment better. Help from citizens is so important to the success of our mission. Thank you for tuning in!

In Florida, the Tampa Bay area faces many ecological issues. Oyster beds and mangroves are important to preventing beach erosion and filtering the water. Yet these resources are threatened. Many people move into the Tampa Bay area each year. The following list includes some of the effects of this growth:

  • Construction has destroyed large portions of the mangrove forest.
  • Wastewater from sewage pipes pollutes the bay water.
  • Illegal oyster harvesting has decreased the oyster population.

We want to protect the ecology of the area and still welcome new people. We need your help!

You will be a key player in helping protect Tampa Bay. You have many choices to make. Let's get started!

Scenario 1

The state of Florida is giving you $1 billion to address Tampa Bay's ecological issues. Local fisheries complain that the biggest issue is the oysters. Restaurants do not have enough to serve their customers. The government issues licenses to certain companies to harvest the oysters. This protects the oyster population. However, many people harvest them without a license, meaning too many oysters are taken from the bay.

What will you do first to help Tampa Bay?

A

Use some of the grant money for lawyers. Seek arrests of people who harvest oysters without a license.

B

Ask volunteers for help restoring oyster beds. Use some of the grant money to build special domes for the oysters.

Scenario 2A

Police report difficulty catching illegal harvesters. People speed away on their boats, creating waves that smack the shore and oyster beds. The oyster population begins to grow because illegal harvesters are chased away. However, the beach erosion continues. Scientists say part of the problem is dying mangroves.

What will you do next?

A

Scenario 3AA

The mangrove restoration efforts are successful. However, the water in the bay is polluted. Some mangroves die, and people do not want to eat the bay oysters. Some drainage pipes from the city lead into the bay. People are dumping chemicals into the drainage system. Water pollution drives the manatees and other marine mammals to find clean water, putting them in danger from boats. The government money is nearly gone.

What will you do next?

A

Use grant money to build water treatment plants for the drainage water. This will remove chemicals from the water before they can reach and pollute the bay. Require boaters to take special precautions to prevent manatee injury.
Go to 4A

B

Use grant money to educate people about the gentle manatees. Post signs near drains warning people not to dump chemicals there. Continue efforts to increase oyster growth because they filter the bay water.

Final Conclusion

Tampa Bay Watch is a real group working on the ecology of Tampa Bay. Its members focus on many issues that relate to each other, as you did in this activity. Residents, visitors, and government are all helping to restore the bay area. Many nearby students help at school and on-site. Thousands of volunteers have worked with Bay Watch since 1993.

Groups like Tampa Bay Watch exist around the world. Your own community likely has groups of people working on ecological issues right now!
