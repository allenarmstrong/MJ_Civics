How Does the Process Work?

The U.S. government collects over two trillion dollars in tax revenueRevenue: income, often used in reference to government or business income each year. That's $2,000,000,000,000. It is a staggering amount to imagine. The taxes fund over a thousand federal programs. Organizing the budget for such a large sum is a very complex task. However, we can simplify the process into three main steps.

Members of CongressCongress: official name for the legislative branch of the U.S. government and the president work together on the nation's budget. Some budget items are fixedFixed: paid regularly and amount does not change or only slightly, like rent payments and car insurance, so that spending on them cannot change without major changes to federal law. Officials receive countless requests from people and groups to make specific changes. It is impossible to fund every want of the people.
