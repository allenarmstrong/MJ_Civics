Investigate!


© 2011 AP Images

What problems or issues face your community? For example, litterLitter: scattered objects or rubbish in parks and other public areas is a problem in many communities. To help solve the problem, concerned citizens may investigate why litter is a problem so they may inform others, gain support, and work together to solve the problem. They might organize a park cleanup or create pamphlets or Internet campaigns to encourage people to keep the park clean for everyone to enjoy.

You can investigate local issues in many ways. Research the problem in the library or on the Internet. Survey your family and friends. Talk to people in the community who would know about the issue. If you choose to interview someone to help investigate your issue, view these How To Interview tips beforehand.

Use the Investigate Sheet to begin researching issues. Be sure to save this sheet into your folder. You will turn it in to your instructor.
