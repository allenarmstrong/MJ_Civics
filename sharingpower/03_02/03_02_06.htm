What Is In Florida's Constitution?

The current Constitution of the State of Florida has been in effect since 1968. Similar to the U.S. Constitution, it contains the following:

  • an introduction, called the Preamble
  • 12 articles, or sections
  • amendments, or formal changes, which appear as notes in the section that the change affects

Examine this image to see the organization of Florida's government under its state constitution. Notice how it compares to the national government. The names and roles of the legislative sections and state supreme court mirror those in the U.S. Constitution.
