CAP File

Part 1

Answer the following questions in your own words.

  1. What is public policy? Why is citizen action important in public policy?

     

  2. What issues in your community (local or state) interest you? (Suggestions include environmental issues, land-use issues, transportation issues, and education.)

     

  3. Choose one of the issues from the above question to use for research in Part 2. Which do you think is most important to the community? Make sure that your issue is a state or local issue that involves public policy. Ask your instructor or a trusted adult if you are unsure whether your issue will work for this activity.

     

Part 2

Conduct your background research on the issue. An example entry appears for you. However, it is just an example. You should choose a different issue of interest to you and your community.

Questions Answers Where You Found the Information (Be specific.)
Why is the issue a problem? How does it affect the community? Example: Mandy discovered that feral cats are a danger to themselves, to wildlife, and to humans.



What groups, organizations, and individuals might support your position on the issue?



 
What government agencies address or would address this issue?



 
Can you find any laws or ordinances in place that relate to your issue? What are they?



 
What other questions do you need to have answered about the issue? List them and the answers you find for them here.



 

Part 3

Identify the solution and judging criteria.

What is your solution to this issue or problem?

 

What important criteria will you use to judge public policy alternatives? Do you have other criteria specific to your issue? Criteria should include being low in cost, easy to carry out, and supported by the community.

 

Part 4

Identify public policy alternatives and the positive and negative aspects of each. Evaluate the ideas. Do they meet your criteria?

 

Public Policy Alternative Positives Negatives
Example: Managing and supporting feral cat colonies Spaying and neutering cats help prevent population growth. Many citizens support the idea. A community may be violating wildlife laws by supporting feral cat colonies. Officials may not support it.




   




   




   

Part 5

Talk with others about your issue and public policy alternatives.

Who have you spoken with about this issue? What was the outcome of those conversations?

 

What do you think is the best action or solution for this issue? Why do you recommend it?

 

Part 6

Choose the best public policy alternative.

After your research and discussion, which public policy alternative do you think is best and why?

 

Part 7

Make your citizen action plan.

What will you do to get your public policy idea made official? Who do you need to contact?
