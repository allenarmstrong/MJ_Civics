Discovery Education

Discovery Education™ is a digital video and image library resource provided for your use in this course. Your course has video links on the lesson pages for you to view. You can also use Discovery Education™ for your course projects or research.

Video links on your lesson pages

When you click on a Discovery Education™ link in the course, you should be automatically logged into the Discovery Education™ site and taken to the video you are to view. If you cannot access the videos, please contact your instructor.

Using Discovery Education™ for your course projects

To use Discovery Education™ for your course projects or research, you will have to login to the Discovery Education™ site.

  1. Visit the Discovery Education website. You will be brought to the Discovery Education™ login page.

      Once you have logged in, it will take you to the search page.
  2. You can search for videos and images by keyword, subject and grade, curriculum standard, or all available titles.
  3. Your search results will provide you with a listing of available videos, images, calendar events, and articles related to your search query.

Downloading and Using Discovery Education™ Videos

  1. Select a video title you wish to view.
  2. Select the "Show Media Controls" tab.
  3. Select your file format: Windows Media Player or QuickTime.
  4. You will see buttons next to your desired video. To stream or view a video, select the "Arrow" button.
  5. To download a video clip, follow the instructions below:

Windows Users

  • Right–click on the "Disk" button next to the video you would like to download.
  • From the menu that appears, select the "Save Target As..." (Internet Explorer), "Save Link Target As..." (Netscape/Mozilla), or "Save Link As..." (Firefox) option.
  • In the file explorer window that appears, browse to the location on your computer where you would like to save the selected video and press the "Save" button.
    Note: You may give your video a different name, but you should use the three–letter file extensions such as ".asf" or ".asx" for Windows Media Player® or ".mov" for QuickTime.
  • To view full screen in Windows Media Player while the video is playing:

    Option 1: Go to "View">"Full Screen." Hit "Esc" when finished.

    Option 2: Click your Alt tab and "Enter" key at the same time. Hit "Esc" when finished.

    Option 3: Right–click in the video window and scroll to "Full Screen." Hit "Esc" when finished.

Macintosh Users

  1. While holding the CTRL key on your keyboard, click on the "Disk" button next to the video segment you would like to download.
  2. From the menu that appears, select the "Download Link to Disk..." (Internet Explorer), "Download Linked File As..." (Safari), or "Save Link As..." (Netscape/Mozilla) option.
  3. In the file explorer window that appears, browse to the location on your computer where you would like to save the selected video and press the "Save" button.
    Note: You may give your video a different name, but you should use the three-letter file extensions such as ".asf" or ."asx" for Windows Media Player® or ".mov" for QuickTime.
  4. To view the video at full screen in Windows Media Player while the video is playing:
  • Option 1: Go to "View" and scroll to "Full Screen." Hit "Esc" when finished.
  • Option 2: Click the "open apple" next to the space bar and the number "4" at the same time. Hit "Esc" when finished.
  1. To view the video at full screen in QuickTime Player while the video is playing:
  • Option 1: Go to "View" and scroll to "Full Screen." Hit "Esc" when finished.
  • Option 2: Go to "Movie" and "Double Size" or "Full Screen" depending on your version of QuickTime. Hit "Esc" when finished.

Using Discovery Education™ Images

  1. You can search for images by keyword in the "search" field.
  2. Your search results will indicate if any images are available related to your search query.
  3. Click on the "Images" link found in the search results summary at the top of the screen.
  4. Click on the image title you wish to view. (An image title is listed in the text color of gold.)
  5. When you find the photo you want to use, click on a desired resolution to display the image for viewing or downloading:
  • Small—very small images—the size displayed in the Search Results list. 
  • Medium—medium sized images that are ideal for most projects (72 dpi).
  • Large—large images that are very detailed, even when printed (300 dpi).
  1. To download an image, follow the instructions below.

Windows Users

  • Select an image to download. Position your mouse over the displayed image and right–click to download: scroll down the pop-up menu and select "Save Target As…" or "Save Link As…" depending on the browser you are using. Change the file name and save to a designated folder.
  • To view the image, browse to where you saved the image. Double click the image to launch your default viewer. (If your default viewer does not launch, select Start, Programs, Accessories, Entertainment—Select "Paint" and then go to "File" and then "Open" the image from there.)

Macintosh Users

  • Select an image to download. Position your mouse over the displayed image and control–click to display a menu. Choose "Save file to disk." You will see a pop–up screen that asks you to save your image. You may give your image a different name, but save the file extension .jpg. Navigate to a folder to save your image.
  • To view the image, navigate to the folder where you saved your image. Double–click the image to launch your default viewer.
