Federal Budget Process Text Version

Steps in the U.S. Federal Budget Process

  1. President's Budget Request

    President sends budget request for upcoming year. It contains details of plans for spending and revenue collection. It also lists change requests from previous year's budget.

  2. Congressional Budget Resolution

    Congress debates the president's request. Members question officials on requested changes. They make their own changes and vote on them. They must pass the resolution for the changes to take effect. If they do not, the prior year's budget will be followed again.

  3. Enforcing the Resolution

    Spending bills that Congress passes must fit within the approved budget. Congress can make exceptions under specific rules.
