What Do I Have to Do?

Imagine your family is planning to move to a foreign country. They ask you to make a recommendation for a new location.


© 2011 Map Resources

Steps

  1. Choose a country of North America to research other than the United States. View the map to help you choose.
  2. Research your country. Helpful resources include the Virtual Library and the CIA World Factbook. Locate the following information about the country you choose:

    Standard of Living Data

    • Total population
    • GDP per capita
    • Literacy rate
    • Average life expectancy
    • Poverty rate
    • Other data that interests you

    Geography Influence

    Explain in paragraph form how the following have affected settlement, trade, and government in your country.

    • Land features
    • Water features
    • Climate
    • Location
    • Natural resources
    • Industries

  3. Compare the standard of living of the country you chose with the United States. This should be written in a paragraph of at least 3–5 sentences.
  4. Examine the information you have gathered. Determine whether or not you recommend this country for your family’s new home. Write down your reasons why you do or do not recommend it in at least three sentences.
  5. Create your recommendation to present to your family. You may choose the format of your presentation. Suggestions include podcast, video, slideshow, and article.

There are many 21st-century tools available for creating and submitting your work in the online environment. For more information on tools your school uses, contact your instructor or visit the Web 2.0 tools area.
