What Is the Circular Flow of Money?

Banks are businesses, just like a skate and surf shop or a bakery. They are different in that the service they provide helps people and businesses grow and reach financial goals. Money is constantly flowing between households and businesses in the economy. A circular-flow diagramCircular-Flow Diagram: visual representation of the movement of resources, money, and products between households, businesses, and government shows this relationship.

People provide resources to businesses, such as their laborLabor: supply of work or workers. Businesses, in turn, provide goods and services to households. They use money to make these trades, such as pay for a person's work and payment for the cost of supplies.

Think of the circles as water pipes. What happens when there is too much water pressure? The pipes could burst. If there is too little water pressure, the water in the pipes gets stale and the pipes may rot. Just as you would want a steady flow of water in the pipes, the Federal Reserve aims to keep a steady flow of money, resources, and goods throughout the economy.
