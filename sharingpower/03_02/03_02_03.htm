What Is the Purpose of State Constitutions?

The U.S. ConstitutionU.S. Constitution: plan detailing the structure, functions, and powers of the government of the United States, also known as the Constitution requires every state to have a form of government called a republicRepublic: a form of government where the people elect leaders to make decisions on their behalf. This means that the people rule through representatives they elect to make decisions on their behalf. Each state's constitution describes the structure and functions of its government.

The 10th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution reserves powers to the states that are not assigned to the national government or denied to the states. Thus, the states have many functions, such as running public schools. State constitutions are longer and have more detail than the U.S. Constitution.
