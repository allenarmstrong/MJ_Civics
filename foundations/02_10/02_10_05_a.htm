Recap Quiz Text Version

  1. The Baron de Montesquieu wrote about
    1. bill of rights
    2. separation of powers
    3. natural rights
    4. common good
  2. Which of the following documents had an immediate effect on convincing colonists to declare independence?
    1. English Bill of Rights
    2. Mayflower Compact
    3. Magna Carta
    4. Common Sense
  3. Which of the following is a true statement about the Declaration of Independence?
    1. It accuses King George III of breaking the social contract.
    2. It divides the U.S. government into three separate branches.
    3. It proclaims permanent loyalty to the British king.
    4. It promises an immediate end to slavery.
  4. The Anti-Federalists
    1. wanted more protection for individual rights before they would ratify the Constitution
    2. argued that the Constitution need to be ratified as soon as possible
    3. believed the federal government should have more power than the state governments
    4. suspected the Federalists of plotting against ratifying the Constitution
  5. In John Locke's idea of the "social contract,"
    1. government has all the power and the people have no choice but to follow its laws
    2. people have the right to form a new government if the current one abuses the people's rights
    3. the people have all the power and the government is only for ceremony
    4. if the people break the social contract, the government can start a rule of tyranny to regain control
  6. The Declaration of Independence contains a list of
    1. natural rights of the colonists
    2. states that would now be independent
    3. military divisions ready to fight against British rule
    4. grievances against the British king
  7. Which of the following pairs do not match?
    1. executive—enforces the laws
    2. legislative—makes the laws
    3. president—creates the laws
    4. judicialinterprets the laws
  8. If a person is arrested for making rude remarks about the leaders in government, which amendment should the person name in his or her defense?
    1. First Amendment
    2. Second Amendment
    3. Fourth Amendment
    4. Eighth Amendment
  9. Which of the following best describes the first step of the amendment process?
    1. Proposal by three-quarters of Congress or the states
    2. Ratification by two-thirds of the states
    3. Proposal by two-thirds of Congress or the states
    4. Ratification by three-quarters of the states
  10. The fact that the president may veto an a law that Congress passes is an example of
    1. checks and balances
    2. natural rights
    3. independence
    4. unconstitutional law
  11. How does the U.S. Constitution both safeguard and limit individual rights?
    1. It lists individual rights to limit individuals from exercising rights they do not have while protecting the rights they do have.
    2. It safeguards rights by preventing government from creating any laws that would limit rights, so it limits the rights of government officials.
    3. It lists the rights of Americans as well as the exceptions to those rights.
    4. It contains a list of rights that it protects but allows governments to create laws that restrict individual rights for the common good.
  12. The Supreme Court may rule a law unconstitutional
    1. only after citizens challenge the law through the courts
    2. as soon as Congress passes the law
    3. six years after the president signs it into law
    4. only when a majority of citizens across the nation demand it
