Going Global State Standards

Going Global Unit Lessons State Standards
Lesson 00: Going Global Introduction N/A
Lesson 01: How Do Other Nations Govern? SS.7.C.3.1 Compare different forms of government (direct democracy, representative democracy, socialism, communism, monarchy, oligarchy, autocracy).
SS.7.C.3.2 Compare parliamentary, federal, confederal, and unitary systems of government.
Lesson 02: How Does Geography Affect Society? SS.7.G.2.3 Explain how major physical characteristics, natural resources, climate, and absolute and relative location have influenced settlement, economies, and inter-governmental relations in North America.
SS.7.E.3.4 Compare and contrast the standard of living in various countries today to that of the United States using gross domestic product (GDP) per capita as an indicator.
Lesson 03: Why Should Nations Diversify? SS.7.E.3.3 Compare and contrast a single resource economy with a diversified economy.
SS.7.G.3.1 Use maps to describe the location, abundance, and variety of natural resources in North America.
Lesson 04A: What's New in the World? (Advanced) SS.7.E.3.3 Compare and contrast a single resource economy with a diversified economy.
Lesson 05: What Are National Policies? SS.7.C.4.1 Differentiate concepts related to United States domestic and foreign policy.
Lesson 06: How Does Currency Affect Trade? SS.7.E.3.1 Explain how international trade requires a system for exchanging currency between and among nations.
SS.7.E.3.2 Assess how the changing value of currency affects trade of goods and services between nations.
Lesson 07: How Do We Participate in a World Community? SS.7.C.4.2 Recognize government and citizen participation in international organizations.
SS.7.C.4.3 Describe examples of how the United States has dealt with international conflicts.
