Self-Check Activity

Question 1 of 9:

Image is a map and the map shows a land mass with a large white section on the west labeled as a "Basin". To the east of the basin we see a large body of water which is labeled as "Lake". The lake has color differences to the northern and southern sections; the north is a lighter blue than the south

The size of the Great Salt Lake Changes with the amount of rainfall.

Correct Answer: a. True.
Feedback: The Great Salt Lake has no outlet besides evaporation, so rainfall has great impact.

Question 2 of 9:

Image is a map and the map shows a land mass with a large white section on the west labeled as a "Basin". To the east of the basin we see a large body of water which is labeled as "Lake". The lake has color differences to the northern and southern sections; the north is a lighter blue than the south

The Great Salt Lake was larger in 1972 than it was in 1987.

Correct Answer: b. False.
Feedback: The early1980s brought heavy rains to the lake, making it larger than it had been in the 1960s or 1970s.

Question 3 of 9:

Image is a map and the map shows a land mass with a large white section on the west labeled as a "Basin". To the east of the basin we see a large body of water which is labeled as "Lake". The lake has color differences to the northern and southern sections; the north is a lighter blue than the south

A human-made structure affected the Great Salt Lake's salt content.

Correct Answer: a. True.
Feedback: The railroad causeway stops the lake water from mixing as usual. The northern section contains more salt.

Question 4 of 9:

Image of a map titled "Growth of Washington, D.C. Area" A Key shows the color red represents the year 1900, the color yellow represents 1950, and the color green represents 1990. Washington is represented on the map with the three colors. 1900 is a small red dot, 1950 is a small ring which surrounds the red dot, and 1990 is a much larger ring surrounding the ring for 1950. A body of water is also named "Chesapeake Bay" to the east. The rings do not touch the bay.

Since it's founding, Washington D.C., has expanded in size.

Correct Answer: a. True.
Feedback: This map uses color to show growth over time.

Question 7 of 9:

Image of a map Titeled "The Korean War 1950-1953". China is labeled in the northwest corner and North Korea conects to the southeast corner of China. South Korea is attached to the south of North Korea and they are both surrounded by water on the east and west. The 38th parallel divides North and South Korea, making the border between the two countries.

Cities are labeled, Chongjin in northeast of North Korea and Pyongyang in the southeast. Seoul in the northwest of South Korea, and Pohang in the southeast. A text box directs our attention to an arror of troop movement and notes that the direction of the arrow shows direction of movement of troops involved in the war. A map key shows red for Chinese troop movement, green for North Korea troop movement, and blue for U.S./U.N. troop movement.

A trio of red arrows moves from China into the Korea's, one in central North Korea, the others into the northern region of South Korea, near Seoul. There are three green arrows moving from North Korea into South Korea. We also see three blue arrows near Pohang, two moving north from southern South Korea to meet the Noth Korean troops and another from the eastern body of water. There are also two move blue arrows coming form the western body of water entering northern and central South Korea Below Seoul. the last blue arrow is moving into central North Korea from the eastern body of water.

Chinese troops attacked by moving north.

Correct Answer: b. False.
Feedback: Chinese troops attacked from the north, meaning they moved south.
