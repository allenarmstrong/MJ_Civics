What Are the Duties and Responsibilities of Good Citizens?

Some of the responsibilities of citizens are required by law. We refer to these as "civic duties."

One citizen duty is to obey laws. People must follow the laws; however, citizens can legally act to change laws they feel are unjust. Responsibilities of citizenship include communityCommunity: a group of people who live in the same area, have a common background, or share similar interests expectations. Good citizens choose to meet these expectations. Americans have specific ideas about what good citizenship means. InfluencingInfluence: to have an effect on something government, such as through contacting leaders or holding office, is one of these expectations.

People who are not legal U.S. citizens can also show good citizenship! Acting like a good citizen helps improve the community for everyone. Fulfilling the responsibilities and duties of citizenship helps secure the common goodCommon Good: the greatest possible conditions of society for the greatest number of people living in it. Ask a trusted adult about their experiences with civic responsibilities.

Duty: Obey Laws

  • Crossing streets in crosswalks.
  • Walking pets on a leash.
  • No fighting.

Duty: Pay Taxes

  • Taxes added to items you purchase.
  • Taxes paid based on money earned.
  • Taxes on special items like gasoline and cell phones.

Duty: Defend the Nation

  • Registering for draft (if required).
  • Serving in military when required by law.

Duty: Serve on Juries

  • Showing up when called for jury service.
  • Examining evidence fairly when serving on a jury/

Duty: Attend School

  • Going to public, private, charter school, or homeschooling.
  • Meeting state requirements.
  • To advance to the next grade or graduate.

Responsibility: Vote

  • Voting for local, state, and national leaders.
  • Voting on issues like whether to build a new park or change a school's hours.

Responsibility: Influence Government

  • Attending public meetings.
  • Writing letters to elected officials or newspapers.
  • Participating in surveys or polls.
  • Watching and discussing important news.

Responsibility: Show support for troops

  • Saying "Thank You" when you see or meet a solider or veteran.
  • Being friendly to kids with parents in the military.
  • Sending treats and supplies to troops overseas.

Responsibility: Help Other People

  • Helping with cleanup and repairs after a storm.
  • Helping family with daily chores like cooking and cleaning.
  • Helping younger children with reading or homework.
  • Volunteering at the library or with local clubs.

Responsibility: Show Respect for Leaders and Symbols

  • Referring to elected officials by title, such as the President by using "President" as the title before the name.
  • Reparing or replacing worn U.S. flags.
  • Following rules when visiting landmarks.
  • Remaining silent with a hand over the heart during the national anthem.

Responsibility: Protect Rights

  • Offering to help settle disputes.
  • Speaking up for people who cannot do so for themselves.
  • Learning about the rights that are protected by law.
