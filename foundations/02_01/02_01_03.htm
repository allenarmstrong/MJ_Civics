How Did Ancient Greece and Rome Influence American Leaders?

Two important ideas about government came from the ancient Greeks and Romans. Each of these ideas describes a method for making political decisions in a society, such as creating laws.

Direct democracy is a form of government where laws are made directly by the citizens. A republic is a form of government where citizens elect representativesRepresentatives: a person chosen to speak and act for their fellow citizens in government to make laws and decisions. As a group, the elected representatives make up the legislative branchLegislative branch: the part of government responsible for making laws. Examine the images for more details about these ideas.

Enlightenment thinkers and American leaders wrote about and discussed these forms of government. Many believed that combining these ideas in some way could provide a better government for all people.

Direct Democracy

  • In direct democracy, citizens vote directly on laws and other issues.
  • The people of ancient Greece were the first to practice direct democracy.
  • Direct democracy can work for a local area, like a small town.
  • Some towns in the Northeast United States still have "town meetings" where citizens vote in person on issues.

Republic

  • In a republic, citizens elect representatives. The elected individuals represent the voters and make decisions on their behalf.
  • The people of ancient Rome had a republic form of government for many years.
  • A republic can work for a large area or population, where not enough time or space exists for every citizen to vote on every issue.
  • The federal government of the United States today is a republic. Citizens vote for representatives to meet in Congress, which is the name for the U.S. legislative branch. They vote on national issues and laws.
