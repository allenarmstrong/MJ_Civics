Did you think it was difficult to choose a law to veto? The U.S. president may veto bills passed by Congress. Veto power is one way the Constitution limits, or checks, the powers of government. The Founding Fathers thought balancing the powers of government was critical for the success of the Constitution. Some of them believed the Constitution needed to have more limits.
