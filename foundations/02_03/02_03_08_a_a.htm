Practice Analysis: Answers Text Version

Section One

Select the issues from this list that led to the writing of the Declaration of Independence.

  • Taxation without consent
  • No representation in Parliament
  • Forced work hours
  • No tea shipped to colonies
  • King did not answer petitions
  • Holding trials in the colonies
  • Quartering of soldiers
  • Battles of Lexington and Concord
  • Shortage of parchment
  • Intolerable Acts, punishment for the Boston Tea Party
  • Sending children to British schools
  • Control of trade with other countries

Section Two

Place in order the three main sections of the Declaration of Independence.

(1) Introduction and Beliefs
(2) List of Grievances
(3) Formal Declaration

Section Three

Match each quote to the section of the Declaration of Independence in which it is found.

  1. "We hold these truths to be self-evident … " Introduction and Beliefs
  2. "We, therefore… solemnly publish and declare, That these United Colonies are, and of Right ought to be Free and Independent States;" Formal Declaration
  3. "He has dissolved Representative Houses repeatedly," List of Grievances
  4. "For depriving us in many cases, of the benefits of Trial by Jury:" List of Grievances
  5. "… we mutually pledge to each other our Lives, our Fortunes and our sacred Honor." Formal Declaration
  6. "… it is their (the people's) right, it is their duty, to throw off such Government, and to provide new Guards for their future security" Introduction and Beliefs
