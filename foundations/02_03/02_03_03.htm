What Led the Colonists to Break Up with Great Britain?

The American colonists were upset over many issues that began shortly after the French and Indian War ended in 1763. In this war, British and American soldiers fought the French and Native Americans to protect their colonies. Britain was fighting in other places as well and deeply in debtDebt: an amount of money, service, or other property owed to someone.

Each colony chose delegatesDelegates: representatives from the 13 colonies sent to the Continental Congress, or representatives, to meet and discuss their problems for the first time in 1774. In this imaginary session of the Continental CongressContinental Congress: meeting of delegates from the British colonies that became the first governing group of the United States of America, listen to the delegates discuss the issues that led to the writing of the Declaration of Independence.
