Should a President Be in Office for Life?

Each term of office for a U.S. president is four years. Franklin D. Roosevelt was elected to four terms as president. At the time, there was no limit to the number of terms a president could serve. He passed away during his fourth term, serving a total of 13 years. Soon after his death, CongressCongress: official name for the legislative branch of the U.S. government passed the 22nd AmendmentAmendment: a formal change to the U.S. Constitution, which limits a president to two four-year terms.
