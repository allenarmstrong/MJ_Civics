Can You Make a Budget Text Version

Instructions: Imagine you are creating a monthly budget for when you complete your education. For each item, select from the available options. You will need a calculator. Keep up with the three following totals:

Total Monthly Income: $0.00
Total Monthly Expenses: $0.00
What's Left: $0.00

Income

First, you must calculate your earnings. Select your expected income based on level of education from this list. The pay you receive is your earnings minus income taxes paid to the government.

Savings

Budget experts will tell you to “pay yourself first.” By saving some of your income, you can plan for big purchases like a boat or vacation. You can even earn some money by saving money in certain types of bank accounts and other investments. Select one savings option.

Instructions: Add the "Monthly Savings" amount to the "Total Monthly Expenses." Add the "Interest Earned" to the "Total Monthly Income." Subtract the "Total Monthly Expenses" from the "Total Monthly Income" to calculate the "What's Left" amount.

Budget Analysis Feedback

Results for "What's Left?" still being a positive amount

You still have money left over. Great job! You kept your spending under your total income. You could put that extra money into savings and plan for something special.

Results for "What's Left" being zero, with total income matching total expenses

You balanced your budget! You have controlled your spending to match your total income. You can adjust your spending to save more money for a special purchase.

Results for "What's Left" being a negative amount

Oops! Your spending is out of control! You are spending more than you are earning. Your budget is in trouble. Adjust your spending to make sure your income covers it.
