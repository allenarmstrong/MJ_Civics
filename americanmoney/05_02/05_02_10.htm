What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be able to describe the incentives of entrepreneurs! Now, you will use this information to profile an entrepreneur.

Steps


  1. © 2011 Brand X Pictures/Thinkstock
    Identify an entrepreneur. You may select an entrepreneur from this list or research another person. You may even Interview a local business person and entrepreneur. If you do, try to include a picture of the person or the business! Obtain approval from your parent or guardian and your instructor before choosing someone who is not on the list.
  2. Research the entrepreneur. Learn as much as you can about the person's background, motivation to start a business, and efforts to grow the business with the goal of earning a profit. You may find the Virtual Library helpful in your research.
  3. Choose a format for your profile. You can make it like an Online Entrepreneur Profile or, for the more artistic, a Song. In your profile or song, be sure to include the following facts about your entrepreneur:
    • personal background
    • the business idea
    • motivations and incentives to start the business
    • how the entrepreneur kept the business competitive
    • how the business has grown and spread to more people

Please view the Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.

  1. Complete the reading and activities for this lesson.
  2. Review your CAP file for this lesson.
  3. Complete and submit your Profile to American Money 02 What Are Incentives?
