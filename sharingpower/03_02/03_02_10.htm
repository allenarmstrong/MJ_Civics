Does the State of Florida Have a Bill of Rights?

The first article of the Florida Constitution is called the Declaration of Rights. It lists 27 rights of Florida's citizens. The rights are similar to those in the Bill of RightsBill of Rights: the first 10 amendments to the Constitution but have more detail. For example, the U.S. Bill of Rights protects freedom of religion. The Florida Constitution does as well, but also says that "Religious freedom shall not justify practices inconsistent with public morals, peace or safety." In this way, Florida's Declaration of Rights is more specific.

Amendment I - Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble, and to petition the Government for a redress of grievances.

Examine these words of the Florida Declaration of Rights and the U.S. Bill of Rights to see how they compare.


How are they similar of different?

Both the Florida Constitution and the U.S. Constitution list rights of the people. They are organized differently, however, and Florida's Declaration of Rights contains more detail than the U.S. Bill of Rights.
