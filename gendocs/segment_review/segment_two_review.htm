Segment Two Exam Review

Congratulations! You will soon complete the second segment of this course. Examine this presentation for topics from segment two. Your exam will cover this content.

This review covers the main objectives you will need to know in order to prepare for your segment exam. Be sure to revisit any unit you feel you need more time to study before beginning your exam.

American Money

Objectives

  • define scarcity and opportunity cost
  • recognize the relationship between supply, demand, and price
  • recognize economic concepts in the development of the United States
  • explain how profits, incentives, and competition motivate people and groups
  • identify diverse entrepreneurs
  • use geographic terms and tools to explain cultural diffusion
  • explain the principles of market and mixed economies
  • identify U.S. laws and regulations used to promote market competition
  • describe impact of the U.S. banking system
  • explain how economic institutions impact the national economy
  • explain how taxes support the economy at different levels
  • compare the national budget to a personal budget
  • discuss the role and function of financial institutions in the U.S. economy
  • discuss the relationship between government and financial institutions

Going Global

Objectives

  • compare different forms of government
  • explain how geography has influenced settlement, trade, and governments in North America
  • compare and contrast the standard of living in various countries today to that of the United States using gross domestic product (GDP)
  • compare and contrast a single resource economy with a diversified economy
  • use maps to describe characteristics of natural resources in North America
  • differentiate between foreign and domestic policy issues
  • explain the need for a system of currency exchange
  • assess how changes in the value of currency affect trade between nations
  • recognize participation in international organizations
  • describe examples of how the United States has dealt with international conflicts
Advanced

  • evaluate current international news stories
