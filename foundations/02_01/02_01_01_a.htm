Cell Phone Text Version

Teen 1 (holding a cell phone)—“Yea, I’m so glad my mom got me a new cell phone. I swear it jumped out of my pocket right into the toilet! They should make these things waterproof. I cannot go five minutes without texting my friends. It’s a good thing I have an unlimited texting and minutes plan.”

Teen 2 (no cell phone)—“Man, my parents won’t let me have a cell phone. They say I am too young to need one. They say if I have an emergency, my teacher will let me use the phone. I must be the only kid without a cell phone.”

Teen 3 (cell phone)—“I have one, but I can only use it for emergencies. I have few minutes and texts. My dad freaked out when I went over my texting limit last month. Now I have to wait until I get home and talk to my friends online.”

Teen 4 (no cell phone)—“I know I’m the only one around here with no cool stuff—no cell phone, no Internet, no television, no video games. I’m really busy anyway with sports and homework, but it would be cool to be a part of what everyone else is doing.”

“As you can see from these teens, parents have different rules about cell phones. This is because opinions about where and when teens should have cell phones are different.

People also have different ideas about how government should work. The Founding Fathers of the United States studied many of these ideas before creating the federal government.”
