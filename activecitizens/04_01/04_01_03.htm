Why Do We Have Different Perspectives?





© 2011 AP Images

People look at what happens around them through their own perspective. BiasBias: preference toward a particular belief or attitude develops from our different experiences in life. This includes our natural personalities and the ways our families raise us. What we learn, the interests and hobbies we pursue, and the behaviors of friends and people we interact with daily shape our opinions as well. For example, perhaps teens who want to expand the library spend most of their free time reading.

Varied experiences and ideas help leaders understand the potential outcomes of their decisions. Good citizens and leaders learn about different views on issues before making judgments of their own. Discovering as much as possible about an issue is important to being an informed citizen. Active citizens should strive to be informed citizens so they can make good choices for their community.
