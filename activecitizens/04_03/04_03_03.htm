What Is a Social Movement?


© 2011 Comstock/Thinkstock

A social movementSocial movement: large, informal group of individuals and organizations supporting a common idea or cause is a large, informal group of individuals and organizations supporting a common idea or cause. The people work together to achieve certain goals. Sometimes the goal of a social movement is a change in public policy. Often the members work toward bringing greater awareness to their cause or issue. With greater numbers of people involved, the more likely the movement can achieve its goals.

Examples of social movements include the Civil Rights MovementCivil Rights Movement: U.S. social movement of the 1950s and 1960s to secure equal rights for African Americans and calls for help for migrantMigrant: someone who moves from place to place, often for employment reasons farm workers during the Great DepressionGreat Depression: worldwide economic downturn from about 1929 to 1939. Social movements tend to rise and fall in popularity, depending on the circumstances of the time. The environmental movementEnvironmental movement: a social movement directed at preserving and restoring the natural features of Earth, for example, is over 100 years old in the United States. Many changes in public policy have happened over the years related to the environment. Some changes include creating federal parks like Yellowstone National Park and limiting the chemicals factories can release into the air.
