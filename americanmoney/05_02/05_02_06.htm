How Does Competition Affect Households?


© 2011 Martin Poole/Digital Vision/Thinkstock

In a market economyMarket Economy: an economic system that relies upon supply and demand to distribute resources and determine prices, households make choices each day about what goods and services to purchase. They also choose how they will buy and use them. Competition between businesses benefits people and households. For example, imagine a pizza shop gives customers a special deal. They sell a pizza and soda meal at a lower price than other shops. More individuals and families may be motivated to go to that pizza shop to save money on dinner out. Household members often see and hear advertisements. Companies compete for business using ads to show why households should choose their product over others. At home, ads grab our attention through many types of mediaMedia: the various means of mass communication as a whole group, such as television, radio, newspapers, magazines, and the Internet. A health-focused household may choose to buy a cleaner that claims it has only natural ingredients. Another family may choose a landscaper because the company offers an incentive of discounted lawn service for customers who use the company's services regularly.
