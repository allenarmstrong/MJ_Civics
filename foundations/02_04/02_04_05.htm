Why Did the Founding Fathers Decide to Write a New Plan?


© 2011 AP Images

Several events of these first 10 years (1777–1787) demonstrated the first government's weaknesses. The most famous of the events is probably Shays' RebellionShays' Rebellion: a protest of farmers in Massachusetts from 1786–1787 over state laws and taxes. Veterans of the Revolutionary War in Massachusetts organized protestsProtest: a public demonstration of disapproval against the state. They were upset about high taxes and punishment of people who could not pay. Some protests were violent.

As the rebellion grew, the state government of Massachusetts struggled to act. The federal government, with no national military or executive branch, could not help. Shays' Rebellion was one of many events that showed the Founding Fathers how important it was to fix the Articles of Confederation.

The Founding Fathers agreed to meet in May 1787 in order to revise the document. Many believed there were too many problems with the Articles and that they should start over. The Founding Fathers chose to create a new constitutionConstitution: a plan, or outline, for government, such as the U.S. Constitution instead of trying to amend the Articles of Confederation.
