Laws: Answers Text Version

Introduction

The officer and Brian meet again! Help Brian choose how to respond to the officer’s questions.

Officer: Hello again, Brian. I trust that you are aware of the time. It is quite close to curfew.

Question 1

How should Brian respond?

  1. Of course. I found in our local newspaper that we now have a curfew of 10 p.m., passed by our city council. I may not agree with it, but it is law so I must follow it.
  2. Of course. However, the Constitution does not mention any curfew, so I don’t think I have to follow a local curfew law.

Feedback for #1—Great response!
You know how to learn about local laws.

Feedback for #2—Oops!
State and local governments may pass laws that do not conflict with the Constitution. The Constitution may not mention curfews, but that does not mean that the city cannot approve a curfew. Try again.

[Officer] You make a good point. How does a teen like you know so much about the effects of laws?

Question 2

How should Brian respond?

  1. I learn about laws only from you, my classes, and my parents. I have no other reliable source of information.
  2. I learned that I can find information about laws at all levels of government—local, state, and federal. Web sites have information on past laws, present laws, even bills under debate. I can learn what people think about them through the media.

Feedback for #1—Oops!
Government web sites will give you basic information about laws. You can learn to analyze people’s viewpoints in print, on television, and online. Try again.

Feedback for #2—Great response!
You have learned how to be an informed citizen on the effects of laws.

[Officer] I am glad you have learned so much. Head on home and have a great night!
