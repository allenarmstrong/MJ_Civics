What Motivates an Entrepreneur?

An entrepreneur starts a business to make a profitProfit: what remains after you subtract your total costs from your income. Profits are not guaranteed, however. It depends on whether people are interested in the product or service for sale, which can change at any time. An entrepreneur takes the risk that the business might fail.

Entrepreneurs have many incentivesIncentive: something that encourages a person to make a choice or take action to work hard so that their businesses will succeed. Each person can have different motivations, beyond making a living and earning profit. Perhaps one person's incentive is to improve his or her community by offering a delivery service for less than other businesses charge. Another person knows the community needs a place to sell used furniture, as none exists. CompetitionCompetition: actions of two or more rivals to sell or buy goods or services, or lack of it, motivates each of these individuals.

Examine these American entrepreneurs to identify their incentives.

Ben Cohen and Jerry Greenfield

Ben Cohen and Jerry Greenfield met as teenagers. In their mid-20s, they decided to go into business together, assuming an ice cream shop in a college town would be successful. They were right. They experimented with bold flavors and chunky additions to meet Mr. Cohen's weak sense of taste and smell. Today, Ben & Jerry's ice cream is famous for its unique flavors.

Debbie Fields Rose

Debbie Fields Rose was a "ball girl" at a professional basketball game, collecting balls that strayed off the court. A young teen, she used her small earnings to buy cookie ingredients. That first five dollars grew to millions as "Mrs. Field's® cookies" grew to national fame. Ms. Rose has authored several cookbooks as well.

Daymond John

Daymond John was a boy in New York City when he saw a hat on TV that he wanted. He sewed his own version of the hat and sold them on the street, making 800 dollars in one day! He went on to start the FUBU® clothing company and today helps other entrepreneurs turn their ideas into reality.

Blake Mycoskie

A trip to Argentina motivated Blake Mycoskie, where he saw poor children and families living in unhealthy conditions. He fashioned an American version of the "alpargata," a traditional shoe in Argentina. For each pair of shoes sold, his company, TOMS®, gives a pair to a child who needs them. In five years, the company expanded to more than 20 countries.

Joseph Unanue

Joseph A. Unanue grew up in Bronx, New York. His father founded Goya Foods®, a maker of Hispanic foods. Joseph and his brothers joined the family business. Under Joseph's direction, Goya Foods® became the largest U.S. Hispanic-owned food distribution company in the United States. It now has over a dozen centers in the United States, Puerto Rico, and Spain.

Vera Wang

Vera Wang was a figure skater in her youth and loved fashion. She worked for a fashion magazine for many years before opening her own design studio. She has designed costumes for figure skaters, but her greatest fame is in her wedding gowns. Many celebrity brides have worn one of Ms. Wang's designs. Vera Wang has expanded her design talents beyond clothing, including jewelry and house wares.

Oprah Winfrey

Oprah Winfrey had a difficult childhood but loved talking and performing. Her media presence began as a teenager when she worked at a radio station. Moving to television and starting her own talk show turned Ms. Winfrey into a millionaire by age 32. Her success inspired her to give back to others in need. She is famous as much today for her philanthropy and advocacy as she is for the talk show she produced.
