Instructions: Read the quotes from each document carefully. Answer the questions that follow.

From “Common Sense,” 1776

  1. “IN the following pages I offer nothing more than simple facts, plain arguments, and common sense.”
  2. “To be always running three or four thousand miles with a tale or a petition, waiting four or five months for an answer, which when obtained requires five or six more [months] to explain it in, will in a few years be looked upon as folly and childishness—There was a time when it was proper, and there is a proper time for it to cease … England [belongs] to Europe, America to itself.”
  3. “Europe, and not England, is the parent country of America. This new World hath been the asylum for the persecuted lovers of civil and religious liberty from EVERY PART of Europe.”
  4. “I challenge the warmest advocate for reconciliation to show a single advantage that this continent can reap by being connected with Great Britain. … Our corn will fetch its price in any market in Europe,”

Questions

  1. Who is the intended audience for this document?
    1. Citizens in Europe
    2. American colonists
    3. Citizens in Great Britain
    4. British around the world
    Feedback: Yes! Thomas Paine wrote Common Sense for the American colonists to read.
  2. Which quote from the document points out that the American colonists were not just English, but from all parts of Europe?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! Thomas Paine points out that the American colonists' roots are from all over Europe, not just Britain, in quote 4.
  3. What is the main idea of this document?
    1. Britain should break away from Europe.
    2. The American colonies need Britain.
    3. The American colonies should break away from Britain.
    4. Britain should have tighter rules for the American colonies.
    Feedback: Yes! Thomas Paine was trying to convince the American colonists to break with England.
  4. Which quote from the document argues that the American colonists can earn a living selling their goods in Europe without Britain’s help?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! Thomas Paine points out in quote 4 that the American colonists do not need Britain in order to sell their good in Europe and make a profit.

From the English Bill of Rights, 1689

  1. “That the pretended power of suspending the laws or the execution [carrying out] of laws by regal [the king’s] authority without consent of Parliament is illegal;”
  2. “That it is the right of the subjects to petition the king, and all commitments [obligations] and prosecutions [putting people on trial for breaking the law] for such petitioning are illegal [against the law];”
  3. “That the raising or keeping a standing army within the kingdom in time of peace, unless it be with consent of Parliament, is against law;”
  4. “That election of members of Parliament ought to be free;”
  5. “That the freedom of speech and debates or proceedings in Parliament ought not to be impeached or questioned in any court or place out of Parliament;”

Questions

  1. Which quote from this document states that citizens should be able to bring requests and complaints to the king?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! The second quote is about the citizens’ right to petition the king.
  2. Which quote from the document supports the idea that the king cannot make laws without the Parliament’s support?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! The first quote says that it is against the law for the king to change or enforce laws without the agreement of Parliament.
  3. What is the main idea of this document?
    1. The king is the highest authority in Britain.
    2. The American colonists are not subject to the king’s rule.
    3. Citizens have certain rights the king is bound to respect.
    4. Britain should create a police group called Parliament.
    Feedback: Yes! The English Bill of Rights in 1689 was an effort to list specific rights that citizens should have in Britain.
  4. How would this document have influenced the American colonists?
    1. They would have used it to justify creating new colonies of their own.
    2. They would have been angry that someone put citizens’ rights in writing.
    3. They would have thought it was meaningless for their needs.
    4. They would have believed themselves to have the same rights as citizens in Britain.
    Feedback: Yes! The American colonists thought of themselves as British citizens. Therefore, they would have thought these rights apply to them as well.

From the Mayflower Compact, 1620 (broken into sections for easier reading)

  1. “We … having undertaken, for the glory of God, and advancement of the Christian faith, and honor of our king and country,
  2. a voyage to plant the first colony in the Northern parts of Virginia (Massachusetts),
  3. do … combine ourselves together into a civil body politic, for our better ordering and preservation …
  4. and … enact, constitute, and frame such just and equal laws, ordinances, acts, constitutions, and offices, from time to time,
  5. as shall be thought most meet and convenient for the general good of the colony, unto which we promise all due submission and obedience.”

Questions

  1. Which quote from the document tells us this was an agreement among new American colonists?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! The second quote states that the people involved in the document were creating a colony in what is now Massachusetts.
  2. Which quote from the document states that the colonists promise to follow all laws and rules?
    1. 2
    2. 3
    3. 4
    4. 5
    Feedback: Yes! The fifth quote says, ‘we promise all due submission and obedience,’ meaning they will abide by the laws.
  3. What is the main idea of this document?
    1. The colonists agree to go their separate ways once reaching the shore.
    2. The colonists agree to share all their goods to help them survive.
    3. The colonists agree to create and follow laws to help the colony survive.
    4. The colonists agree to return to Britain at the first sign of trouble.
    Feedback: Yes! Colonists who arrived on the Mayflower agreed to create a government, create laws, and follow laws to help themselves and the colony survive.
  4. How would this document have influenced the American colonists?
    1. They would have seen it as the basis and reason for forming a government.
    2. They would have thought it a useful list of the rights that citizens should have.
    3. They would have used it as a reason to create more colonies.
    4. They would have used it as a list of laws.
    Feedback: Yes! The Mayflower Compact is a statement of purpose for government. People agree or consent to government to ensure their security and survival.

From the Magna Carta, 1215

  1. “No free man shall be seized [arrested] or imprisoned, or stripped of his rights or possessions ... except by the lawful judgment of his peers.”
  2. “To no one will we sell, to no one deny or delay right or justice.”
  3. “No widow shall be compelled to marry, so long as she wishes to remain without a husband.”
  4. “No town or person shall be forced to build bridges over rivers except those with an ancient obligation to do so.”

Questions

  1. Which of the following describes the main purpose of this document?
    1. Limit the rights of the citizens
    2. Expand the rights of those guilty of crimes
    3. Limit the powers of the monarch
    4. Expand the powers of the monarch
    Feedback: Yes! The Magna Carta was an attempt to stop the monarch from abusing his power. It limited what he could demand of the citizens.
  2. Which quote from this document protects someone accused of a crime from losing his property?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! The first quote says that no one can have their rights or property (possessions) taken away unless found guilty of a crime.
  3. Which quote from the document states that a person accused of a crime should not have to wait a long time for trial, or cannot buy his way out of punishment?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! The second quote is about ensuring justice in a speedy and moral manner.
  4. Which quote from the document states that people who are not slaves cannot be forced to work for the monarch?
    1. 1
    2. 2
    3. 3
    4. 4
    Feedback: Yes! The fourth quote is about the monarch forcing citizens to build bridges. The authors wished to prevent the forced labor of free people.
