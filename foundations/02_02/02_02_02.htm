Just like people today, media influenced the way the American colonistsColonist: a member of a colony, one of the inhabitants of the 13 British colonies that became the United States of America thought about government. In the 1700s, though, the main types of media were books, pamphlets, and newspapers. The colonists would meet in private homes to discuss ideas. Let's learn more about the documents the American colonists thought were most powerful.

Objectives

After completing this lesson, you will be able to:

  • identify the Magna Carta, English Bill of Rights, Mayflower Compact, and Common Sense
  • recognize these documents' impact on colonial views of government
