Where Are the Courts?

Each state has state-level courts throughout the state to serve the people's needs. Usually, each county has at least one state court.

Though the United States is very large, luckily Americans do not have to travel to Washington, D.C., for federal-level cases. The exceptions would be, of course, if they lived in Washington, D.C., or if the U.S. Supreme Court agreed to hear their appeal!

This map shows how Congress divided the nation into federal circuits. People who must appear for a federal case may have to travel within their circuit. Which circuit do you live in or is nearest to you?

Each state constitution creates its own court system. They are similar to the federal court system because each level includes trial courts, appellate courts, and a supreme court.

The federal court system groups the states into 12 circuits. It also includes the Washington, D.C., circuit, a federal circuit, and the U.S. Supreme Court. Each state has at least one U.S. District Court. Each circuit has at least one U.S. Court of Appeals. There is only one U.S. Supreme Court and it is in Washington, D.C. If a case makes it to that level then the people involved may have to travel quite far.
