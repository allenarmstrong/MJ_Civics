Discussion-Based Assessments

A Discussion-Based Assessment is a phone call between you, your instructor, and your parent or guardian. Your instructor will discuss the unit's topics with you to assess your knowledge and discuss your progress in the course.

In addition, this is an opportunity to ask your instructor questions. You will need to contact your instructor and complete a Discussion-Based Assessment for each unit.

© 2011 AP Images

Collaboration

Throughout this course, you will have opportunities to collaborate with your fellow classmates.

You are required to submit one collaboration assessment per segment, but you may choose to collaborate more than once. Collaboration can take many forms. Although you may sometimes feel as if you are alone working in the course, it is important to remember you are still part of a larger learning community.

You have many opportunities to collaborate with and help your fellow classmates. You will be able to have deeper conversations concerning class issues and topics.

Service Learning Project

This course requires completion of a service-learning project. Service is helping other people in the community. This project gets you away from the screen and making a difference! You may already be involved in some type of service activity that will suit this requirement. You will submit part of this project in Segment One and the other in Segment Two.

Visit the Service Learning Unit early and often. Work on this project will extend over your time in this course, so planning early is important.

Pace

Pace is your rate of movement through the course. The words "rate" and "movement" suggest progress, which is required on a weekly basis. A benefit of learning online is that you determine your own schedule. Adhering to that schedule, however, can be a challenge.

One of your most important tasks is selecting the pace at which you will complete the course projects and lessons. As an online student, you are expected to follow the chosen pace and submit the activities for grading in a timely manner. You will need to determine your completion date and pace. Think about the following questions:


© 2011 AP Images
  1. When would you like to be done with the course?
  2. How much time per day or week will you have to devote to the course?
  3. In what order do you intend to complete the units?


Many factors will influence your decision. Talk to your parent(s) or guardian(s), guidance counselor, and instructor to determine which pace would be best for you.

Traditional Pace

  • Complete each segment of the course in 16 to 18 weeks.
  • Spend approximately four to eight hours per week on the course.
  • Find more information about traditional pace in the Course Information area.

© 2011 Creatas Images/Creatas/Thinkstock

Accelerated Pace

  • Complete each segment of the course in eight or nine weeks.
  • Spend approximately eight to 15 hours per week on the course.
  • Find more information about the accelerated pace in the Course Information area.

© 2011 Creatas Images/Creatas/Thinkstock

Extended Pace

  • Requires instructor, parent or guardian, and guidance counselor approval.
  • Your instructor will design your individual pace. You will be required to submit a petition for extended pace documenting approval from your parent or guardian and your school guidance counselor.
  • Find more information about extended pace in the Course Information area.
