Why Join an International Organization?

A government can help meet its foreignForeign: relating to, from, or located in another country policyPolicy: a course of action accepted or used by a government to respond to a problem goals by working with other countries in an organized group. U.S. foreign policy goals include security for its citizens and peace around the world. They also include helping others in need and encouraging democracy. Many countries have similar goals for foreign policy. In an international organization, people from multiple countries meet to discuss and act on issues that relate to these goals.

The United Nations (UN) is an example of an organization created to promote all of these goals. It formed at the end of World War II to keep the world's leaders in regular contact. Early members hoped that through frequent discussions and solving problems together, the UN could prevent world war in the future.


George Washington, the first U.S. president, advised the country to trade and be peaceful with foreign countries. However, he said to avoid any more connection between governments. He said it would be dangerous. Do you think his views are realistic today? Note your thoughts in your CAP file.
