What Do I Have to Do?


© 2011 AP Images

You should now be a currency master! Now you will demonstrate your understanding of the need for and effects of currency exchange.

Imagine you are an experienced international traveler. You are writing an article for a travel magazine to help others visit your favorite country. The focus of your article is on currency and exchange.

Steps:

  1. Choose a country. Note the exchange rates for dates in October of 2011 and 2006.
      2006 U.S. currency exchange rate 2011 U.S. currency exchange rate
    Canada (Canadian dollar, or CAD) U.S. $1 = 1.13 CAD U.S. $1 = 1.02 CAD
    India (Indian rupee, or INR) U.S. $1 = 45.19 INR U.S. $1 = 49.02 INR
    European Union Eurozone (euro, or EUR) U.S. $1 = 0.79 EUR U.S. $1 = 0.72 EUR
    South Africa (South African rand, or ZAR) U.S. $1 = 7.50 ZAR U.S. $1 = 7.86 ZAR
    Source: x-rates.com
  2. Write a paragraph that explains why a traveler will need to visit a currency exchange. Include how exchange rates are determined.
  3. Write a paragraph to explain what the traveler can expect from the exchange of currency for the specific country. Include an example of how much the traveler with U.S. $100 would receive in 2011 of the foreign currency. For example, in late 2011, a traveler to Brazil could exchange $100 for 175 Brazilian reals. How would the amount have been different five years earlier? Include why the amounts are different and how that affects the traveler.
  4. Some of the readers of your pamphlet are U.S. business owners. Some will be importers, and some are exporters. In a paragraph, explain to each whether they would have benefited more from trading with the country in 2011 or 2006 and why.

Please view the Article Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.
