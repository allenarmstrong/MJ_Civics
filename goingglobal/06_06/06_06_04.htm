Why Do Nations Need to Trade Currencies?


© 2011 AP Images

Each country generally has its own currency. When one trades goods and services with another country, it needs a way to determine the value of its currency against the other. For example, if a U.S. company wants to purchase coffee from Columbia, it must have Columbian pesos to pay for it. The company needs to know how many of its U.S. dollars it will cost to get the pesos it needs. Countries need to be able to exchange their currencies just as they exchange goods and services.

People who travel, whether for business or fun, also need to exchange currencies. If you visit a country in the European Union (EU), you might exchange U.S. dollars for euros, the official currency for many of the EU's member countries. Countries in the EU that accept euros are part of what we call the "Eurozone." Many places you visit might only accept euros, so you will need the local currency to buy food, a place to stay, and to have fun.
