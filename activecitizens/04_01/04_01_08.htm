How Can I Find Out More?


© 2011 AP Images

You will find that at any given time, Americans are debating countless issues in all communities. You can learn more about issues important in your area by talking with trusted adults as well as through the mediaMedia: the various means of mass communication as a whole group, such as television, radio, newspapers, magazines, and the Internet. Active citizens use the media as a source of information. However, even those in the media have bias. Careful reading and listening will help you sort out the facts from the opinions to help you determine your own perspectives.

The media include newspapers, books, websites, radio stations, television stations, and magazines. You have many choices for ways to learn about important issues and perspectives.

The Virtual Library has great links to trusted news sites you may explore.
