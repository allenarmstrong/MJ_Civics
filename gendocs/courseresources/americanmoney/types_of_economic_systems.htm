Types of Economic Systems

  • Command
  • Market
  • Mixed
  • Traditional

Economy is based on government decisions.

The government chooses the goods and services to produce, how much to produce, and prices to charge.

Leaders also determine training, education, employment opportunities, and pay.

In a true command economy, the government would control every part of the people's economic lives. It would try to prevent uncontrolled growth.
Click to Reveal
Government would control trade with other countries.

Karl Marx would have agreed most with this type.

Click to reveal economic characteristics of a market economy:

Click to Reveal
Economy is based on choices of individuals and businesses. They determine the goods and services to provide based on competition.
Click to Reveal
Individuals and business firms seek to earn profits. Competition is encouraged.
Click to Reveal
People can own property, their own ideas, and make business agreements.
Click to Reveal
In a true market economy, the government would not make economic decisions.
Click to Reveal
Individuals and companies can choose to trade with people in other countries. In a true market economy, trade without limits would be possible.
Click to Reveal
Adam Smith would have agreed most with this type.

Click to reveal economic characteristics of a mixed economy:

Click to Reveal
The majority of the world's countries are mixed economies. They are somewhere between command and free market.
Click to Reveal
These countries' leaders seek to combine the best of both. They encourage individual ideas and progress. They want the protection government can provide.
Click to Reveal
Think of command and free market as opposing ends of a spectrum. Mixed economies describe everything in between. Thus, two mixed economies may be very different from one another. They differ in how involved the government is in answering the three basic questions.
Click to Reveal
A mixed economy generally allows trade with other countries. The government still has some control over what can be traded, where, and how much.
Click to Reveal
John Maynard Keynes would have agreed most with this type.

Click to reveal economic characteristics of a traditional economy:

Click to Reveal
Economy is based on individual and household activity. Most people are farmers or have a trade, like making shoes.
Click to Reveal
Local leaders are most important in the village or town.
Click to Reveal
Traditional economies are usually far from factories and cities. They have little connection to larger governments. However, the town or village may be within a nation that has cities and factories in other areas.
Click to Reveal
We also call these "subsistence economies" as well. That means the people produce what they need to survive.
Click to Reveal
In a traditional economy, people trade with others who live nearby. There is very little or no global trade.
