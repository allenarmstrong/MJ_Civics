Review: Answers Text Version

  1. If the United States has a strong dollar, what happens to the price of chocolate imported from Switzerland?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: The price appears to fall, because the dollar can buy more Swiss currency.
  2. If the United States has a strong dollar, what happens to the amount of chocolate imported from Switzerland?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: More chocolate is imported. The price of the chocolate appears to be lower, so the importer can purchase more chocolate.
  3. If the U.S. dollar is strong, what happens to American demand for the currency of Egypt, the Egyptian pound?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: The demand for Egyptian currency increases. Americans must buy pounds to buy the products of Egypt. Americans can get more Egyptian pounds for their dollars.
  4. What happens to the sales of U.S. importers with a strong U.S. dollar?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: Sales can increase because the U.S. importer can get more foreign goods for less cost and pass the savings on to customers.
  5. What will happen to the price of American soybeans for Japanese importers if the U.S. dollar is strong?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: The price will increase for them. A strong dollar makes another country's currency worth less.
  6. If the U.S. dollar is strong, what will happen to the amount of American soybeans exported to Japan?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: Exports will decrease. They are more expensive for Japanese businesses, so the demand will be less.
  7. What will a strong U.S. dollar do to the Indian demand for dollars?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: The demand for U.S. dollars decreases. Since the demand for exports decreased, there is less need for dollars to pay for those exports.
  8. How does a strong U.S. dollar affect the business of U.S. exporters of food crops?
    1. Increase
    2. Decrease
    Feedback: Their sales will likely decrease. The demand for U.S. exports will go down.
  9. If you were planning a trip to South Africa, which would you prefer?
    1. Strong dollar
    2. Weak dollar
    10. Feedback: With a strong U.S. dollar, you could get more South African rands than with a weak dollar. It makes goods and services in other countries less expensive for you.
  10. Which is the best for the United States?
    1. Strong dollar
    2. Weak dollar
    3. It depends
    Feedback: The strong and weak dollar can benefit different types of U.S. businesses. U.S. importers are businesses that sell foreign products. They prefer a strong dollar. It makes the prices of imports lower, and so Americans will buy more. U.S. exporters are businesses that sell American products to other countries. They prefer a weak dollar. It makes the prices of exports lower, and so foreign businesses will buy more.
