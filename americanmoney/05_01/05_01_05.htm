What Are Supply and Demand?

Goods and services are scarce because we have unlimited wants. We use the term supplySupply: the total amount of a good or service available for purchase to describe the total amount of a good or service available for purchase. Makers of goods, like sneakers, or businesses that offer a service, like cell phone service, try to determine the level of supply that people will purchase.

We use the term demandDemand: the total amount of a good or service that people are willing to buy to describe the total amount of a good or service that people are willing to purchase. When a new style of sneaker comes out, demand may go down for older styles.

The interaction of supply and demand helps determine the price of a good or service. For example, stores near a beach expect a greater demand for sunscreen. The owners likely will have a larger supply of sunscreens available for purchase. They may even charge more than stores not near a beach, knowing that many people will forget sunscreen and need to buy some in a hurry!
