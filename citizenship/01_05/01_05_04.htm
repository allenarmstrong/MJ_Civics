How Are Citizens and Noncitizens Alike?

Millions of people immigrateImmigrate: to move from one country to live in another to the United States each year. In 2009, more than 21 million people living in the United States were not U.S. citizens. They may come for work, to attend school, or for other reasons. Many apply to become U.S. citizens.

All people, whether legalLegal: allowed by law U.S. citizens or not, can act like good citizens. Most follow the rules of the communityCommunity: a group of people who live in the same area, have a common background, or share similar interests. They work hard to help people and set a good example for others. Noncitizens can show good citizenship traits without all the rights of being a legal citizen.
