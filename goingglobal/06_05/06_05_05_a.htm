Goals of U.S. Foreign Policy Text Version

Spreading Democracy

Many Americans believe they have the best form of government yet created. U.S. leaders encourage other countries to move toward democracy. They support changes that give the people more freedom and participation in their government.

Humanitarian Help

U.S. resources are abundant. Many Americans believe they have a responsibility to help others around the world. They bring relief to people in other countries suffering from issues like war, pollution, disease, and hunger.

World Peace

The United States wants to prevent conflict. It makes treaties and partnerships that help promote peaceful relationships with other countries.

National Security

The U.S. government has a responsibility to keep its people safe. It protects the country's borders. It assists U.S. citizens traveling outside the country. The country develops weapons and trains soldiers in case the country needs to enter a conflict to maintain security.
