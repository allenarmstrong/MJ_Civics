Word Bank: president, Congress, issues, public, Veterans' Benefits and Services, Natural Resources and Environment, fixed, growth, Non-defense Spending, Congressional Budget Resolution, Community and Regional Development, President's Budget Request, General Government, grant, and mandatory

The President's Budget Request is the first step in the federal budget process. The president outlines changes to the budget, which reflect changes in priorities for the upcoming year. Congress debates the changes in the next step, the Congressional Budget Resolution. Priorities depend in part on current issues important to the public. More than half of the budget is made up of mandatory spending, meaning those expenses are fixed. The category that varies the most is the Non-defense Spending.

Expenses in this category affect our everyday lives. If a disaster struck the West Coast, funds to help victims would come from Community and Regional Development. Health care for former military comes from Veterans' Benefits and Services. Protecting federal land areas for wildlife comes from Natural Resources and Environment. The pay for someone who works on staff at the White House would come from General Government. Americans disagree on whether increasing federal spending, such as a student grant program, leads to general economic growth.
