Quiz: Answers Text Version

Instructions: Answer whether the following statements are True or False.

  1. Only the Federal Reserve can buy U.S. Treasury securities. False

    Feedback: Members of the public can also buy securities, such as savings bonds.
  2. Responsible use of credit can be good for the economy. True

    Feedback: People who are careful about their use of credit can help grow the economy as well as meet their own goals.
  3. The U.S. government sometimes spends money on credit. True

    Feedback: It can and does spend money on credit, but Americans debate whether it should be allowed. Using credit carries risks even for the federal government.
  4. Using a credit card can end up costing you more money. True

    Feedback: You will pay more money if you do not pay off the credit debt right away. Fees can add up quickly the longer you take to pay it off or if you make a late payment.
  5. The government does not owe the debt to anyone because the government just prints more money. False

    Feedback: The people, businesses, and government agencies that buy securities own the federal debt. It is paid back to them when they cash in the securities after a certain amount of time.
  6. Credit helps people start businesses, which benefits other people and businesses in the economy. True

    Feedback: Starting a business increases exchanges of goods and services between people and other businesses.
  7. Banks and businesses offer different forms of credit. True

    Feedback: Banks offer different types of loans, installment credit, and credit cards. Businesses also offer credit cards and service credit, such as electricity service.
  8. Congress limits use of credit because it hurts the national economy. False

    Feedback: Credit helps grow the U.S. economy when used responsibly. Congress makes rules to prevent broad consequences from poor use of credit.
