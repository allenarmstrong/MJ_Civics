What Would Happen If We Had No Financial Institutions?

To understand the true impact of banks, the Fed, and the U.S. Treasury, imagine they did not exist. Financial institutionsFinancial Institutions: an organization that offers services related to money, such as a bank encourage economic growth. People can increase their money by earning interest on securities they buy, like savings bonds. Banks make loans that allow households and businesses to reach their goals. They follow U.S. laws designed to protect both banks and the people who use them. Government agencies like the Fed monitor the economy and make adjustments to control growth. Responsible use of credit encourages this growth at a faster rate.

Without banks, people would not have a place to go for loans or other credit services. Without credit, people would save for many years to buy a home or start a business. Without federal agencies like the Fed, people would not have protection from banks that go out of business or charge unfair fees. People might never reach their financial goals as an emergency can quickly reduce savings.
