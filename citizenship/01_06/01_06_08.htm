What Else Can I Do?


© 2011 David De Lossy/Thinkstock

As a minorMinor: a person under the age of legal qualification for adult rights and responsibilities, you do not have all the responsibilities of an adult. However, respecting the laws and people's rights should be part of your daily life. You can show great citizenship by learning as much as you can. Give your studies and other activities your very best effort.

Many young people are active in their community. They join clubs at school, the library, or at community centers. They tutor young students. They sometimes serve as student advisors to local government. Through these activities, minors can be good citizens.




Work with a team to think of more examples of great citizenship in your daily life. Your team could make a game of the examples to teach others about citizenship. Select the Collaboration Option for details on using this activity to meet your collaboration requirement.
