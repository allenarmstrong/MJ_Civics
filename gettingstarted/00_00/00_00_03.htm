What Are My Collaboration Options?

To receive credit for the collaboration requirement in this course, you must complete at least one of the collaboration option activities, or you may choose a teacher-sponsored collaboration activity. Please contact your instructor to see if this option is available. Only one collaboration activity and submission per segment is required for this course. When you see the collaboration symbol, this is an opportunity to work with one or more of your classmates.


Collaboration options for Segment One: Lessons Citizenship 04, Citizenship 06, Foundations 04, Sharing Power 03, and Sharing Power 08

Collaboration options for Segment Two: Active Citizens 07, American Money 01, American Money 05, Going Global 05, and Going Global 07

Review the explanations of each collaboration option so you can start thinking about which one you may choose.

Segment One

Citizenship 04

Are you interested in helping the environment? In this activity, you can collect and share information about groups of people working to help the environment in your area. Learn how you can help and share the information with others.

Citizenship 06

Are you passionate about being a good citizen? In this activity, you can work with a team to think of examples of good citizenship in your daily lives. Your team could make a game of the examples to teach others about citizenship.

Foundations 04

Do you think you could create a better government? In this activity, you can imagine you have your own island. With classmates, you can design a government to protect the people of your island.

Sharing Power 03

Are you interested in how laws specifically affect teenagers? In this activity, you can work with a team to create a presentation about the rule of law and teens.

Sharing Power 08

Are you interested in solving problems in the community? In this activity, you can work with your fellow online students to discuss issues and brainstorm solutions for your communities' problems.

Segment Two

Active Citizens 07

Do you ever wonder what makes a good leader and what they should be responsible for? In this activity, you can work with your fellow online students to create a job description for top leaders of your community.

American Money 01

Are you interested in how communities make decisions? In this activity, you will work with your fellow online students to identify, discuss, and solve a community issue.

American Money 05

Do you ever wonder how your tax dollars are spent? In this activity, you can work with your fellow online students to serve as a "watchdog" to ensure that the local taxes are returning benefits to the community.

Going Global 05

Are you interested in global issues? In this activity, you will work with your fellow online students to create a presentation to share with Americans about an important global issue.

Going Global 07

Do you ever wonder how the president responds to conflicts? In this activity, you will work with your fellow online students to advise the president on how to respond to a conflict.
