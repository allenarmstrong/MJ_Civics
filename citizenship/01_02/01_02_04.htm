Are Cultural Groups the Same as Nations?

The short answer is "They can be." For example, U.S. citizensCitizen: a person who is native or naturalized to a country share an "American" culture based on the history of the nation. Yet a person from South Carolina and one from Iowa can sound very different when they speak. A person may be a "Southerner" as well as an "American." People who define themselves as "Japanese" can live in Canada.

Cultural groupsCultural Group: a specific group of people who share similar practices and beliefs are transnationalTransnational: reaching beyond national boundaries. They exist within and across nations. Cultures and cultural features spread around the world through migrationMigration: movement from one place to another and tradeTrade: exchange of goods and services. We call this process cultural diffusionCultural Diffusion: the spreading of ideas, language, and goods across a geographic area. Geography and climate also affect how cultures change over time.
