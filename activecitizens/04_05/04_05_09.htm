What Do I Have to Do?

You should now be an expert on freedom of expression and the First Amendment! Now you will use this knowledge to argue a side in a case involving the First Amendment in schools.

Steps


© 2011 Michael Greenberg/
Digital Vision/Thinkstock
  1. Choose one of the pretend cases listed in the Case Decision Template. Use the template to complete your assessment.
  2. Imagine you are a Supreme Court justice analyzing the case. You must determine several things:
    • The type of expression found in the First Amendment that the case relates to
    • How the Tinker and Hazelwood cases might affect your decision on the case
    • Whether you think the rule in question represents a reasonable limit on freedom of expression
  3. In writing your "opinion," explain the reasons for your decision. Whose position in the case is correct based on what you know about the First Amendment? Include at least three reasons for your judgment. Use an explanation of either Tinker v. Des Moines or Hazelwood v. Kuhlmeier as one of your reasons.
  4. Discuss the case and your work with a trusted adult. Does he or she have any other insight about the case you may not have considered? Include any additional ideas or information into your work. Name the person with whom you shared your work and describe how they helped you.
  5. Save your work into one file with your name.

Please view the Case Grading Rubric before starting the assignment.
