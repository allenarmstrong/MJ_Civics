What Else Do Banks Do?

Besides keeping deposits safe and granting loans, banks can invest in the stock marketStock Market: the exchange of shares of stock between buyers and sellers. The value of stocks is based on supplySupply: the total amount of a good or service available for purchase and demandDemand: the total amount of a good or service that people are willing to buy, just like the price of goods and services. For example, if more people want to buy, or demand, stocks, the price of stock goes up.

The Fed has rules for banks to try to keep the economy stableStable: not likely to change or move. Some of the most important goals of the Fed are full employment, which means as many people as possible are working, and low inflationInflation: general rise of prices over time, which means a slow but steady rise in prices over time.
