What Is a Deficit?

When a budget shows spending that is higher than income, the difference between the amounts is a deficitDeficit: an amount of expenses that exceed income. For example, if your income was $2500 and your total expenses were $3000, you ran a $500 deficit. A deficit is the amount of spending that exceeded income.

You might be wondering how a household, business, or government can spend more than it earns. It can do so by borrowing money, also called using credit. Credit comes in many forms such as bank loans and credit cards. Credit represents a debtDebt: an amount of money, service, or other property owed to someone. You can think of debt as the accumulation of deficits. If you do not adjust your budget and keep running a deficit, you are adding to your overall debt.

On the other hand, if you managed to spend less than your income, you ended up with a surplusSurplus: an amount of income that exceeds expenses. If your total expenses were $2200 and your income was $2500, you had a $300 surplus. A surplus happens when a budget shows total expenses that are lower than total income.

Note that there is no magic solution for correcting a deficit or getting rid of debt. No matter whether it is a person, household, business, or government, only three options exist to fix the budget. Income must be increased, expenses decreased, or a combination of the two must occur.
