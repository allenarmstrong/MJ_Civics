What Are Some Current Issues and Perspectives?

Let's examine a few current public issues that Americans debate. First, we will consider perspectives on increasing the spending of tax funds on programs to help people in need.



Make sure to take notes in your CAP File on these issues. Taking notes will help you complete your assessment!



Follow the slides to experience the 'Perspectives' talk show!

Newscaster: Welcome to Perspectives, the show where we consider different views on current public issues. Today we will look at the issue of emergency assistance in the United States. Our guest, Mr. Advocate, is a leader in several organizations seeking additional help for Americans in need. He believes the federal government should spend more on emergency food and assistance for those in need. Our other guest, Ms. Representative, is a member of Congress. She believes the government is already spending more than it should on emergency assistance. Welcome!

Ms. Representative and Mr. Advocate: Thank you.

Newscaster: Let's start with Mr. Advocate. Please, tell us why you believe the nation's government needs to do more and spend more to help those who are struggling to meet their needs.

Mr. Advocate: We have millions of Americans right now living in "food insecure" homes. This means they do not have regular access to food. They need emergency assistance. Right now, they rely on organizations like mine. We rely on donations. We appreciate all our donations, but we do not receive enough to keep up with the need.

Ms. Representative: We already spend billions of taxpayers' dollars on assistance programs. Some people rely on the help for long periods. Instead, we should use taxpayer money to fund programs that can help people find jobs. This would eliminate their need for assistance.

Mr. Advocate: Many people do not end up needing help because of their own choices. An event like a family illness or natural disaster can ruin people's ability to support themselves. It's not an easy or quick fix. Let's not forget the Preamble to the Constitution. It states that one purpose of the Constitution is to "promote the general welfare."

Ms. Representative: I am familiar with the Constitution, and note that it says "promote," not "guarantee." We should promote ways for people to prepare for and handle unexpected crises. We need to focus on long-term solutions. Helping people find full-time work will allow more taxpayer dollars to go to other important programs besides emergency assistance.

Mr. Advocate: Long-term solutions do not help the millions of children who need food now. Children who go without food do not sleep, grow, or learn as well as they should. They are the future of this nation. Many poor families in this nation do have two parents working full-time. Work is not a guaranteed way to fix these problems.

Ms. Representative: The programs we have are working. The overall portion of the population needing help decreased over the last 10 years. We do not need to make any changes without further studying the data. Americans are not willing to spend more of their tax money on more of the same type programs that already meet the need.

Newscaster: Thank you, Mr. Advocate and Ms. Representative. That's all the time we have for today. You heard some perspectives on emergency assistance in America. Is there a middle ground? Do you have a different perspective? We hope to hear from you. Tune in next time to Perspectives. Good night.

Ms. Representative and Mr. Advocate:Thank you!
