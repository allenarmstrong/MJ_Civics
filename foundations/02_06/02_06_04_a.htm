The three branches of government are the Legislative, Executive and Judicial. Checks and balances exist between one another.

From Legislative to Executive
Congress approves budget, can override a veto with a two-thirds vote, can impeach the president, Senate approves treaties and nominations for federal positions
From Executive to Legislative
President can propose laws, veto bills, call a special session of Congress

From Executive to Judicial
President nominates federal judges, can grant pardons
From Judicial to Executive
Supreme Court can declare executive actions unconstitutional

From Judicial to Legislative
Supreme Court can declare a law unconstitutional if someone challenges the law in court
From Legislative to Judicial
Congress can impeach federal judges, can set number and location of federal courts, can set number of judges, can propose amendment to the Constitution
