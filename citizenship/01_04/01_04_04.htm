Why Does Ecology Matter?

Ecology includes all living things. That includes you! Because living things are connected, a problem for one often creates problems for others. One event, like an oil spill, is a cause with many effects. Examine the chart.

A disaster like the oil spill is only one example of an ecological issue. Many problems develop over longer periods, such as beach erosionBeach Erosion: the wearing away of sand and rocks along the coastline, endangered speciesEndangered Species: a group of living things with a population so small it is at risk for extinction, and climate changeClimate Change: change in the weather patterns for an area over time, including temperature and rainfall. The choices we make can worsen or lessen the impact of these issues. For example, feeding junk food to wild animals can harm their health. This is why one civicCivic: connected with the duties and obligations of belonging to a community responsibility is to be aware of ecology and ways to protect our environment.
