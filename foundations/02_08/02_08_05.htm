How Were the Amendments Ratified?

Twenty-six of the first 27 amendments followed the same process. Two-thirds of both houses of Congress voted for proposal. Then three-fourths of the state legislatures voted to ratify, making the amendment part of the Constitution. Each state makes its own schedule. Therefore, a proposed amendment could wait months or years for ratification.

In one case, Americans decided an amendment they had ratified was a mistake.
