How Can We Approach Foreign Policy?

Each country approaches foreign policy in different ways. Leaders might not work with other countries other than for trade. We call this approach "isolationIsolation: a foreign policy approach of a country that separates itself from events in other countries." The opposite extreme would be invading to take over territory from another country. We call this position "imperialismImperialism: a foreign policy approach where a country takes control over an area claimed by another country."

The foreign policies of most countries fall between these extremes. Leaders that focus on diplomacyDiplomacy: discussion between leaders or representatives from separate nations talk often with other world leaders. They wish to address problems before they become conflicts. Ambassadors are people who work in other countries toward diplomacy. Sometimes, leaders believe they must use military interventionIntervention: a foreign policy approach where a country takes direct action in events of another country, usually involving military to deal with a problem.



A country's foreign policy approach can change for many reasons. New events might require a change in approach. It might use different approaches for different issues or countries. For example, a country might have an ambassador working in one country (diplomacy) while fighting a war with another country (intervention) at the same time.
