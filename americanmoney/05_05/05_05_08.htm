How Do the National Budget and Personal Budget Process Compare?

Note that the 2010 U.S. Federal Budget shows a deficit. This amount is added to the existing U.S. federal debt. To pay off the debt, either revenue must increase through higher taxes or spending must decrease, or a mix of these two options. Since people do not like increased taxes but do like the benefits of government programs, fixing a deficit and reducing debt is difficult.

Surplus or Deficit:$600$-1,293,489,000,000All budgets have the potential to end up balanced, with a surplus, or in deficit. The last step in budgeting is to make changes to balance income and expenses. A balanced budget accounts for all income and expenses. It is better, though, to have a surplus than a deficit. Failing to correct a deficit leads to debt, which costs more in the end. Debt can be difficult to pay off.
