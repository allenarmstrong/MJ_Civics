How Can Public Policy Improve Our Lives?

Public policy is all around us. Public policy includes the actions that governments take to carry out laws and programs. From the food you eat to what you learn, laws and regulationsRegulation: an official rule or order that guides behavior related to laws, or to carry out a law affect many aspects of our lives. For example, imagine if people in an area began complaining that their drinking water tasted bad. A government agency would respond by testing the water. They might find that a farm chemical to get rid of pests is seeping into the water. Lawmakers might pass a law against using the chemical and fund efforts to clean the contaminated water. An executive agency would ensure that farmers stop using the chemical.

Laws and regulations help make sure our food is safe, our homes are secure, and we have opportunities to learn and grow into responsible citizensCitizen: a person who is native or naturalized to a country.

The U.S. ConstitutionU.S. Constitution: plan detailing the structure, functions, and powers of the government of the United States, also known as the Constitution is based on federalismFederalism: division of powers among local, state, and national governments. Federalism is the division of powers among local, state, and national governments. The U.S. Constitution distinguishes between federal and state responsibilities. Each state's constitution outlines rules for local governments. Each of the three levels of government makes laws that affect our lives.
