Solving Problems with Maps Check Answers

Lesson 3 of 5:


Question 1 of 7:


You are in Chicago, Illinois, at 5 p.m. Your email pals in Osaka, Japan, asked you to call between 9 and 10 a.m. their time. Should you call now?
Answer for those who chose A – Yes: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose B – No: “Yes, that’s correct! You should call between 7 and 7 p.m. your time to meet their request.”


Question 2 of 7:


Your Family is flying from Virginia to California for a family vacation. Your flight leaves at noon. The flight takes seven hours. What time will it be in California when your flight lands? Note that you will cross several time zones.
Answer for those who chose A – 5 p.m.: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose B – 4 p.m.: “Yes that’s Correct! You will land at 4 p.m. in California. It will be 7 p.m. in Virginia; there is a three hour time zone difference between these states.
Answer for those who chose C – 3 p.m.: “Oops, try again.”


Question 3 of 7:


Remember, you left Virginia at noon and landed in California seven hours later. Your cousins in Texas texted you at 2 p.m. their time while you were on the flight. Assuming you respond as soon as you get off the plane, how long will your cousins wait for your response?
Answer for those who chose A – 4 hours: ”Yes that’s correct! They will wait about four hours. When they text at 2 p.m. Texas time, it is noon in California. Because you will land at 4 p.m., they will have waited about four hours, or until 6 p.m. Texas time.”
Answer for those who chose B – 8 hours: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose C – 2 hours: “Oops, try again.”


Question 4 of 7:


You live one time zone to the west of your online instructors. When they set appointments with you, do you remind them that you are one hour ahead of one hour behind them?
Answer for those who chose A – One hour ahead: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose B – One hour behind: “Yes that’s correct! You are one hour behind your instructors. So, for example, if your instructors set appointments between 10 and 11 a.m. their time, you should expect to be on the phone between 9 and 10 a.m. your time.”


Question 5 of 7:


Your aunt and uncle are serving in the military and are stationed near Berlin, Germany. They send the family an email saying they can be online to chat at 9 p.m. their time. If you are in Miami, Florida, what time should you plan to be online to chat and when should your cousins in Texas be online, their time?
Answer for those who chose A – 3 p.m. and 2 p.m.: “Yes that’s correct! You should be online at 3 p.m., and your cousins should be online at 2 p.m.”
Answer for those who chose B – 5 p.m. and 4 p.m.: “Oops, try again.”


Question 6 of 7:


In the chat room, your aunt and uncle mention that on October 21 they will leave on a 6 a.m. flight to visit New Orleans, Louisiana, and they hope the whole family can meet there. If they fly westward and it is 1 p.m. on October 21 in New Orleans when they arrive, how long were they flying?
Answer for those who chose A – 16 hours: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose B – 12 hours: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose C – 14 hours: “Yes that’s correct! They were travelling 14 hours. New Orleans is seven hours behind Berlin.


Question 7 of 7:


Your aunt and uncle change their mind and fly eastbound to visit Honolulu, Hawaii, instead. The flight will take 16 hours. If their flight leaves at 7 a.m. on October 21, What day and time is it when they arrive in Honolulu?
Answer for those who chose A – 11 a.m., Oct. 22: “Yes that’s correct! They will arrive in Honolulu at 11 a.m. Hawaii tome on October 22. Honolulu is 12 hours behind Berlin.”
Answer for those who chose B – 9a.m., Oct. 22: “Oops, try again.”
Answer for those who chose C – 11 p.m., Oct. 21: “Oops, try again.”
