Supreme Court Justices Text Version

[Narrator] The Supreme Court is the most important court in the country. The Supreme Court is made up of a group of nine people who know a lot about our national laws and the Constitution. These nine people are called "Justices," which means they are people that make fair decisions.

Our laws and Constitution are the rules that everyone in our country must follow. When there is a question about the Constitution or a national law, the Supreme Court Justices decide what they mean. Supreme Court Justices have their jobs for life. This guarantees that they can make tough decisions without worrying about losing their jobs.
