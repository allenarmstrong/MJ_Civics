Why Do Wars Still Occur?

Though many international organizations exist to prevent it, war and conflict still happen. Sometimes diplomacyDiplomacy: discussion between leaders or representatives from separate nations fails. A group like the United Nations or NATO can do more than talk. For example, the UN can interveneIntervention: a foreign policy approach in which a country takes direct action in events of another country, usually involving military by sending peacekeeping troops into a country at war. Not all governments wish to be a part of an international group. Sometimes people within a country challenge their government. The government may not want other countries involved in its domesticDomestic: relating to issues within a country issues.

When in a crisis, a government has four basic ways it can respond. The foreign policy spectrum shows these responses. The response could change during a conflict. A government could use a different response for each situation.

Membership in an international organization is an act of diplomacy. However, a country's leaders might not approve responses of a group like the UN. It may decide to go against the decision and lose support of the international organization.
