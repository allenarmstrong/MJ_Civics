Dividing responsibilities was a tough task for these siblings. The Founding FathersFounding Fathers: name given to the early leaders of the United States who helped design the nation's government of the United States faced a similar challenge in dividing government responsibilities. They had to find a way to give government enough power to function well and yet protect the people from tyrannyTyranny: a government in which a single ruler possesses and abuses absolute power, or the abusive use of power.

The U.S. ConstitutionU.S. Constitution: plan detailing the structure, functions, and powers of the government of the United States, also known as the Constitution separates the federal government into three branches. Each branch has its own structure, functions, and processes. Each branch is organized differently, has different jobs to do, and depends on the other branches to keep the country running. Let's learn more about the branches of the U.S. government!
