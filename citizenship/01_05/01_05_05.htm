Why Does Legal Citizenship Matter?

Some rightsRight: a protection or privilege due to a person by law, tradition, or nature in the United States are only for legal citizens. These rights include having a trial by juryJury: a group of ordinary citizens who decide together whether a person accused of a crime is guilty or not guilty and obtaining a passportPassport: government identification allowing travel outside of and reentry into a country. Voting in federal elections and running for federal office are other examples.

People who live in the United States have certain dutiesDuty: an obligation and responsibilities, like paying taxes. However, some duties and responsibilities are only for legal citizens. One example is serving on a jury. Another is voting in national elections.

Americans often debate which rights and duties should be only for legal U.S. citizens. Examine these issues to see which rights and duties are only for legal U.S. citizens.
